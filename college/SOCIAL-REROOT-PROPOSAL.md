# Proposal — re-rooting the social register as a four-way maritime fusion

*Dev-agent proposal for the founder/Rector. Nothing here is canon. Candidates only; the ruling
and merge fall to the Rector. Canon at proposal time: v5.25, 1218 headwords.*

## 0. The number, corrected to roots

The 88/12 split was tag-coverage, not stratum. Collapsing transparent derivations
(-mus/-mys/-ji/-ldā/-stā/-ôr/-ām/-tū/-in — ~172 headwords hang off a root counted separately)
leaves **~1,046 independent roots**, of which **~133 are tagged loans (≈13%)**. Add the
untagged Slavic numerals and the untagged loans and the true loan-root share is ~15–18%. Still
substrate-heavy — the drift is real — but the derivational machinery (correctly Finnic) was
inflating it. This proposal changes ~15 roots; it does not move that number materially. It fixes
the *face* of the language, not the mass.

## 1. Guard — do NOT touch (keep-despite-closeness)

The structural glue is high blast-radius (every text leans on it) and its closeness to Estonian
is legitimate Baltic-Finnic cognate, not leak. These stay, this pass:

- **Pronouns**: mä, sä, tä, mēg, tēg, ne · **negation** äb · **question words** ken, mis, kui,
  miks, kus, kun, ka
- **Light verbs**: um, ūli, votā, tulē, lǟdô, tēdô, nǟgô, panā, sāmā, laskā, pidā, vôī, volē

These are the skeleton. Re-rooting them would break the corpus for no divergence gain — a shared
Finnic pronoun is not "Vulgar Estonian," it is the substrate doing its job.

## 2. The re-rooting table (social / politeness register)

The port is a Hanseatic Baltic trading city: Middle Low German was the working lingua franca of
the counting-house and the quay, Slavic the interior and the familiar, Catalan-Romance the
cultivated seed, Livonian the deepest layer. I let each word land where that port would actually
put it. The pattern that emerged (see §3) is not forced — it fell out of the judging.

Judged on: **(a) defensible** — what it's built from and why this port · **(b) coherent** — fits
phonology, takes endings, no collision · **(c) blast radius** — corpus touched.

## 3. The flavor that fell out (not imposed)

Letting the fusion land where it wanted produced a coherent sociolinguistic split, which is why
it feels defensible rather than arbitrary:

- **The businesslike register is Low German** — hello, please, thank-you, welcome, help, greet
  (*moin, bidô, dankô, velkô, helpô, grȫtä*). This is right: in a Hanseatic port the language of
  everyday public dealing *was* Middle Low German. The quay and the counting-house speak Hansa.
- **The affectionate register is Slavic + Catalan** — the endearments (*lēvü, kohanü, estimô*)
  and the existing *amōr/arm*. Warmth comes from the interior-familiar and the cultivated seed,
  not from trade. The home speaks Slavic and Romance.
- **The polished/ceremonial layer is Catalan** — *mersü*, *adē*, building on the §76 cultivation
  register.

So the face of the language stops reading as Estonian-with-a-macron and starts reading as what it
is: **Hansa in the market, Slav and Latin in the heart.** Each new social word coined from here
reinforces that split instead of diluting it — the flavor builds on itself.

## 4. Blast radius, total

Every affected word appears 1–4 times, only in coursebook example sentences and reader texts —
no grammar-rule dependencies. A merge re-sources ~15 headwords and updates ~25 example-sentence
occurrences. Low risk, fully reversible per word. If accepted, the Rector merges into the
coursebook, updates the touched examples, regenerates `data/` and the bundles, and logs the
ruling.
