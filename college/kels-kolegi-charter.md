# Kēļs Kolēgi — The Nelôxi College of Language
## Contributor Charter & Word-Coining Protocol, v1.0

This document is everything a contributing agent needs to coin new Nelôxi vocabulary. Hand it over whole. Contributors follow it exactly; the Rector (the originating thread) reviews batches before they enter canon.

**Canon authority:** the coursebook (*Nelô kēļ: A Course and First Dictionary*) is the single source of truth. This charter summarizes its rules for production purposes. Where they conflict, the coursebook wins.

**Register rule:** Nelôxi is treated as a real language. Contributors never discuss, mention, or hint at source languages, inspirations, or design history in any deliverable. Words simply *are* Nelôxi.

---

## 1. The sound system (hard constraints)

**Letters:** a b d e f g h i j k l m n o p r s t u v x y ä ö ô ç ñ · long vowels ā ē ī ō ū ǟ ȫ · geminate l·l

**Fixed values (never violated):**
- ç = [ts] · x = [ʃ] · ñ = [ɲ] · j = [j] · h always pronounced
- ô = back unrounded vowel; ä ö y = front vowels; macron = long vowel (phonemic)
- Doubled consonants (tt, kk, ll, ss, nn, mm) are long and meaningful

**Letters that DO NOT exist:** c (bare), q, w, z. Do not use them.

**Syllable and word shape:**
- No word-initial consonant clusters in ordinary (old-stratum) vocabulary. *tr-, st-, pl-, fl-, kl-* etc. are illegal word-initially. Break them with a vowel (*tirist*, *eskōl*) or wear them away (*rānd*). Exception: **late-stratum loans** may keep light clusters — see §11.
- Medial clusters are fine (*eskōl, markôt, setmān*).
- Words end in a vowel or in a consonant / light cluster (-n, -s, -t, -r, -l, -k, -d, -st, -nt, -rt, -lm, -rv and similar).
- Words are 1–3 syllables preferred; 4 is the ceiling for ordinary vocabulary.
- Stress is always initial (this affects how a word "reads" — front-load the distinctive material).

**Aesthetic center of gravity:** vowel-rich, soft, with ô, ǟ, ō, ç, x, ñ appearing regularly. A page of Nelôxi should look diacritic-dense. If a batch of new words could pass for plain English or plain Spanish, it has drifted.

## 2. Harmony (suffix rule only)

Roots may mix any vowels freely. **Suffixes harmonize to the root's final vowel:**
- Final vowel is a, o, u, ô → back forms: linking vowel **-ô-**, goal **-lô**
- Final vowel is ä, ö, y, e, i → front forms: linking vowel **-e-**, goal **-lä**

Contributors do not need to supply inflected forms, but every proposed noun must be *inflectable*: test it mentally with -n, -s, -l before submitting.

## 3. Reserved morphology (collision hazards)

These endings carry grammar. New words may *end* in these letters, but a contributor must check that the result does not masquerade as an inflected form of an existing word:

| Ending | Function |
|---|---|
| -n | object case |
| -s | linking case (of/'s) |
| -l / -lô / -lä / -lt | place / goal / source cases |
| -k | together case (with) |
| -d | plural |
| -i | past tense |
| -ji | agent participle (one who does) |
| -dôt | passive participle (done) |
| -ks | conditional (would/should) |
| -mi | comparative (more/-er) |
| -tô | ordinal (jedôntô "first") — same shape as the partitive; check both readings |
| -ū | nominalization of postpositions/relational words only (al → alū) |
| -mus / -mys | deverbal result/abstract noun (valdā → valdômus) |
| -stā / -stä | denominative verb: engage in/work with X (kalā → kalāstā) |
| -ldā / -ldä | denominative mental-activity verb (môtô → môtôldā); linking -ôldā/-eldä after consonant |
| -ām / -äm | place/arena of the action (rēdā → rēdām "court"; pēlā → pēlām "field") |
| -ôr | person associated with a noun/adjective (nôuk → nôukôr; kēļ → kēļôr) — distinct from deverbal -ji |
| -in | device/instrument that does a verb (kūlô → kūlin "speaker") — non-person, distinct from -ji and -ôr |
| -tū / -ty | privative: lacking/without X (kōlhô → kōlhôtū "gutless"; rā → rātū "penniless") |

**Worked example of a collision:** a new count word may not be *sadā* because *sadā* already means "to fall (rain/snow)." This is the exact class of error the check in §5 exists to catch.

## 5. The coining workflow (per word)

1. **Concept first.** Take the assigned English gloss.
2. **Check canon.** Scan the headword list (§7) — is the concept already covered, or does a near-synonym exist?
3. **Coin a candidate** within the sound rules of §1. Prefer forms that feel kin to the existing lexicon.
4. **Phonotactic check:** no initial cluster, legal final, ≤3 syllables, no forbidden letters.
5. **Collision check:** the candidate must not (a) duplicate a headword, (b) equal an inflected form of an existing headword under §3, or (c) sit one letter away from a common word with unrelated meaning.
6. **Inflectability check** (nouns/adjectives): attach -n, -s, -l mentally; (verbs): attach -i, -ji, -ks.
7. **Record** in the submission format of §6, with one example sentence using only canon grammar and, wherever possible, canon vocabulary.

## 6. Submission format

One table per batch, 25–40 words, one domain per batch:

| Headword | Part of speech | Gloss | Example sentence | Translation |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| *word* | n. / v. / adj. / adv. / postp. | one-line English | full Nelôxi sentence | English |

Below the table, a short **collision notes** line flagging anything the contributor was unsure about. Batches without collision notes are assumed unchecked and will be returned.

## 10. The Adaptation Engine (backstage only — never appears in reference materials)

For cultural vocabulary — trade, sea, town, law, craft, church, money — contributors need not invent from scratch. Mine a Middle Low German wordlist and run these mechanical rules. The output is canon-shaped by construction.

**MLG → Nelôxi adaptation rules:**
1. **Stress to the first syllable**, always: *be'tālen* → *'betālô* "to pay."
2. **Epenthetic final vowel** on nouns ending in heavy consonants, harmonized: *markt* → *markôt*; *amt* → *amtô* "office."
3. **w → v**: *wīn* → *vī*.
4. **sch → x**: *schip* → *xip*.
5. **Long vowels survive as long vowels**: *wīn* → *vī*.
6. **Verb suffix -ēren → -ā**: *parlēren* → *parlā*.
7. **Agent -er → -ôr**, or calque with native **-ji**: *fisker* → *kalāji* (calqued).
8. **Initial clusters resolve by loan age** — see §11.

**Sourcing note:** use the rules, not any accompanying etymological narrative, as authority. Historical claims about specific MLG forms should be treated as unverified worldbuilding unless independently checked; the derivation machine works regardless.

## 11. Loan strata: adaptation depth encodes age

Nelôxi vocabulary shows its borrowing history in its shape. Contributors assign each cultural loan to a stratum:

- **Old stratum (fully nativized):** all rules of §1 and §10 apply without exception — initial clusters are *worn away entirely* (*strand* → **rānd** "shore"), finals are opened, everything is soft. Use for: anything the culture has had for centuries — basic trade, sea, farm, faith.
- **Late stratum (partially adapted):** light initial clusters (*st-, sk-, br-, kr-*) and heavier finals are tolerated (*strānd* "beach, waterfront"). Use for: recent, urban, technical, administrative vocabulary. Sparingly — the late stratum should feel like a thin modern crust, not a second language.
- **Doublets are encouraged** where the same source plausibly arrived twice: *rānd* (old, broad, poetic) / *strānd* (late, nautical). Record both with distinct glosses and registers.
- **The sanding is a direction, not a boundary.** Every late-stratum word is on its way to the native shape; common speech softens what charts and contracts keep crisp. Contributors may record a softened everyday variant beside an audible formal one (*strānd* on the harbor map, *tarānd* in a fisher's mouth), and when only one form is submitted, prefer the soft shape unless the word is distinctly technical, administrative, or new.

In-world reference materials describe stratum differences purely as **register** ("older word, broader sense" / "newer word, technical sense") — never as borrowing history.

## 12. Rector's Rulings (binding addendum, v1.1)

1. **rānd is canon** — *rānd* "shore, waterside (broad, poetic)" vs. *strānd* "beach, waterfront (nautical, technical)." Do not re-coin; build on both.
2. **Cluster ceiling:** late stratum permits **two-consonant onsets only** — *st- sk- sp- br- pr- kr- tr- dr- gr- fl- pl- kl- bl- gl-*. Three-consonant onsets (*skr-, spr-, spl-*) are barred even late. *strānd* is a grandfathered exception, not a template; soften new *skr-* etc. to a single onset (*skrīn* → *sirīn*).
3. **Stratum ratio:** old stratum is default; late stratum is a thin crust — target ~1 in 6–7 words per batch, reserved for genuinely administrative/technical/recent concepts. When in doubt, soften.
4. **-ū is productive.** Any newly coined postposition ships with its -ū nominalization in the same batch.
5. **Verbs have no infinitive.** The bare stem is citation form, present tense, and the complement of *volē/vôī/pidā*: *Mä volē lugē* "I want to read."
6. **SUPERSEDED by §37 and later number rulings.** Do not use the old -nd ordinal rule or the withdrawn Finnic numerals. Current ordinals add **-tô** to the current dozenal cardinal forms.
7. **Superlative is periphrastic:** *kôg* "all" in linking-plus-postposition form **kôust** "of all," before the comparative — *kôust sūrmi* "biggest." No superlative suffix exists; do not coin one.
8. **-lt is invariant** — the source case never harmonizes (unlike goal -lô/-lä). *setmānôlt, matīlt* both correct.

New canon words added this ruling: **kôg** all · **kôust** of all (superlative frame) · **rānd** shore, waterside.

## 13. Rector's Rulings (binding addendum, v1.2 — U11 canonization)

**Precedent set:** **-ū is NOT a general verb→noun suffix.** It nominalizes postpositions and relational words only (*al → alū*). For the abstract/result noun of a **verb**, use the new suffix **-mus / -mys** (harmonized): *valdā → valdômus* "government," *lubā → lubômus* "permit," *maksā → maksômus* "tax," *balçā → balçômus* "ballot." Contributors: never form a deverbal noun with -ū again.

**U11 outcome:** 35 submitted, all accepted; the four erroneous -ū deverbal nouns were reconstructed with -mus. Two supporting headwords canonized: **pôhô** "base, foundation" (so *pôhôlēgô* "constitution" decomposes) and the **-mus** suffix itself. *rīgô* "state" accepted as a **late-stratum** administrative term. *pôhôlēgô* is recorded as a transparent compound, not a separate headword.

**Watch-notes for future batches:** (a) *vānô* "oath" / *vana* "old" are a length-only minimal pair — legal but avoid stacking more near-homophones on this cluster; (b) result-noun of a verb always -mus, agent always -ji, relational noun always -ū — keep the three lanes clean.

## 38. Founder's Amendment (v3.6 — the long hundred's divisibility system completed)

The long hundred (§37) was adopted for its *divisibility* — 120 splits cleanly by 2,3,4,5,6,8,10,12 — which is the historical reason the Hansa used it for bulk timber, grain, wool, herring: whole shares, no fractions. §37 canonized the units but not the sharing. Completed:

## 47. The two values of x (canonical, founder-set)

The letter **x** has two pronunciations in Nelôxi:

- **[ʃ]** ("sh") — the normal, native value, used in ordinary vocabulary (*xip, xeç, xolkô*).
- **[ks]** (hard, as in English "box") — the **older value**, preserved in proper nouns and archaic/ceremonial forms.

The country **Nelôxia** and language **Nelôxi** take the [ks] value with **second-syllable stress**: *nel-OX-ee-ah*, *nel-OX-ee*. This is not a one-off — it is the general rule that **proper nouns (place names, personal names, old titles, institutions) and inherited/archaic words tend to keep x = [ks]**, while everyday vocabulary uses x = [ʃ]. Stress in these [ks] forms may also fall off the initial syllable, unlike the native default.

**For contributors:** when coining place names, personal names, dynastic/ceremonial terms, or deliberately archaic vocabulary, x = [ks] is available and appropriate. When coining ordinary words, x = [ʃ]. If a word could read either way, the register decides: elevated/onomastic → [ks]; common → [ʃ]. Mark [ks] words in their gloss as *(x = [ks])* so the value is unambiguous.

The native endonym *nelô kēļ* follows normal rules (there is no x in it).

## 59. FOUNDATION: the partitive case (v5.6)

Added the **partitive** (**-t** / **-tô/-tä**), the workhorse Finnic case, previously absent. It marks partial/unbounded/indefinite nouns against the whole/bounded object case (-n). Four jobs:
1. Partial & uncountable objects: *juô lētet* "drink (some) milk".
2. After numerals >1 and quantity words: *tri kalāt* "three fish", *mult pant* "much bread" — **this supersedes the old "singular noun after numeral" rule** (§ number system); the counted noun is now partitive singular, which is the Finnic mechanism and its actual reason.
3. Negated objects are ALWAYS partitive: *äb juô lētet* "don't drink milk" — never -n under negation.
4. Aspect: partitive object = ongoing/incomplete action (*lugē livrôt* "am reading at a book"); -n object = completed (*lugē livrôn* "read the book through"). This gives the language aspect WITHOUT a separate aspect marker — the object case carries it.

This is a foundational build (not vocabulary): it makes the language generatively Finnic, resolves how aspect works, and gives delegates a rule for partial/negated/counted objects instead of guessing. Ripple: the number-system "singular after numeral" note and any negated-object examples in the corpus should be reread as partitive. Delegates: counted things and negated objects take -t/-tô, not -n.

Foundation-building session in progress: partitive (done), then essive/translative endings, verb aspect & derivation chains, consonant gradation, compounding grammar, quarry-allocation rule. Building the skeleton so delegation becomes extension, not invention. 1,041 headwords (no lexical change; this is structure).

## 60. FOUNDATION: verb-to-verb derivation chains (v5.7)

## 78. FOUNDATION: eventive adjectives (v5.16)

Founder ruling accepted: **eventive adjective** is the name. Nelôxi permits a limited class of adjectives to appear in the ordinary attributive slot before an object noun while characterizing the event carried by the verb rather than the object itself. This is normal grammar, not slang or metrolect. The construction is conventional rather than fully free: strongest with adjectives whose meanings naturally apply to events or actions (cold answer, quick cake), not with every descriptive adjective.

The object keeps its ordinary case, so the existing aspect system remains intact: object **-n** marks bounded/completed action, and partitive **-t/-tô** marks partial, ongoing, or unbounded action. Examples: *Tä donā kylm vastômusôn* "she gives a cold answer / answers coldly"; *Tä küpsā nobē kōkôn* "she bakes a quick cake / bakes the cake quickly"; *Tä küpsā nobē kōkôt* "she is quickly baking cake." Non-eventive readings remain ordinary: *Tä lugē vana livrôn* is "she reads an old book," not "she reads oldly." Module 10 and the coursebook adverb section updated. No lexical change; 1091 headwords.

## 79. THE CREOLE PRINCIPLE (standing doctrine — college/CREOLE-PRINCIPLE.md)

The single most important vocabulary doctrine, written up because it is the most persistent failure in the project: the language keeps drifting toward a tidy Finnic purebred because Finnic is the grammatical substrate and therefore the nearest, tidiest reach for any gap. Nelôxi is a POLYGLOT MARITIME CREOLE — Finnic grammar, five-way loan vocabulary — and must stay dirty. **Rule: when a gap opens, first ask which of the five quarries would have OWNED the word, then borrow liberally and nativize — derivation-from-Finnic is the fallback, not the default.** Concretely (the Finnish move): grab the source word, add a Nelôxi ending, commit — *legitimate*→legitīm, *structure*→struktūr, *system*→süstēm. Why invent when borrowing + an ending will do. **Forward only** — do not re-source already-committed words; fix the generator, not the history. Bias toward the visible loan; a high loan ratio is correct by design. Domain→quarry map: trade/tools/bureaucracy→Low German; market/food/frontier→Slavic; rough/colloquial→Scandinavian; **law/learning/abstract/scholarly/sentiment→Romance-Latin** (the critical case — abstract vocabulary must come in Romance, as in English's Latinate abstract register on Germanic grammar, NOT as native Finnic compounds); body/kin/land/sea + grammar→**Livonian-Karelian core only** (Livonian primary, Karelian secondary — NOT generic Finnic, NOT Finnish/Estonian; both extinct-but-documented, good coherent bases; other Baltic-Finnic lects tappable only if a gap needs it, none placed yet). **The five quarries are CO-PRIMARY, not core-plus-specialists** — given the territory (Latvia, Prussia, Vistula corridor, Lithuania, Slavic frontier, Karelia), the state is demographically overwhelmingly non-Finnic, so the loan quarries do broad EVERYDAY work, not niche work. The domain map shows where each is densest, not its only place. Ordinary non-core words (window, roof, neighbor, to hurry) should frequently be Low German or Slavic, not Finnic. Sidelining the loan quarries re-creates the purebred problem by the back door. Finnic is the path of least resistance so the creole requires ACTIVE counter-pressure on every coinage. Narrow exception: flag a coinage only when its morphology encodes a contestable claim. Otherwise coin/borrow by rule and commit — no approval needed. See CREOLE-PRINCIPLE.md for the full doctrine. No lexical change this ruling. 1,091 headwords.

## 81. CORRECTION: percent semantics stated precisely (v5.16)

Founder correction to §77's percent wording. The **%** symbol does not change meaning — it means **per hundred** everywhere, and is kept unchanged for typographic and keyboard compatibility. ## 109. Surface Balance / Loan Ending Doctrine — -ô is not the default (founder ruling, v5.43)

The creole doctrine (§79) was working — borrow from the owning quarry, sand to fit, don't Finnicize — but **-ô had quietly become the universal sanding tool**, so hundreds of loans walked through the same -ô machine and the surface flattened. The fix is NOT less borrowing (the loan ratio is correct); it is that **-ô is one domestication ending, not the automatic ending for every loan noun**. A borrowed word may enter with its final consonant preserved, its source-final long vowel preserved, a learned suffix preserved (-ī, -ēt, -ūr, -iôn), a true derived Nelôxi ending (-ômus, -ôr, -in, -ām), or as a raw international scar — with **-ô only when it is the cleanest landing form**, not because the word is a noun. Zero-final (consonant-final) loans are explicitly legal; the corpus already carries dek, dent, flag, risk, pont, port, drōk, büdjet, analüüs, struktūr, identitēt. **Endings as register, not decoration:** -ī learned Greek/Latin abstractions, -ēt/-ūr learned/technical nouns, -iôn formal institutional/action nouns, -ômus true derived event/result nouns, -ôr roles/agents, zero for raw/stable loans, -ô for common domesticated count nouns where it is the best fit.

## 120. Past-tense two classes; the "no longer" and "every" gaps closed (v5.53)

Three rulings the corpus probe (`reader/folktale-fisher-sea.md`, the fisher-and-the-sea folktale) forced. All fold into grammar/09 and grammar/14; two add headwords. **+2 → 1815.**

**(a) The past tense is built in two conjugation classes.** The marker is always **-i**; the surface split resolves the long-open `-ā`-verb ambiguity (*donā→donai* vs *kalāstā→kalāstī*):
- **Class I — basic (root) verbs:** drop the final stem vowel, add **-i**. After root **-a-/-ā** the *-i* fuses to the diphthong **-ai** (*donā→donai*, *votā→votai*, *ootā→ootai*, *vōigā→vōigai*, *kantā→kantāi*); after other root vowels it is plain **-i** (*lǟdô→lǟdi*, *pelgô→pelgi*, *sōudô→sōudi*, *itkô→itki*).
- **Class II — derived verbs** (frequentative *-ldā/-tā*, continuative/inchoative *-stā/-stō*): the suffix's consonant blocks the diphthong, giving long **-ī** (*kalāstā→kalāstī*, *môtôldā→môtôldī*, *armôldā→armôldī*).
- **Strong basic verbs** lexicalize a long **-ī** past — the three highest-frequency roots: *tulē→tulī*, *elā→elī*, *nǟgô→nǟgī*. Monosyllabic vowel-roots take a **-si** past to stay distinct from the present: *dī→**dīsi*** "said." *This preserves every past form already in canon.*

**(b) "No longer / anymore" — gap closed.** New headword **amplü** (negative-polarity adverb, Romance/Latin *amplius*), pairing with preverbal `äb`: *Tä äb pelgi mert amplü* "He no longer fears the sea." Native *enäm* stays off-limits (Estonian cognate, §112); the abstract-grammatical function sources Romance by doctrine (§79). Retires the module-09 "do not fill it" flag.

**(c) "Every / each" — gap closed.** New headword **elkü** (distributive determiner, Low German *elk*): *elkü matī* "every morning," *elkü kalāji* "each fisher." Native *iga* (Estonian) and Livonian *jegā* are the off-limits cognate, so §112 reaches for the highest-contact everyday quarry — Low German — as with the other function words (*moin, gūd*). Distinct from *kôg* "all" (mass) and *elg* "elk, moose."

**Minor gaps ruled in passing** (folktale gap-list): *pelgô* "to fear" **governs the partitive** (*pelgô mert* "fear the sea"); *mer* is a **front-harmony** noun (*merel, meren*), *rannã* **back-harmony** (*rannãl, rannãlô, rannãn*); "alive" needs no coin — the productive eventive adjective off *elā* gives **eläv** "living, alive" (module 10).

## 121. Codifying the composition frontier — five constructions, no new words (v5.53)

The calibration texts (`reader/calibration-texts.md`) confirmed the structure is sound but left five constructions flagged ⟨open⟩ in composition §9 — the things that block an agent from writing a *long* text with dialogue and simultaneous action. **All five resolve from existing material; no headword is coined.** This ruling turns them from gaps into settled frames (folded into grammar/14, demonstrated in `reader/folktale-merchant-daughter.md`). **No count change → still 1815.**

**(a) "while" (simultaneous) — closed.** Frame **samā tītôl kui** "at the same time as" (*samā* "same" + *tīt* "time" in the adessive *tītôl* + *kui* "as"): *Samā tītôl kui kalāji parandai vôrkôn, tytār vendai kalān* "While the fisher mended his net, his daughter sold the fish." No dedicated conjunction needed; *kun* "when" stays the everyday near-synonym.

**(b) Reported speech — closed.** Nelôxi has **no sequence-of-tense**, so indirect discourse is simpler than in English: the embedded verb keeps its *original* tense under **et**, never backshifts. *Tä dīsi, et kalā um värskē* "She said (that) the fish was fresh" — literally "…that the fish *is* fresh." Embedded **content** questions take the bare wh-word (*ken/mis/kus/kun/kui*): *Handlôji kysi, kui mult kalā maksā* "The merchant asked how much the fish cost." Embedded **polar** questions take the question particle **ka**: *Pǟr kysi, ka handlôji tulē* "The father asked whether the merchant would come."

**(c) The analytic causative — closed.** "Make/have someone do X" is **laskā** (the *lassen*-type auxiliary, already canon as "let, allow") + object + bare verb: *Handlôji laskai poigôn bringā kalān* "The merchant had the boy carry the fish." This is distinct from the **synthetic** causative *-tā* (module 02), which derives a transitive from an intransitive (*langô→langôtā* "lower") — it does not stack on an already-transitive verb, which is exactly why the analytic *laskā* frame is needed.

**(d) Cleft / focus — closed.** "It was X who/that …" is **se um/ūli X, ken/mis …**: *Se ūli tytār, ken vastai* "It was the daughter who answered." Built from *se* + the copula + a relative *ken/mis* clause; no new construction.

**(e) Tense-carrying relative clauses** — already ruled (the full **ken/mis** clause, module 07/VERB-REF); re-confirmed here as the partner of the cleft.

**Two addenda logged in passing:**
- **Past of -ū stems.** The §120 two-class rule didn't state the *-ū* root vowel; it patterns with the other non-*a* vowels → plain **-i** (*lōjū→lōji* "set (of the sun)").
- **No general "to make/do" light verb.** The reverse index has only *specific* make-verbs (*valmistā* "make ready," *kuinā* "cook," *ehitā* "build," *lepā* "make peace"); an unregistered *tek*- had leaked into `reader/parallel-showcase.md` (corrected to *kuinā* "cook"). A generic light "make/do" was first logged here as a gap; **§122 reframes it as a *feature*** — the language requires the specific act-verb — and opens the abstract cluster (dignity, conscience, equal, brotherhood, "to blow") by native metaphor rather than by loan.

## 122. The texture doctrine — abstractions coined from Nelôxian material life (v5.54)

**The founder's correction:** the language was starting to feel like a cipher — foreign words with a landing vowel bolted on (*dignitā* ← Latin *dignitas*, *egāl* ← French *égal*). That is relexification, not a culture. This ruling sets the method and reworks the batch that exposed it. See `college/METAPHOR-FIELDS.md` for the full doctrine and the seven metaphor fields. **+5 → 1820.**

**(a) The doctrine.** For **abstract and culture-bearing** concepts, coin by **native metaphor** from Nelôxian material life (the sea, the salt & scale, the black-book, the net, the hearth, the parish, the pitch) — compound or extend native roots, and write the gloss as *an image with a stance*, like the press-register words (*langômus* "the bleed," *luxô* "the gifted passenger"). **Reserve loans only for genuinely imported institutions** (church Latin/Greek, bureaucracy Romance, sport/tech international, wharf Low German). This refines §79: the owning quarry of an abstraction the culture already lives *is the culture itself*. The test — *would a fisher-trader on this coast reach for this image?*

**(b) The batch, reworked from the material life** (the flat relex *dignitā/konxentsô/egāl/fraternitā/blāsā* is **rejected, never merged**):
- **nimpundô** "dignity, standing" — *nim* 'name' + *pundô* 'weight': the weight a person's name carries (the salt-&-scale field; trade-honor).
- **isülivrô** "conscience" — *isü* 'self' + *livrô* 'book': the black-book one keeps on oneself, after *mustlivrô* the parish debt-register (the black-book field).
- **ühüvāgô** "equal" — *ühü* 'shared' + *vāgô* 'scale, balance': of one weight, level on the scale (the salt-&-scale field; the market-scale as justice). Adjective, invariant.
- **ühüvôrk** "brotherhood, fellowship" — *ühü* 'shared' + *vôrk* 'net': those who pull one net (the net-&-boat field; maritime crew-bond, not Roman *fraternitas*).
- **tūļā** "to blow (of the wind)" — denominal from *tūļ* 'wind': the wind winds. (Native word-formation from their own wind-word — not Low German *blasen*.)

**(c) "Make/do" stays un-coined — as a feature.** No generic light "make/do": the culture names the specific act (*kuinā* cook, *ehitā* build, *valmistā* ready, *tȫdô* work, *parandā* mend). The refusal of an empty verb is itself texture. (§121's "genuine gap" framing is superseded.)

**(d) Still open — the imported abstractions.** Concepts that genuinely *arrived* (and so may take loans by §79) or need their own metaphor next: "to act/behave" (existing *kondutô* 'conduct' can verbalize as needed), and any legal-institutional terms the corpus texts surface. These are coined case-by-case against the fields, never in bulk relex.

## 123. Relex pass-back over the abstract register (v5.55)

A sweep of the abstract-experiential vocabulary (emotions, virtues, social abstractions) for §122 relex — loan skins on concepts the culture already lives. Findings recorded in `college/METAPHOR-FIELDS.md` ("The pass-back"). **The emotion field is healthy**: shame *häbū*, joy *ilo*, grief *leinô*, fear *pōr/pelgô*, pride *ūpū*, longing *iglū*, hope *lōtū*, memory *mēl*, calm *rahu*, trust *usaldā*, love-verb *ārmastô* are all native; courage rides the body (*klȫtô* "balls → nerve"). **One clear offender reworked; the rest kept as legitimate loan register.**

**(a) Reworked — the sharpest miss.** **fatômô** "fate" was Latin *fatum* in a culture whose founding proverb is *Mer donā, ja mer votā* "the sea gives and the sea takes." Fate here is not Rome's — it is your **share from the sea**. New: **merjagô** "fate — the share the sea deals you" (*mer* 'sea' + *jagô* 'portion'; the sea-field). *fatômô* is kept as the **literary/bookish doublet** (like English *destiny* beside *lot*), re-glossed accordingly — not deleted. **+1 → 1821.**

**(b) Kept as legitimate loan register (native counterpart exists, or genuinely imported).** These are *not* relex sins — they are the learned/formal half of a native–loan doublet, the pattern English itself has (freedom/liberty, deep/profound):
- **amōr** "love (n.)" — the romantic/elevated register beside native **arm** "affection," **ārmastô** "to love." Kept.
- **libertā / līberü** "freedom / free" — the legal-institutional register (the UDHR sense); *līberü* already carries the native-ish "open, unobstructed (of a passage)." A native *lived*-freedom coin (the mooring field — "slipped line, open water") is left as an **open invitation**, not forced.
- **animô** "soul" — arrived with the church (Latin *anima*) beside folk **duhô** "spirit," **navô** "the afterworld." Kept as the church register.
- **mortô** "the dead" — church-register beside folk **navô**. Kept.
- **felīç** "happy" — Romance, embedded in canon texts; flagged, low priority; a native happy-adjective off *ilo* "joy" is a candidate if the founder wants it.

**Principle affirmed:** a loan is a *sin* only when it is the **sole** word for a lived abstraction (as *fatômô* was). A loan that sits **beside** a native word, carrying a distinct learned register, is exactly how a real language breathes — keep it.

## 124. The freedom idiom — texture at the phrase level (v5.56)

The founder gave lived-freedom an **image**, not a word — better than either. The §123 "open invitation" for a native lived-freedom is now filled by an idiom. This opens `reader/idioms.md`, the phrase-level texture layer. **+2 → 1823.**

**The idiom:** **Säs eläji kōrrô karrēl ja menjā sigôkarnt** — literally *"your animals are running in the street eating ham"* — **= freedom, the lived kind:** everything let loose, nobody minding the gate, license bordering on happy chaos. It is *not* legal *libertā*; it is freedom you can *see* — the yard thrown open, the livestock in the road gorging on what should have been the winter ham. Wry, earthy edge (said half in envy, half in warning). Clipped: **säs eläji karrēl** "your animals in the street."

**Two coins it made canon** (both native, filling real gaps the idiom exposed):
- **eläji** "animal, beast, creature — a living thing" (*elā* 'to live' + *-ji* 'the living one'). The corpus had specific animals (cow, pig, dog) but **no generic word for "animal"** — now it does, natively.
- **sigôkarn** "pork, ham — pig-meat" (*sigô* 'pig' + *karn* 'meat'). Head-final compound.

**Doctrine logged:** idioms are ruled like words — the image must sit in a **metaphor field** (§122), be built from attested words (coining any new one by the native-metaphor doctrine), and be glossed *with its register and edge*. An idiom without a stance is just a sentence. This is how the culture speaks in whole breaths, not only in single words.

## 125. "Happy" carved native — the bright, proud, celebratory kind (v5.57)

The founder specified the *sense*: this culture's "happy" is **glee, celebratory joy, pride** — not mild contentment. That fills the §123 open flag (a native "happy" off the joy field) and completes the doublet. **+1 → 1824.**

- **rôimõ** "happy — the bright kind: glee, celebratory joy, pride" — native adjective off **rôim** "delight, gladness" / **rôimā** "to rejoice" (the *celebratory* joy root, not mild *ilo*). Ending *-õ*, the native quality-adjective suffix (as in **kaunõ** "beautiful"). Carries the pride of a feast, a win, a full boat. The family reads: **rôim** (n. delight) → **rôimõ** (adj. happy) → **rôimā** (v. rejoice).
- **felīç** re-glossed to its true register: "content, pleased, glad" — the **mild everyday** borrowed word. The doublet stands (§123): the culture's *own* happiness is the brighter, prouder native **rôimõ**; *felīç* is the small pleased-ness. (Existing texts that used *felīç* for quiet contentment — e.g. the merchant tale's "went home content" — stay correct.)

*(The form was first written **rôimõ**, changed to *rôimē* under a lint block, then restored to **rôimõ** by founder ruling — see §126.)*

## 126. The native -õ ending survives the reform; the gate flags, it does not veto (founder ruling, v5.57)

**Founder ruling:** a rule made at one point does not get to reject work that is grammatically sound. When *rôimõ* "happy" was blocked because *õ* is a "retired grapheme," that was the rule overreaching — *rôimõ* is exactly parallel to canonical **kaunõ** "beautiful." If the word makes grammatical sense, it exists.

**(a) The native grammatical -õ ending is a *living* morpheme.** The v4.0 reform (§104) retired *õ* as a **stem** grapheme — it was fixing Estonian-style body-part *roots* (*nõsô→nôsô*, *sõrmô→sôrmô*). It never touched the native **adjective/adverb -õ ending**, which is why *kaunõ, harvõ, sagõ, seejärõl* are all still canon. New coinage may take a word-final **-õ** on that ending. *rôimõ* is restored as the headword for "happy."

**(b) The lint gate flags for review; it does not veto founder judgment.** The retired-grapheme and Estonian-citation checks exist to catch *drift* — accidental Estonian spellings, stray *š/č/ê*. They are a guardrail, not a court. A grammatically sound form the founder wants **stands**, even if a check would flag it; the check is then corrected or exempted, not the word. (Implemented: `tools/regen_data.py` now exempts a word-final *-õ*; stem *õ* and *êîûâšč* elsewhere still flag as real drift.)

**(c) Standing principle.** This generalizes: **do not reject creative work solely because an earlier rule says so.** Rules here were written provisionally, ahead of needs we hadn't met yet (cf. §115's dialect-flexibility note, §79's evolution). If a coinage is internally consistent and makes sense, it is kept; the rule bends to the language, not the language to the rule. No headword count change — *rôimõ* replaces the interim *rôimē*.

## 127. Translating a long text — the fisher tale; kinship is not possession; images over -ô (founder ruling, v5.58)

Translating *The Fisher and His Wife* (Grimm, Low German — a homecoming to one of Nelôxi's quarries; `reader/fisher-and-wife.md`) surfaced more in one movement than abstract coining does in a session. The founder reshaped every coin and ruled a structural feature. **+7 → 1831.**

**(a) Kinship is not possession, and not gendered — a structural rule.** Nelôxi does **not** say "his wife," "her husband," "their children." Kin are not owned; *tä* is genderless. You **name people and bond them to each other** — by *ja* "and," apposition, or the comitative *-k* — never by a possessive pronoun, never by a gendered kin-word. Possessives keep working for *things* (*täs līnô* "his line," *täl um kōt* "she has a house") — you may own a line or a house, not a person. Folded into grammar/07.
- New non-gendered kin noun **ühüfôk** "spouse, life-partner" (*ühü* "shared" + *fôk* "hearth" — the one you share a hearth with), extending the bond-family *ühüvôrk*/*ühüvāgô*. **läpx** "child" is already genderless. *(Corroboration: the corpus already carried a plain non-gendered **kaimô** "spouse" — this culture never gendered the word; **ühüfôk** is the warm hearth-image register beside it.)*
- Model (founder's own): *"Sergei, his wife Misha, and their children Mary and Miiko"* → **Sergei ja ühüfôk Misha, ja läpxôd Mary ja Miiko** — everyone named and bonded, no *täs/nes*, no "wife."
- The title is therefore **Kalāji ja Ühüfôk** "The Angler and the Hearth-Mate," not "…and *His* Wife."

**(b) Images over -ô — the founder's coins, not mine.** The first pass defaulted to loan-*-ô* shapes (*buttô, kātô, kuningpoig, kōtkõ*); **all rejected, never committed.** Reworked as native images:
- **pakalā** "flounder" = *pa* "loaf" + *kalā* "fish" — **the loaf-fish**, flat as a loaf.
- **hīrlôukkōt** "hovel" = *hīr* "mouse" + *lôuk* "trap" + *kōt* "house" — **a house full of mousetraps.** (Sub-words **hīr** "mouse," **lôuk** "trap, the little snapping jaws.")
- **kivīkōt** "cottage" = *kivī* "stone" + *kōt* "house" — **a stone-house.**
- **kuldxapkô** "crown" = *kuld* "gold" + *xapkô* "cap" — **the gold cap.** So "prince" is not a *kuning*-compound but a picture: *taikô poig kuldxapkôk* "an enchanted boy with a crown" — a boy child wearing a crown.
- "fisherman" reads as **angler** (*kalāji*, the line-fisher of the tale).

**(c) The method affirmed.** One translated movement → seven words and a structural rule, every coin an image the founder could see, every gap now waiting in a *context* instead of a vacuum. The next movements (stone-house → palace → king → emperor → pope → God; the sea darkening green→purpôr→grīs→black) are a mapped seam. Translating long texts is the engine.

## 128. Prince, the short word — kuldapoig (founder ruling, v5.59)

The full image is *taikô poig kuldxapkôk* "a boy child wearing a crown" (§127); the **short everyday word** for prince is **kuldapoig** "the gold-boy" (*kuld* "gold" + *poig* "boy"). Both stand — the kenning for colour, the compact word for running text (the fisher tale's flounder now says *mä um taikô kuldapoig* "I am an enchanted prince"). **+1 → 1832.**
