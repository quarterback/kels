# Uusatôm Cyrillic Overlay — Metropolitan Nelôxi

*Brief standard for writing Metropolitan Nelôxi in a Black Sea Cyrillic hand. This is a
**script overlay for Metropolitan Nelôxi**, not a Maré/Saharannaise/Congolaise grammar and not a
separate dialect. It was standardized around Uusatôm, the Black Sea founding-diaspora city, where
Nelôxi civic writing met Greek, Ukrainian, Yiddish, Turkish, and Genoese trade practice.*

## 1 · Scope and principle

The Uusatôm Cyrillic overlay is **reversible transliteration**. It changes letters, not grammar:
word order, case endings, tense marking, negation, loan scars, and Metropolitan register choices
remain exactly the same as in the Latin text.

Use it for:

- local Uusatôm signage, archives, school readers, and parallel-script editions;
- Metropolitan sentences whose authors want a Black Sea civic flavor;
- names and raw loans that already belong to the Metropolitan written standard.

Do **not** use it to:

- rewrite Metropolitan into Maré, Saharannaise, Congolaise, Ukrainian, Russian, or Church Slavonic;
- replace Nelôxi endings with Slavic case endings;
- smooth away accepted loan scars.

## 3 · Diacritics, special letters, and accepted scars

### Vowels

| Latin | Cyrillic | Example pattern |
|---|---:|---|
| ā | аа | `kalā` → `калаа` |
| ē | ее | `tulē` → `тулее` |
| ī | ии | `matī` → `матии` |
| ō | оо | `sōla` → `соола` |
| ū | уу | `ūli` → `уули` |
| ȫ | ёё | `tȫ` → `тёё` |
| ǟ | ӓӓ | Long front low vowel in words such as `lǟdô`. |
| ô | о̂ | `satāmôl` → `сатаамо̂л` |
| ä | ä | Keep Latin `ä` unchanged: it is the grammar-visible front vowel of forms such as `äb`. |
| ü | ӱ | Front rounded vowel: `kuigü` → `куигӱ`. |
| ę | е̨ | Polish/Lithuanian nasal scar, preserved rather than normalized. |
| ą | а̨ | Polish/Lithuanian nasal scar, preserved rather than normalized. |

`ä` remains Latin because it is highly frequent in invariant grammar and visually prevents false
Slavic reading of `äb`, `tä`, and case-bearing forms. This is the one mixed-script convention of the
overlay.

### Consonants and multigraphs

| Latin | Cyrillic | Note |
|---|---:|---|
| x | ш | Nelôxi `x` = /ʃ/ in normal writing; archaic/proper-name `[ks]` `x` may be annotated if needed |
| ç | ц | Nelôxi `ç` = /ts/ |
| ñ | нь | Iberian nasal scar in words such as `Españô`, `peñç`, `deveñç`, `deseñç` |
| ļ | ль | palatal lateral where preserved |
| sch | ш | German scar; keep as a known fossil before transliteration |
| sz | ш | Polish/Hanseatic scar when deliberately left raw |
| dž | дж | accepted Metropolitan/international scar |
| ch | х | raw-loan scar; otherwise ordinary `c` is not a native Nelôxi letter |
| w | в | raw Germanic/Baltic scar |
| q | к | raw international scar |
| c | к | raw Latin-letter scar unless part of `ch`; use `ç` for Nelôxi /ts/ |

## 4 · Invariant grammar rules

The overlay is explicitly **non-regrammaticalizing**:

1. **Negation `äb` is transliterated wholesale** as `äб`; never replace it with a Slavic negative
   particle or move it.
2. **Case endings stay Nelôxi.** Transliterate final `-n`, `-t/-tô`, `-l`, `-le`, `-s`, `-lt`, and
   other case morphology as part of the word; do not add Cyrillic/Slavic case endings.
3. **Past `-i` stays `-и`.** A verb such as `tulī` is a Nelôxi past form, not a Slavic past-tense
   candidate.
4. **Ordinal/adverbial `-ks` stays `-кс`.** `esmõks`, `lopõks`, and similar forms keep their Nelôxi
   suffix.
5. **Translative/result `-m` stays `-м`.** Do not reinterpret it as a Cyrillic grammatical ending.
6. **Bare imperatives stay bare.** `Segā, kuni sup um paks.` becomes `Сегаа, куни суп ум пакс.`;
   do not add person/number endings.
7. **Function words remain function words.** `ja`, `agā`, `sest`, `kun`, `ka`, `et`, and `jōs` are
   transliterated, not translated.

## 5 · Worked Metropolitan examples

Examples are drawn from existing Metropolitan composition and showcase sentences.

| Latin Metropolitan | Uusatôm Cyrillic overlay | English gloss |
|---|---|---|
| *Matī, kalāji lǟdô merelä.* | *Матии, калаайи лӓӓдо̂ мерелä.* | In the morning, the fisher goes to the sea. |
| *Tä volē kaptā kalāt, agā tūļ um fort.* | *Тä волее каптаа калаат, агаа тууль ум форт.* | He wants to catch fish, but the wind is strong. |
| *Tä ootā, sest mer äb um vaik.* | *Тä оотаа, сест мер äб ум ваик.* | He waits, because the sea is not calm. |
| *Kun tūļ langô, tä seilā.* | *Кун тууль ланго̂, тä сеилаа.* | When the wind drops, he sails. |
| *Tä kaptā mult kalāt ja tulē kōju felīç.* | *Тä каптаа мулт калаат йа тулее коойу фелииц.* | He catches many fish and comes home happy. |
| *Mati, kalāj tul satāmôl.* | *Мати, калаай тул сатаамо̂л.* | In the morning, the fisher comes to the harbor. |
| *Babkô komprā gribôn ja kuin supôn.* | *Бабко̂ компраа грибо̂н йа куин супо̂н.* | Grandmother buys mushrooms and makes soup. |
| *Ka sä tul? — Nūlô, nūlô…* | *Ка сä тул? — Нууло̂, нууло̂…* | Are you coming? — Just now. |

## 6 · Uusatôm lore cross-reference

This overlay belongs with the **Uusatôm** story, not with Maré:

- `world/gazetteer.md` identifies Uusatôm/Odessa as the Black Sea founding-diaspora city and the
  one southern map-name built from Nelôxi `uus` + `satām`, with `Odessa/Odesô` surviving as the
  outsiders' exonym.
- `world/boundaries.md` treats Uusatôm as the Black Sea anchor: an old, cosmopolitan diaspora port
  whose Greek, Ukrainian, Yiddish, Turkish, and Genoese accretions sit on Baltic-Nelôxi bones.

So the overlay is a local writing technology for Metropolitan Nelôxi in that port tradition. It is
not a license to import Slavic grammar, and it does not change the standard Latin canon.
