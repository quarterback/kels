# Nelõxi — a Livonian-Catalan constructed language

**Working name:** *Nelõxi* (neh-LOH-shee), from Neloxia. In-language, the tongue calls itself **nelõ kēļ** ("Nelox-tongue"), on the Livonian pattern *līvõ kēļ*. — *editable; propose alternatives freely.*

**Design in one line:** Livonian bones (Finnic grammar, soft phonology, contact-worn vowels) wearing Catalan skin (concrete vocabulary, Romance verbs, a diacritic-dense orthography), with Low German poking in at the edges. English is invisible — it only lends sentence structure (SVO, pronoun-driven verbs).

**Division of labor between sources:**
- **Livonian / Finnic** → the grammar, function words, nature, body, emotion, everyday verbs
- **Catalan** → concrete nouns, Romance verbs, the orthographic rules and diacritics
- **Low German (Plattdüütsch)** → the Hanseatic coastal Germanic: structural bits (the genitive *-s*), and maritime/trade vocabulary. Its unshifted soft consonants (p t k, b d g — no High German *pf/tz/ch*) fit the phonology natively.
- **English** → word order and pronoun-based conjugation only (never vocabulary)

## 1. Sounds & spelling

### Vowels
**Short (9):** a e i o u ä ö y õ
- `a e i o u` — pure, Spanish/Finnish-style (no English gliding)
- `ä` = [æ] (*cat*) · `ö` = [ø] (French *peu*) · `y` = [y] (French *tu*)
- `õ` = [ɤ] — the distinctive Livonian back-unrounded vowel; core, not borrowed

**Long (macron marks length, Livonian-style; phonemic):** ā ē ī ō ū, plus front ǟ ȫ
- Any vowel may be lengthened with a macron; the common ones are `ā ē ī ō ū`. Length can change meaning.

### Consonants
Stops: `p b t d k g` — voiced stops **kept** (Catalan & Livonian both have them)
Fricatives / affricate: `f v s h` · `ç` = [ts] · `x` = [ʃ] ("sh")
Nasals: `m n` · `ñ` = [ɲ] (settled: the tilde, not Catalan *ny*)
Liquids: `l r` · `l·l` = [lː] (Catalan geminate-L, the interpunct — signature diacritic)
Glide: `j` = [j] (consonantal *y*; so *ja* = "ya")
Palatal series (Livonian flavor, optional): `ļ ņ ŗ ț ď` — available for extra Baltic texture

**Orthography rules (Catalan-derived):**
- `ç` = [ts] always (a real distinct sound — justifies its existence next to `s`)
- `k` = [k] always; `x` = [ʃ]; `h` = [h] (pronounced)
- Geminate consonants are meaningful: `tt ll ss nn kk` etc. lengthen the sound (Livonian/Italian style)

**Phonotactics (the "softness"):**
- No gnarly word-initial clusters — Livonian/Finnic breaks them up (*strada* → *turātõ*, *train* → *tenõ*)
- Catalan-style **final** consonants and light final clusters *are* allowed (softer than Finnish, not toothless)
- Syllable shape roughly (C)(C)V(C) — open, vowel-heavy

### Vowel harmony — *light / Livonian-messy*
Roots may freely mix front and back vowels (loanwords keep their shape). But **suffixes harmonize to the root's last vowel:**
- Back set — last vowel is `a o u õ` → back linking vowel `-õ-`, back suffix forms
- Front set — last vowel is `ä ö y e i` → front linking vowel `-e-`, front suffix forms

## 2. Grammar

### Word order & articles
- **SVO** by default (the English substrate), but **topic-fronting is allowed**: any case-marked element may move to the front for emphasis or topic — the case suffixes keep it unambiguous. *Pūs al donai livrõn läpxelä* "Under the tree, (I) gave the book to the child."
- Adjectives **precede** nouns: *sūr kōt* "big house."
- **No articles.** Definiteness comes from context, or optionally the demonstrative **se** "that/the" (Livonian *se*): *se livrõ* "the book."

### Pro-drop (contextual)
Pronouns are **dropped when context already answers "who"** — second clauses, answers to questions, imperatives: *Mä lugē livrõn ja donā sen säle* "I read the book and (I) give it to you." Pronouns are kept when a subject is first introduced (verbs don't conjugate for person, so the pronoun carries that load).

### Postpositions (no prepositions)
Relations beyond the 5 cases use **postpositions after the genitive** (Finnic order):
- **al** "under" — *kōtõs al* "under the house"
- **tāga** "behind" — *pūs tāga* "behind the tree"
- **ilma** "without" — *ilos ilma* "without joy"
- **jeds** "before / in front of" — *kōtõs jeds*

### The 5 cases (middle path)
Suffixes ride on SVO. Vowel-final roots take the bare suffix; consonant-final roots insert a harmonizing linking vowel (`-õ-` back / `-e-` front).

| Case | Function | Suffix | Example (*livrõ* "book" / *kōt* "house") |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Nominative** | subject | –∅ | *livrõ*, *kōt* |
| **Accusative** | direct object | **-n** | *livrõn*, *kōtõn* |
| **Genitive** | of / possession (precedes head) | **-s** | *livrõs nām* "the book's name" |
| **Locative** | in / at / on / from | **-l** | *kōtõl* "in the house" |
| **— "into / onto"** | emphatic goal | **-lõ / -lä** | *kōtõlõ* "into the house" |
| **Comitative** | with / by means of | **-k** | *ilok* "with joy" |

The four overt markers `-n -s -l -k` are each a single consonant, non-colliding, easy to stack. The fat **locative** swallows *in/at/on/from* so you rarely need prepositions — the biggest Finnic payoff for the least cost.

### Plural
Livonian plural **-d** (with linking vowel): *kōt → kōtõd* "houses," *livrõ → livrõd*.

### Verbs
Deliberately light — **pronoun + bare stem**, English-substrate style (the speakability win).
- **Present:** bare stem. *mä donā* "I give," *tä donā* "he/she gives."
- **Past:** **-i** (Finnic). *donā → donai* "gave," *lǟdõ → lǟdi* "went."
- **No future tense** (Finnic) — present covers it, disambiguated by context/time words.
- **Copula "to be":** one form **um** for all present persons (*mä um*, *tä um*, *ne um* = am/is/are). Past **ūli**.

**Participles** (used freely — they replace most relative clauses):
- Agentive **-ji** — "one who Xes": *lugēji* "reader / reading one" → *lugēji läpx* "the child who reads"
- Passive past **-dõt** — "X-ed": *donādõt livrõ* "the given book"

### Negation
Negative particle **äb** before the verb (Livonian): *mä äb donā* "I don't give." Standalone "no!" = **nē**.

### Questions
Yes/no questions: sentence-initial particle **ka** (or rising intonation). *Ka sä donā?* "Do you give?"

### Pronouns (genderless 3rd person — Finnic)
| | singular | plural |
|---|---|---|
| 1st | **mä** (I) | **mēg** (we) |
| 2nd | **sä** (you) | **tēg** (you all) |
| 3rd | **tä** (he/she/it) | **ne** (they) |

Possessive = genitive `-s`: *mäs* "my," *säs* "your," *täs* "his/her."

### Numbers (Livonian-based, Catalan-spelled)
1 **üks** · 2 **kaks** · 3 **kolm** · 4 **nēļa** · 5 **vīç** · 6 **kūç** · 7 **seis** · 8 **kōtõks** · 9 **īdõks** · 10 **kīm**

> **Mä lugē livrõn ilok.**
> I read book-ACC joy-COM

**2.** *The child loves the sea.*
> **Läpx ārmastõ mern.**

**3.** *My friend went into the big house.*
> **Mäs amīk lǟdi sūr kōtõlõ.**
> my friend go-PAST big house-INTO

**4.** *Don't you want bread?*
> **Ka sä äb volē pan?**
> Q you not want bread-ACC

## 5. Rules: SOLIDIFIED (v1.1)

All core decisions are locked:
- Vowel/consonant inventory and orthographic values (`ç`=[ts], `x`=[ʃ], `ñ`=[ɲ], `l·l`=[lː], `õ`, macron length)
- Light vowel harmony (roots free, suffixes harmonize)
- 5 cases: -∅, -n, -s, -l, -k (+ emphatic goal -lõ/-lä); SVO default; adjectives precede nouns
- Pronoun-driven verbs (bare stem + past -i); no future tense; no articles; genderless 3rd person; copula *um/ūli*
- Negation *äb*; question particle *ka*; plural *-d*
- **Broken tone: excluded** (by design, for speakability)
- Genitive is pre-head; locative stays unified (in/at/on/from)

**v1.1 amendments (Finnic-deepening, all optional habits — no new machinery):**
- Contextual pro-drop (drop pronouns when context answers "who")
- Postpositions after genitive replace all prepositions (*al, tāga, ilma, jeds*)
- Two participles: agentive **-ji**, passive past **-dõt**
- Topic-fronting of any case-marked element; SVO remains default

Changes after this point are amendments, not redesigns.

## 6. Corpus roadmap (Duolingo-style units)

Vocabulary grows unit by unit, each with ~25–35 words + phrases + 5–8 practice sentences, all generated under the locked rules:

1. **Basics I** — greetings, courtesy, yes/no, to be, personal pronouns in action
2. **Family & people** — kinship, names, descriptions
3. **Food & drink** — eating, wanting, the market
4. **Home & places** — rooms, town, the fat locative earning its keep
5. **Nature & weather** — the Livonian heartland vocabulary
6. **Sea & trade** — the Low German layer's home unit
7. **Time & numbers** — days, counting, past tense workout
8. **Feelings & talk** — emotion verbs, speech, questions
9. **Movement & travel** — going, coming, the -lõ goal case
10. **Body & health** — anatomy, states

Each unit is additive; the master dictionary accumulates across units with source tags (L/C/G).
