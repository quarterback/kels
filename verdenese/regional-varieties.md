# Verdenese — the Regional Varieties

*Three regional varieties, each riding an existing mechanism of the system — never a new grammar.
The doctrine of the homeland dialects holds here as everywhere: dialects are lexical and register
overlays on the standard; the spine is untouchable (`../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md` §1). What
Valdória adds is the range: one variety is the default, one re-runs the Canarian-Meralian fusion, and
one is not a variety of Verdenese at all but a **bilingual settlement** under it.*

---

## 1 · Coastal / eastern Verdenese — the default standard

The Romance-heaviest form and the standard described everywhere else in this section: the
Italian/Portuguese-leaning saturation of the Atlantic core (Rīu Grandē, Santa Katarīna, Roçā,
Korentē), per the Crisol Principle's quarry map. The lexicons (H, Y), the dialogue, and the
composition guide are written in it. Nothing more needs saying: this *is* Verdenese.

## 2 · Spanish-zone Verdenese — the Canarian move, inland

In the Spanish-zone provinces (**Korentē, Misjôn**, and the southern border country), Spanish is
not the ambient layer but the **fused body**: the variety re-runs, on a river frontier, exactly
what Canarian Meralian did on the islands — **Spanish body, Nelôxi joints**, by foundational contact
rather than an academy's choice. The grammar is the full standard (no Meralian-style restructuring here); the
domestic lexicon tilts from the convergent/Italian-Portuguese shapes to the Spanish ones.

The shibboleths, east ↔ Spanish-zone:

| English | Coastal/eastern (standard) | Spanish-zone | Canarian Meralian (for comparison) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| hello | **bondiā** | **buendiā** | *buenā* |
| thanks | **grasiē** | **grasiā** | *grasiā* |
| milk | **latē** | **lēçē** | *lēçē* |
| cheese | **kexô** | **kēsô** | *kēsô* |
| fish (table) | **pexē** | **peskādô** | — |
| the grill-rite | **xuraskô** (register-mate) | **asādô** (sole form) | — |

The third column is the finding: the Spanish-zone forms **converge with Canarian Meralian** —
*grasiā, lēçē, kēsô* — though the two varieties fused independently, an ocean apart, from the same
well. Two settler standards ran the same experiment and got the same answer; the cousin-pairs are
logged in both sections (lexicon H rulings; `../mare/canarian.md`). The joints never move:
*Buendiā! Lēçē um, bidô?* is Spanish to the roots and coursebook to every bone.

## 3 · Western / Ebony Pearl — the bilingual settlement (the Meridian model)

The Ebony Pearl (`toponymy.md`) is different in kind. The Freedmen-homestead and Afro-Argentine
settler waves **kept speaking English** — it was never dissolved into Verdenese: ~8M native
speakers, co-official in the western provinces, maintained *alongside* the national tongue (the
Meridian model — local languages persist under the standard; `verdenese.md` §6). The region is
bilingual, not mixed: **English at home, in community, and at church; Verdenese as the national
frame** — two languages in one town, not one fused lect. Code-switching is a fact of the street;
the *standard* absorbs only what the loan-layer rule admits.

**The bounded loan-layer.** English contributes loanwords to western Verdenese only where the
English-speaking community *owns the domain* — rail, rolling stock, industry, the homestead, and
its church — the Valdória Car & Foundry world. This is the deck-loan-layer mechanism of old Maré — the sailors' register that fed Meralian — run
on land: a closed domain list, never a general quarry.

| Headword | Source | Gloss | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **rēl** | Eng. *rail* | rail; the railway | The domain's anchor word. |
| **jārd** | Eng. *yard* | rail yard | *y-* [j] → *j*. |
| **swiç** | Eng. *switch* | switch, points | *[tʃ] → ç* (§2, the same rule as *çipā*). |
| **frēt** | Eng. *freight* | freight | |
| **bokskār** | Eng. *boxcar* | boxcar | |
| **pulman** | Eng. *Pullman* | sleeper car | The founder-world's car genericized, as in a dozen real languages. |
| **fôrman** | Eng. *foreman* | foreman, gang boss | |
| **xop** | Eng. *(work)shop* | the works, the shop floor | |
| **hōmsted** | Eng. *homestead* | the homestead — the family land-grant | The settlement's founding institution, named in its own tongue. |
| **mītin** | Eng. *meeting* | the church meeting, the service | |
| **çôrç** | Eng. *church* | the community church (the institution) | Beside generic crust *kirīk*: *kirīk* is a building anywhere; *çôrç* is the Ebony Pearl's own. |

**A yard exchange** (western Verdenese — English loans in their owned domain, coursebook joints):

> — **Bondiā, fôrman! Swiç um jôvā?** — *Morning, foreman! Are the points good?*
> — **Jā, agā frēt aribā tard. Bokskār um jārdôl.** — *Yes, but the freight's arriving late. The
> boxcars are in the yard.*
> — **Dālē. Laburām!** — *Right. Let's get to work!* *(the hortative -m, coursebook)*

Every English root above declines and derives like any Verdenese noun (*jārdôl* "in the yard";
a switch-tender would be *swiçôr*, never *switchman*) — the seam discipline unbroken. And the
line is hard: *rēl* and *hōmsted* are western Verdenese canon; an English hearth-word — a *haus*,
a *milk* — is a wrong-well rejection anywhere in the country (`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md` §4).

## 4 · What the three varieties prove

One standard, three existing mechanisms, no new machinery: the east is the Crisol Principle
itself; the Spanish zone is the Canarian fusion re-run and independently confirmed; the west is
the Meridian coexistence model plus the old Maré deck register's bounded loan-layer. Verdenese did not need a single new
rule to cover a country that runs from an Italian serra to an English rail town — which is the
pluricentric system working exactly as designed.
