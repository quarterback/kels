# Verdenese Lexicon — Domain H · Batch 1
### The Hearth: Home · Family · Food · Work · Feelings

Archived under the (proposed) Kēļs Ateneô per `verdenese.md`. All roots sourced by the Crisol
Principle (`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md`): the owning quarry is tagged **[Pt.]** Portuguese, **[It.]**
Italian, **[Sp.]** Spanish, **[Rom.]** the convergent Romance shape (where the three cousins
agree), **[Ger.]** the minor German layer. Roots run through the crisol phonology (§2). These are
**Verdenese canon only** — they do not enter mainland canon (`../data/*`, coursebook, charter §7).
Citation form given; full declension follows mainland rules.

**70 headwords approved** (plus the formula *bon kamī* and the register-mates *xuraskô* and
*bondī*, archived in notes). The saturation core: ordinary life first — this is the batch that makes
Verdenese the most Romance-leaning standard in the system, and the batch where the near-cousin
discipline (§3 of the doctrine) is enforced hardest.

## Rulings applied on archive

- **H·1 — the convergence rule is the default.** Where the three Romance cousins agree, the fused
  shape is archived as [Rom.] (*kasā, pan, festā, fīlô, vesīnô*); a single-language tag is used
  only where the communities genuinely diverged, and then the tag is the datum: the table leans
  Italian (*latē, ñoki, polentā*), the countryside Portuguese (*kexô, gaūxô, pexē*), the road and
  the ranch Spanish (*çakrā, estansiā, peôn, dālē*).
- **H·2 — lunfardo shapes are legal source forms.** *laburô* and *bondī* are borrowed as the
  ports already spoke them — Italian and Portuguese words worn into Riverplate Spanish. The crisol
  was running before Nelôxi arrived; Verdenese archives the result, not a purist reconstruction.
- **H·3 — the potato dodge.** Sp. *papa* "potato" is refused because **papā** is "dad" (and
  Canarian Meralian's *papā* "potato" would make the homonymy cross-standard); the Portuguese
  **batatā** takes the domain. Logged as the first collision resolved *between* co-standards.
- **H·4 — rite doublets kept by register.** *asādô* [Sp.] is the standard's word for the
  grill-rite; *xuraskô* [Pt.] is archived as its eastern register-mate, not merged. Both canon,
  the way *dugu/wuro/dək* stand in Congolaise.
- **H·5 — the shelf/land split.** *jerbā* [Sp.] is the market product; the living plant is
  **kaʼa** [Gn.] (lexicon Y). *kampô* [Rom.] is the settler's open country; **ñū** [Gn.] is the
  land-register grassland. One reality, two registers, two quarries — the crisol's own version of
  the Congolaise tie-break.
- **H·6 — back-flow candidates.** *matē, tererē* (Y), *gaūxô*, and *saudādē* flagged for drift
  into the metropolitan lexicon as prestige loans up the trade routes; promotion only by the
  Rector's merge, never automatic.
- **H·7 — -ôr only.** *tropôr, jerbôr, kolonôr, plantôr, panôr* archived; the source agents
  *tropeiro, yerbatero, plantador, panadero* logged as refused morphology, root retained. The seam
  is the thesis (`verdenese.md` §4).
