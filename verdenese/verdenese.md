# Verdenese — the Austral standard of Nelôxi

*Verdenese kēļ.* The Nelôxi of the **Valdórian Citizens Republic of Edeland** — everyday
**Valdôria**, ceremonial **Auriverdia** — the young South American settler-nation spanning the
continent Atlantic-to-Pacific (capital **Portô Venla**, currency the **austral**, pegged to the
Neloxian system; the country lore is being written under `../world/`): the **fifth full standard**
of the language, a **co-standard**, not a dialect. It runs the full Nelôxi grammatical skeleton
unaltered and saturates its domestic lexicon in the **Romance tongues of the immigrant ships** —
Portuguese, Italian, Spanish — while borrowing the names of everything the land itself owns from
**Guaraní**, the land's donor language. Governed from its own seat — proposed as the **Kēļs
Ateneô** (the Athenaeum of the Language), headed by the **Diretôr** — counted on its own terms,
bridged to the system by tags, never merged into the coursebook.

## Why it exists — the thesis run forward in time

The other standards test the skeleton against distance: Saharannaise against one Afro-Asiatic
substrate, Congolaise against a three-tributary Niger-Congo confluence, Maré against the open sea
itself. Verdenese tests it against **time and youth**. Valdória is the youngest of the Nelôxi
lands — it took shape in the 1800s, not centuries ago: a Nelôxian maritime expansion company
(acting on an Italian tip that came up through the Corsican and Italian parts of Neloxia) built
villages and infrastructure on a sparse subtropical frontier, and European immigrants moved in —
Italians, Portuguese and Brazilians, Spanish-speakers, Germans, Swiss, Poles, Ukrainians, Basques.
**Nelôxi was the neutral unifying tongue** — it belonged to none of the immigrant groups, so it
became the common language of all of them. This is the classic-creole case the whole language
claims for itself, observed happening: a shared deck-language precipitating fast among immigrants
who needed one. Where the African standards show the skeleton *carrying* a foreign lexicon,
Verdenese shows the skeleton being *chosen* — and then filling, in two generations, with the
vocabulary of whoever stepped off the ships. The fingerprint word is **tropôr** "drover": a
Romance root (*tropa*) wearing the Livonian agent suffix, the gaucho's trade declined like any
native noun.

## The model — pluricentric, five seats, one spine

Nelôxi is **pluricentric across five seats** — Lisbon, Brasília, Luanda, Macau, and now the
settlement that out-Brasílias Brasília:

- **Metropolitan Nelôxi** — the coursebook, governed by the **Kēļs Kolēgi**, headed by the
  **Rector**. Five-quarry Baltic creole; the trunk.
- **Saharannaise** — the Atlantic standard (Atlanta), the **Kēļs Dīwān**, the **Amīn**.
- **Congolaise** — the Riverine standard (Soninka & Toro), the **Kēļs Penc**, the **Ardo**.
- **Maré** — the Ocean standard (the Meridian States), the proposed **Kēļs Vakt**, the **Lōts**.
- **Verdenese** — the Austral standard (Valdória), seat proposed as the **Kēļs Ateneô** (the
  Athenaeum of the Language — Romance *ateneo*, the civic hall of letters every Riverplate city
  keeps), headed by the **Diretôr** (the Director — the title inherited from the settlement
  company's Language Office, which the Ateneô grew out of). *Proposal only; the founder may
  rename, as with the Kēļs Penc and the Kēļs Vakt.*

The seat-name runs the same genitive frame — **Kēļs Kolēgi ↔ Kēļs Dīwān ↔ Kēļs Penc ↔ Kēļs Vakt ↔
Kēļs Ateneô** — with the institution-noun drawn from each seat's own learned register. Like
*dīwān* and *penc*, *ateneô* is itself a first headword of the standard it names.

Neither seat owns another. What makes the five one language is the **shared spine** — the common
grammar and orthographic agreement, `../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md`, which Verdenese obeys
exactly as its sisters do. The grammar is untouched: Verdenese takes none of Maré's licensed
sanding.

## §1 · The Root Ecology — saturation, not reroot

**Kept — the grammatical skeleton.** Unchanged mainland grammar: no verb agreement, strict SVO,
pre-verbal negation *äb*, bare-stem imperative and hortative *-m*, past *-i*, conditional *-ks*,
the full derivational suffix set (**-ôr, -in, -mus/-mys, -ū, -stā, -ldā, -ām/-äm, -tū/-ty**,
causative *-tā*, comparative *-mi*), agglutinative case with vowel harmony, dozenal number. Borrow
the root, never the grammar.

**Saturated — the domestic core is Romance.** Where Metropolitan balances five quarries, Verdenese
tilts hard toward the Romance immigrant languages — **Portuguese and Italian the twin
heavyweights, Spanish the ambient layer** — because that is who settled it. Home, family, food,
work, feeling: the everyday lexicon is dominantly Romance on the Nelôxi frame (*kasā, latē, pexē,
laburā, lindô, festā*). This is the single most Romance-leaning standard in the system — **more**
Romance than Metropolitan's own core, whose domestic layer stays Baltic. The sourcing doctrine is
the **Crisol Principle** (`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md`), Verdenese's §79.

**Borrowed — the Guaraní land-layer.** Guaraní is **not a substrate and not structural** — nobody
in the settlement learned it as a spoken language. It is the **land's donor language**: everything
the land itself names — flora, fauna, rivers, terrain, local foods, phenomena, toponyms — is
borrowed heavily from Guaraní (*kaʼa, kaʼaguy, ñandū, jaguaretē, avatī, mandiʼô, Yguasū*), exactly
as real regional Spanish and Portuguese absorbed hundreds of Guaraní local-specific words and none
of the grammar. Thick layer, bounded domain.

**Minor — the German loan-layer.** The German immigrant communities contribute a smaller layer,
subordinate to the Romance mass, in a handful of domains — some food, farming, craft (*kukā,
ximiā*).

**Bounded — the English rail-and-industry layer.** English is a living co-official community
language in the western provinces (§6), **not** a Verdenese quarry. Its influence on Verdenese is
a bounded loan-layer — rail, rolling stock, industry, homestead and church vocabulary from the
western industrial world (*rēl, jārd, swiç, fôrman*) — never a domestic-core source. See
`regional-varieties.md`.

**Retained — the Romance-Latin learned crust**, shared with every standard. In Verdenese uniquely,
the crust and the domestic core are drawn from the same broad family — which reshapes the
intelligibility profile (§2).

## §2 · The Asymmetric Intelligibility Standard — the shallowest asymmetry

The three tiers hold, but Verdenese is the **most transparent co-standard toward the trunk**,
because its saturation layer is cousin to the crust every standard already shares:

1. **Level 1 — formal, state & archival** (high written intelligibility). The Romance crust and
   the shared spine; a mainland clerk reads a Valdórian decree as easily as an Atlantan one.
2. **Level 2 — urban vernacular** (asymmetric, but shallow). One or two roots swap (*markôt →
   ferjā*) — and even the swapped root is often half-guessable from the mainland's own Romance
   layer. A mainlander manages in Portô Venla sooner than anywhere else in the system.
3. **Level 3 — domestic and the land** (split). The Romance hearth-layer (*kasā, latē, notē,
   frīu*) still yields to educated guessing; **the Guaraní land-layer does not**. In the monte, on
   the river, at the yerba harvest, a mainlander is as lost as in the cattle-camp at Futa —
   *Jaguaretē um kaʼaguyl* offers the copula and nothing else. Verdenese's opacity lives where its
   land does.

The reverse direction is total as everywhere: a Valdórian, schooled in the standard, reads the
mainland page without effort. The Brazil-reads-Lisbon asymmetry, fifth instance.

## §3 · Orthographic sanctioning

Verdenese writes in the Nelôxian state-archive script under the shared orthographic agreement
(`../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md` §2), extended by the **crisol phonology table** in
`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md` §2 — and no further. In brief: the one new grapheme is the **puso ʼ**
(the Guaraní glottal stop), kept in land-layer words as the stratum scar (*kaʼa, mandiʼô*);
prenasal onsets **mb nd ng** kept (digraphs of existing letters, the Congolaise precedent);
**Guaraní nasal-tilde vowels are refused** — nasality resolves to the adjacent nasal consonant or
flattens (*ñeʼẽmbuku → Ñeʼembukū*), no redundancy with the archive's vowels; Guaraní *y* [ɨ]
flattens to the archive's **y** (*kaʼaguy*); source final stress lands as a macron (*ñandú →
ñandū*); Romance *ch/c(e,i)* → **ç**, Portuguese *ch/x* → **x**, Spanish *j/g* [x] → **h**,
*gn/nh/ñ* → **ñ** (the Canarian conventions, now a second Romance-fusing standard deep); no long
**ō** — the agent suffix stays exactly **-ôr**.

## §4 · Hybrid morphology — the seam, with the near-cousin discipline

Romance or Guaraní root + mainland derivational suffix, fully productive: **tropôr** (drover),
**jerbôr** (yerba-harvester), **kolonôr** (settler-homesteader), **plantôr** (planter). The
discipline is sharper here than anywhere: because Romance is *close* to the learned crust, its
agent morphemes look almost legal — and are refused all the same. Portuguese **-eiro**, Spanish
**-ero**, Italian **-aio/-iere**, and Guaraní **-hára** never enter: *tropôr*, not *tropeiro*;
*jerbôr*, not *yerbatero*. Root borrowed, morpheme refused — the rule that keeps the most
Romance-leaning standard from drifting into being just a Romance language
(`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md` §3).

## §5 · The evaluation matrix

Submissions are processed with three actions, logged in the lexicon files:

1. **APPROVE & ARCHIVE** — assign the domain and its owning quarry (`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md` §1),
   apply the crisol phonology (§2), refuse any borrowed morpheme (§3), record the entry with
   source, quarry tag, gloss, and any cross-standard doublet or cousin (the Canarian cousin-pairs
   are frequent: *kasā/kasā*, *grasiē/grasiā*).
2. **REJECT — WRONG WELL** — flag a Baltic calque for a pampas reality, a Guaraní root reaching
   into the domestic core, or an English root outside its bounded western domains; correct to the
   domain-owner's root.
3. **BACK-FLOW ASSESSMENT** — judge whether a Verdenese word is riding the trade routes north
   (*matē, tererē, kaʼa, gaūxô* are the live candidates). Noted here; becomes another standard's
   canon only by that seat's merge.

## §6 · The national-language settlement — the Finland/Canada model

Valdória is **not** monolingual-Verdenese, and the standard's doctrine depends on knowing what
sits beside it:

- **Verdenese (Nelôxi)** — the national, unifying language, official nationwide. You want it
  anywhere in the country; you learn it if you're staying.
- **English** — a co-official **living community language** of the western provinces (the Ebony
  Pearl region): the Freedmen-homestead and Afro-Argentine settler waves kept speaking English —
  it was never absorbed into Verdenese. ~8M native speakers, maintained **alongside** Verdenese,
  not fused into it (the Meridian model: local languages persist under the standard). Hence the
  quarry rule in §1: English is a neighbor language with a bounded loan-layer, not a source.
- **Spanish** — the fused, ambient Romance layer, broad de facto use (coastal and southern), no
  official status; its weight is already *inside* Verdenese's Romance saturation, and in the
  Spanish-zone provinces it fuses deepest (`regional-varieties.md`).
- **Portuguese, Italian, German (Riograndenser), Guaraní** — recognized regional-heritage
  languages.

The Finland (Finnish + Swedish) / Canada (English + French) settlement, on the Nelôxi frame: one
national tongue, one entrenched community language, and a practical texture the traveler learns
fast — you can get by on Spanish in the east, the west is genuinely English-speaking, and
Verdenese carries you everywhere.

## Accounting

Verdenese headwords are counted **on their own terms**, as a fifth peer lexicon — not folded into
the coursebook total or any sister's. They do not enter `../data/*`, the charter §7 list, or the
coursebook. Shared and back-flow words bridge by region tag **(Verd.)**, per the COMMON-REGISTER
convention, which this standard joins as a fifth tag alongside (Metr.), (Sah.), (Cong.), and
(Mar.).
