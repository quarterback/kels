# Verdenese Lexicon — Domain Y · Batch 2
### The Land: Forest · Grassland · Rivers · Fauna · Flora · The Land's Foods

Archived under the (proposed) Kēļs Ateneô per `verdenese.md`. This is the **Guaraní loan-layer**
— the land's donor language, tagged **[Gn.]** throughout, run through the crisol phonology
(`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md` §2): the puso **ʼ** kept as the stratum scar, nasal-tilde vowels resolved
to written nasals, Guaraní *y* [ɨ] flattened to the archive's **y**, final stress to a macron.
These are **Verdenese canon only** — they do not enter mainland canon.

**30 headwords + 5 bound naming elements approved.** The layer that mirrors how real regional Spanish and Portuguese
absorbed Guaraní: hundreds of local-specific words, none of the grammar. Heavy inside its domain,
absolute at its edge (`CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md` §1) — this batch is thick because the land is, and it
contains not one hearth-word.

## Rulings applied on archive

- **Y·1 — a loan-layer, not a substrate.** Guaraní was never learned as a spoken language in the
  settlement; it contributes names, not structure. The layer is heavy inside the land's domains
  and contributes **nothing** outside them — no kin terms, no function words, no grammar. This is
  the recorded behavior of the real Riverplate contact, kept deliberately.
- **Y·2 — nasality is resolved, never tilded.** *tukã → tukān*, *ñakurutũ → ñakurutū*, *pirãi →
  pirañā*, *ñeʼẽmbuku → Ñeʼembukū*: the nasal-tilde vowels are refused (no redundancy with the
  archive), and nasality lands on a written nasal consonant or flattens with the macron.
- **Y·3 — the puso is the scar, never sanded.** *kaʼa, kaʼaguy, mandiʼô, kapiʼyva, ñatiʼū, kaʼi*
  keep **ʼ** exactly as Congolaise keeps *ɓ ɗ* and Saharannaise its emphatics. Writing *kaa* or
  *ka-a* is drift, and the opposite of the doctrine.
- **Y·4 — the shelf/land split holds across the H/Y line.** *kaʼa/jerbā* and *ñū/kampô* are not
  doublets to merge but the crisol working: the land's register and the settler's register, both
  canon (with H·5).
- **Y·5 — the dorado exception is recorded, not hidden.** One river fish carries a Spanish name
  because the settlers coined it themselves; the [Sp.] tag inside a [Gn.] batch is the datum. No
  false Guaraní is ever back-formed to tidy a table.
- **Y·6 — bound elements stay bound.** *y, itā, guasū, mirī, parā* are archived for the map and
  for reading the map — they are not free nouns, and "water" at the table remains *agua* [Rom.].
