# Verdenese Reader — *Arriving in Venlasport* (a visitor's day, in dialogue)

*Realistic modern dialogue in Verdenese — connected speech across a visitor's first hours in the
capital: the colectivo in from the terminal, checking into a hotel, and a café. The register is
contemporary travel, not folklore: the modern infrastructure words are the **shared crust**
(transparent system-wide — *otelô, pāsportô, wifī, kambôr, kartô, minutô*), the human exchange is
**Verdenese**, and the greeting opens each one. Every frame-word is coursebook canon; every
content root is archived Verdenese (lexicons H and Y). This is the austral leg of the series that
runs `../saharannaise/dialogue-arrival.md` and `../congolaise/dialogue-arrival.md`.*

## Scene 1 · The colectivo (terminal → hotel)

> **Visitor.** Bondiā!
> *— "Morning!"*
>
> **Driver.** Bondiā, bondiā. Kus-lô?
> *good-day. where-to?* — "Morning. Where to?"
>
> **Visitor.** Otelô Pindô-lô. Mis prēu?
> *hotel Pindô-to. what price?* — "To the Hotel Pindó. How much?"
>
> **Driver.** Xeç austrāl.
> *six austral.* — "Six australs." *(dozenal count, crust currency-word)*
>
> **Visitor.** Dālē. Um kaug?
> *OK. is far?* — "Fine. Is it far?"
>
> **Driver.** Nē, prop. Trankīlô — düna minutô.
> *no, near. easy — twelve minutes.* — "No, close. Relax — about twelve minutes."
>
> **Visitor.** Grasiē.
> *thanks.* — "Thanks."

## Scene 2 · The hotel desk

> **Clerk.** Bondiā! Velkô.
> *good-day! welcome.* — "Hello, welcome."
>
> **Visitor.** Bondiā. Mä volē kambôr — dva notēt.
> *good-day. I want room — two nights.* — "Hello. I'd like a room, for two nights." *(partitive
> after the numeral, coursebook rule)*
>
> **Clerk.** Jā. Pāsportô, bidô.
> *yes. passport, please.* — "Certainly. Your passport, please." *(pāsportô, crust)*
>
> **Visitor.** Sīn. Wifī um?
> *here. wi-fi is?* — "Here you go. Is there wi-fi?"
>
> **Clerk.** Jā, wifī um jôvā. Sīn um vôtī.
> *yes, wi-fi is good. here is key.* — "Yes, good wi-fi. Here's your key." *(wifī, vôtī crust)*
>
> **Visitor.** Grasiē. Agua um jôvā?
> *thanks. water is good?* — "Thank you. Is the tap water safe?"
>
> **Clerk.** Jā, jā — jōdô trankīlô.
> *yes, yes — drink easy.* — "Yes — drink away, no worries."
>
> **Visitor.** Lindô. Grasiē.
> *lovely. thanks.* — "Great. Thanks."

## Scene 3 · The café

> **Waiter.** Bondiā! Mis sä volē?
> *good-day! what you want?* — "Hello! What can I get you?"
>
> **Visitor.** Bondiā. Kafē... vai matē?
> *good-day. coffee… or mate?* — "Hello. A coffee — or, actually, is there mate?"
>
> **Waiter.** Matē, muidū. Ja çipā um kalēnt.
> *mate, of course. and chipá is hot.* — "Mate, of course. And the chipá is fresh out."
>
> **Visitor.** Dālē — matē ja çipā, bidô.
> *OK — mate and chipá, please.* — "Perfect — the mate and a chipá, please."
>
> **Waiter.** Ja menjā? Milanēsā um lindô avuī.
> *and eat? milanesa is lovely today.* — "Something to eat? The milanesa's good today."
>
> **Visitor.** Jā, milanēsā tambē.
> *yes, milanesa too.* — "Sure, a milanesa as well."
>
> *— later —*
>
> **Visitor.** Um magūs sūr. Grasiē. Prēu, bidô?
> *is tasty big. thanks. price, please?* — "That was excellent. Thank you. The bill, please?"
>
> **Waiter.** Sīn. Kartô um jôvā.
> *here. card is good.* — "Here. Card is fine." *(kartô, crust)*
>
> **Visitor.** Grasiē. Çāu!
> *thanks. bye!* — "Thanks. Bye!"
>
> **Waiter.** Çāu, niñô. Bon kamī!
> *bye, dear. good road!* — "Bye now. Safe travels!"

## What it demonstrates (a modern register, working)

- **Two layers, cleanly split.** The *infrastructure* is the shared crust, identical from Daxla to
  Ndar to Venlasport: *otelô, pāsportô, wifī, kambôr, vôtī, kartô, minutô, austrāl*. The *human*
  layer — greeting, thanks, taste, ease — is Verdenese: *bondiā, grasiē, dālē, trankīlô, lindô,
  çāu, matē, çipā, milanēsā*. A visitor learns the crust once for the whole language; the
  Verdenese is the warmth.
- **The spine carries the modern conversation.** Questions (*Mis prēu?*, *Kus-lô?*, *Wifī um?*),
  the cases (*Otelô Pindô-lô* "to the hotel," *dva notēt* the partitive after a numeral), the
  dozenal price (*xeç, düna*) — all coursebook grammar, the same a mainlander uses.
- **The shallow asymmetry, audible.** A mainlander overhearing this manages more of it than in any
  sister capital — *kafē, matē, milanēsā, grasiē* half-open themselves — until the land-word
  arrives: *çipā* [Gn.] opens to nobody. The Level 3a/3b split of `verdenese.md` §2, in a café.
- **The national ritual gates the scene** — the waiter's *muidū* ("of course") to the mate
  question is the point: you can order coffee in Venlasport, but you were always going to be
  offered mate.
- **Contemporary, not quaint.** Card payment, wi-fi, safe tap water (a quiet contrast with the
  Ndar scene's bottled *ndiyam*), the affectionate service-register *niñô*, and the ordinary local
  sign-off *Çāu — bon kamī!*
