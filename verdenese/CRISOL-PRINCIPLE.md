# The Crisol Principle — the doctrine of Verdenese

*Verdenese's answer to the mainland CREOLE-PRINCIPLE (`../college/CREOLE-PRINCIPLE.md`, §79) and
to the Congolaise CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE. Where Saharannaise reroots into one substrate pair and
Congolaise fuses three peer tributaries, Verdenese is a **settler crisol** — the melting-pot of
the immigrant ships (Sp. crisol, Pt. crisol, It. crogiolo: the crucible) poured onto the Nelôxian
frame. This document is the rule that keeps the pot from boiling over: Romance saturation without
Romance grammar, and a land-layer that stays the land's.*

Valdória's ground truth is unlike the Sahara's diglossia or the river valley's three-way
multilingualism. It is an **immigrant frontier of the 1800s**: Italians, Portuguese and
Brazilians, Spanish-speakers, Germans, Swiss, Poles, Ukrainians, Basques, settled by a Nelôxian
company on sparse subtropical land — with **no one's language in the majority**. Nelôxi was the
neutral frame all of them could take up, and their vocabularies poured into it. The doctrine is
faithful to that ground: the Romance mass fuses (because Portuguese, Italian, and Spanish were the
demographic mass and are cousins to begin with), the smaller communities leave bounded layers, and
the land — whose people nobody in the settlement learned to speak with — contributes **names, not
structure**.

---

## 1 · The quarry map — who owns what

The core rule, exactly parallel to §79's "borrow from the quarry that owns the domain." When a gap
opens in Verdenese, the root comes from the owner below — not from whichever the writer knows
best, and never from a Baltic quarry for a Valdórian reality.

| Quarry | Role in the crisol | Owns the domains of |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Portuguese & Italian** | the twin heavyweights — the two largest immigrant masses, fused past separating | the domestic core: home, family, food and the table, the field and the harvest, feeling, courtesy, the everyday city |
| **Spanish** | the ambient layer — the neighbor-continent's tongue, soaked in rather than shipped in | the market and the road, ranching and the campo, courtesy formulae, the shared South American vocabulary (*mate, asado, estancia*); fuses deepest in the Spanish-zone provinces (`regional-varieties.md`) |
| **Guaraní** | **the land's donor language — a loan-layer, NOT a substrate.** Nobody local learned it; it names what was here first | flora, fauna, rivers and waters, terrain, local foods and phenomena, toponyms — and nothing beyond the land |
| **German** | a minor bounded layer — the Riograndenser and Swiss colonies | some food, farming, and craft terms (*kukā, ximiā*) |
| **English** | **a neighbor language, NOT a quarry** — the living community language of the west (`verdenese.md` §6) | a bounded loan-layer only: rail, rolling stock, industry, homestead and church vocabulary (`regional-varieties.md`) |
| **Romance-Latin** *(shared with all standards)* | the learned crust, the transparent top | law, administration, high scholarship, formal & ceremonial naming |
| **Livonian-Karelian** *(shared, invariant)* | the grammar | the spine — cases, negation *äb*, derivation, dozenal number; **never touched** |

**The convergence rule.** Portuguese, Italian, and Spanish are cousins, and on the frontier their
words fused where they already agreed. Where the three converge on one shape, Verdenese takes the
convergent form, tagged **[Rom.]** (*festā* ← festa/festa/fiesta; *fīlô* ← filho/figlio/hijo ←
Lat. *filius*). Where they diverge, the form that won **records which community settled that
domain**, and the tag says so: the table leans Italian (*polentā, ñoki, latē*), the countryside
leans Portuguese (*kexô, gaūxô*), the road and the ranch lean Spanish (*asādô, estansiā, dālē*).
Divergence is data, exactly as the Congolaise tie-break is.

**The land-layer line.** The Guaraní rule cuts the other way from every substrate in the system:
it is generous inside its domain and absolute at its edge. The jaguar, the yerba, the river, the
forest, the armadillo — Guaraní, thickly, without apology (*jaguaretē, kaʼa, Yguasū, kaʼaguy,
tatū*). Mother, bread, house, work — never (*mamā, pan, kasā, laburô*). This mirrors how real
regional Spanish and Portuguese absorbed Guaraní: hundreds of local-specific words, none of the
grammar, no domestic core. A Guaraní root proposed for a hearth-domain meaning is a wrong-well
rejection every time.

## 2 · The crisol phonology — one Verdenese, many sources

The donors are harmonized into **one consistent sound and one orthography**. The rules extend the
shared orthographic agreement (`../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md` §2) as the sisters' did, and
no further:

| Feature | Rule | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **The puso ʼ** | Guaraní glottal stop **kept** in land-layer words — the stratum's signature scar, as Saharannaise wears *ṭ ṣ ḍ ẓ* and Congolaise wears *ɓ ɗ*. This is Verdenese's audible fingerprint. | *kaʼa* "yerba", *kaʼaguy* "forest", *mandiʼô* "manioc" |
| **Nasal vowels refused** | Guaraní *ã ẽ ĩ õ ũ ỹ* are **not** imported — no redundancy with the archive's vowels. Nasality resolves to an adjacent written nasal (*n, m, ñ*, or a prenasal onset) or flattens. | *ñeʼẽmbuku → Ñeʼembukū*, *ñandú → ñandū*, *ñatiʼũ → ñatiʼū* |
| **Prenasalized onsets** | *mb, nd, ng* **kept** as onset digraphs (letters already in the archive; the Congolaise precedent) | *mburukujā* "passionflower", *mborevī* "tapir" |
| **Guaraní y [ɨ]** | flattens to the archive's **y** — the flattening recorded, as Mande *ɔ→ô* is | *kaʼaguy*, *kapiʼyva* "capybara", *Yguasū* |
| **Final stress → macron** | Guaraní and Romance final stress lands as a long final vowel (initial stress stays the spine's, the length keeps the source audible) | *ñandú → ñandū*, *tatú → tatū*, *avati → avatī* |
| **ch / c(e,i) [tʃ] → ç** | Spanish/Italian affricate to the archive's *ç* (the Canarian precedent) | *chipá → çipā*, *ciao → çāu*, *charlar → çarlā* |
| **Portuguese ch / x [ʃ] → x** | the archive's *x* [ʃ] serves | *churrasco → xuraskô*, *peixe → pexē* |
| **Spanish j / g [x] → h** | the open breath, not the Nelôxi *x* (Canarian rule) | *Jujuy → Huhūi*, *Tarija → Tariha* |
| **gn / nh / ñ → ñ** | ñ is already a Nelôxi letter | *gnocchi → ñoki*, *ñandú → ñandū* |
| **seseo** | the settler Romance never had [θ]; *z / ce / ci* land as **s** | *dulce → dulsē* |
| **-ão / -ón → -ôn** | the Romance nasal finals flatten to the archive's *ôn* | *peón → peôn*, *misiones → Misjôn* |
| **Geminates simplified** | *tt→t, ll→l, rr→r, zz→s* — length in Verdenese is the macron's job | *latte → latē*, *tranquillo → trankīlô* |
| **No long ō** | shared rule; a Verdenese long-o is written **ô**, and the agent suffix stays exactly **-ôr** | *formosa → Formôsa* |

The extension is **closed**: a submission may not invent orthography outside this table without a
ruling from the seat. The one new grapheme over the mainland inventory is exactly **ʼ** (the
puso) — everything else is letters and digraphs the archive already owns.

## 3 · The hybrid seam — root borrowed, morpheme refused

The proof-pattern is *žəmlôr* and *gaynôr*, and Verdenese needs its discipline **more** than any
sister: Romance is cousin to the learned crust, so a Romance agent suffix looks almost legal — and
is refused all the same. The root is taken bare; the Nelôxian *-ôr* does all agent-formation:

| Concept | Source word | Native agent morpheme *(refused)* | Verdenese form |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| drover, cattle-driver | Pt. *tropeiro* | *-eiro* | **tropôr** (*tropa-* + *-ôr*) |
| yerba-harvester | Sp. *yerbatero* (also *tarefero*) | *-ero* | **jerbôr** (*jerbā-* + *-ôr*) |
| settler, homesteader | Rom. *colono* | — | **kolonôr** (*kolôniā-* + *-ôr*) |
| planter | Rom. *plantador* | *-dor* | **plantôr** (*plantā-* + *-ôr*) |
| baker | Sp. *panadero* / Pt. *padeiro* | *-ero / -eiro* | **panôr** (*pan-* + *-ôr*) |
| field-hand | Pt. *campeiro* | *-eiro* | **kampôr** (*kampô-* + *-ôr*) |
| yerba worker (Gn. frame) | Gn. *kaʼa apohára* | *-hára* | *(same jerbôr — the Guaraní agent never enters)* |

The refused list — Portuguese **-eiro**, Spanish **-ero**, Italian **-aio/-iere**, the Romance
**-dor**, Guaraní **-hára** — is not exhaustive; the rule is general: if it is a donor *ending*,
it stays out, and the Nelôxian ending does the job. Likewise verbs: a Romance verb enters as a
bare stem on the Nelôxi conjugation (*laburā* "to work" → past *laburai*; *mateā* "to drink mate"
→ *mateai*), never with its own conjugation. Every *-ôr* form and every *-ai* past is a small
demonstration that the spine is Nelôxian and only the well of roots is austral.

## 4 · What this doctrine forbids

1. **No Baltic calque for a Valdórian reality.** The yerba is *kaʼa*, not a "green-leaf" compound;
   the flood-forest is *kaʼaguy*, not a "wet-wood." Name the reality; do not translate it. (The
   mainland compound ban, `../saharannaise/saharannaise.md` §1, applies in full.)
2. **Guaraní stays the land's.** Never structural, never domestic-core: no Guaraní kin terms, no
   Guaraní function words, no Guaraní grammar. The land-layer line (§1) is absolute at its edge.
3. **English stays bounded.** A rail word in the west (*swiç, jārd*) is legal; an English
   hearth-word anywhere is a wrong-well rejection. English is a neighbor language under the
   Finland/Canada settlement (`verdenese.md` §6), not a quarry.
4. **No borrowed morphology.** Roots only (§3). The Romance endings are the sharpest temptation in
   the whole system precisely because they look like the crust; refuse them.
5. **No private spellings.** One crisol phonology (§2). A root's origin shows in the root — and,
   for the land-layer, in the puso — never in an off-inventory grapheme.
6. **The grammar never changes.** Verdenese takes none of Maré's licensed sanding; the Maritime
   Amendment is Maré's alone. The spine is what makes it one language.
