## 1. Finite Verbs

### Core principle
Verbs are **isolating**: **no person or number agreement.** Pronouns and context supply
subject/number. This is the language's most un-Finnic feature (Finnic languages conjugate
heavily for person; Nelôxi does not) — Finnic noun morphology, analytic verb syntax.

| Form | Marker | Example | Gloss |
|------|--------|---------|-------|
| Present (dictionary) | — | *mä donā* / *tä donā* / *ne donā* | I / she / they give |
| Past | **-i** | *donā → donai*, *lǟdô → lǟdi*, *kantā → kantāi* | gave, went, sang |
| Conditional | **-ks** | *voleks*, *tuleks*, *pidāks* | would want / come / should |
| Future | — (none) | *Demā mä lǟdô* | Tomorrow I go |

- Copula is suppletive and person-blind: **um** (all persons, present) / **ūli** (past).
- No aspect (no perfective/imperfective distinction).
- Dictionary form serves both present and citation; no separate infinitive.
- **Stem note:** a long final vowel may shorten before **-ks**: *tulē* "come" → *tuleks*.

---

## 2. Negation

Particle **äb** precedes the bare verb; the stem never changes.

| Positive | Negative |
|----------|----------|
| *mä donā* | *mä äb donā* |
| *sä volē* | *sä äb volē* |
| *ne lǟdô* | *ne äb lǟdô* |

Standalone "no" is **nē**.

## 3. Modality & Mood

### Modals (analytic — precede the plain verb)
| Modal | Gloss | Example |
|-------|-------|---------|
| *vôī* | can | *mä vôī parlā* "I can speak" |
| *pidā* | must | *mä pidā lǟdô* "I must go" |

### Conditional (**-ks**)
| Verb | Conditional | Gloss |
|------|-------------|-------|
| *volē* | *voleks* | would want |
| *tulē* | *tuleks* | would come |
| *pidā* | *pidāks* | should (lit. "would have to") |

*Predicted but not yet attested:* modal + conditional (*vôīks* "could") is a regular
formation but does NOT yet appear in canon. Flag as a gap; do not treat as attested.

---

## 4. Participles

| Form | Suffix | Function | Example |
|------|--------|----------|---------|
| Doer | **-ji** | agent noun / reduced relative | *lugēji* "reader"; *lugēji läpx* "the child who reads" |
| Done | **-dôt** | perfect/passive participle | *donādôt livrô* "the given book" |

**Relative fallback:** when a participle can't carry the load (past tense, its own objects),
a full **ken/mis** clause takes over:
> *mēs, ken kantāi vespôrôl* — "the man who was singing" (beside compact *kantāji mēs*)

## 5. Derivational morphology (verb-involved)

### Verb → derived word (four distinct lanes)
| Suffix | Type | Example | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|---------|
| **-ji** | doer (person) | *lugē → lugēji* | reader |
| **-in** | device/instrument | *kūlô → kūlin* | speaker |
| **-mus / -mys** | result/abstract noun (harmonized) | *valdā → valdômus* | government |
| **-ām / -äm** | place/arena of the action | *pēlā → pēlām* | field |

### Noun → verb (denominal)
| Suffix | Type | Example | Meaning |
|--------|------|---------|---------|
| **-stā / -stä** | "engage in / work with X" | *kalā → kalāstā* | to fish |
| **-ldā / -ldä** | mental-activity verb (linking *-ôldā/-eldä* after C) | *môtô → môtôldā* | to think |

---

## Summary

Every verb's full finite paradigm is **four forms**: bare (present), **-i** (past),
**-ks** (conditional), plus the two participles **-ji** / **-dôt**. No agreement, no future,
no aspect. Rich agglutinative *noun* morphology beside near-zero-inflection *verbs* — the
deliberate signature of the language.
