# Congolaise Lexicon — Domain P · Batch 2
### Cattle & Pasture · Kin · The Body · The Seasons

Archived under the Kēļs Penc per `congolaise.md`. This is the **Pulaar heartland** — the domestic,
pastoral, bodily core that the Confluence Principle (`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §1) assigns almost
wholly to Pulaar, the living productive tributary. It is the **Level-3 core**: a mainlander is
illiterate here without training, and that is the target. Tributary tags **[Pul.] [Mnd.] [Wol.]**;
fused phonology (§2) applied. **Congolaise canon only.**

## Rulings applied on archive

- **P·1 — the domestic core is Pulaar, and that is the doctrine, not a default.** Every headword
  here sits in a domain the Confluence Principle assigns to Pulaar (kin, body, cattle, season).
  A Wolof or Mande root proposed for any of them is a **wrong-well** rejection (§5).
- **P·2 — the scar words are load-bearing.** *ɓiɗo, ɓernde, huɗo, cēɗu* (ɓ/ɗ) and *koyŋal* (ŋ)
  carry the graphemes that mark the stratum. They are archived with the scars intact; sanding
  them to *biɗo/bernde/koyngal* would erase the fingerprint and is barred (§3, doctrine).
- **P·3 — *wuro* is one of three.** Archived with the explicit three-way settlement split
  (*wuro/dugu/dək*); glossing it as a bare "village" that competes with the others is the error the
  tie-break rule exists to prevent.
- **P·4 — *pulāku* kept whole and untranslated.** A cultural keyword, not a dictionary equation;
  the gloss is a description, not a synonym. Same treatment the mainland gives its own untranslatables.
- **P·5 — *-ôr* only.** *gaynôr* archived; *gaynaako* logged as refused morphology.
