# Congolaise Lexicon — Domain G · Batch 3
### Gold & Trade · Craft & Textile · Griot & Music · Town & Hospitality

Archived under the Kēļs Penc per `congolaise.md`. This is the **cultural signature** of the state —
the trans-Saharan gold road (Soninke-Mande, the classical layer), the crafts and the griot, and
the hospitality and market of the town (Wolof, the edge). It carries the standard's richest
**back-flow candidates**: words the wider world already half-knows (*kora*, *jembe*, *teraanga*).
Tributary tags **[Pul.] [Mnd.] [Wol.]**; fused phonology (§2) applied. **Congolaise canon only.**

**32 headwords approved.**

## Rulings applied on archive

- **G·1 — gold, salt, road, rule are Soninke-Mande, and that is the classical layer doing its
  job.** These are the trans-Saharan trade-and-statecraft register; a Wolof or Pulaar root proposed
  for *sanu/kôgô/jula/mansa* is a **wrong-well** rejection. The prestige of gold speaks the old
  trade tongue.
- **G·2 — the settlement triad closes here.** *dugu* [Mnd.] archived alongside *wuro* [Pul., Batch
  P] and *dək* [Wol.]: the polity, the homestead, the lived city. Three canon words, no merge,
  the tie-break rule (`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §1) made concrete across three batches.
- **G·3 — the griot splits, and the split is canon.** *jeli* (lineage-keeper, classical) and
  *gewəl* (performer, edge) are both archived; collapsing them to one word would erase the real
  division of the institution and is barred.
- **G·4 — *kolon* ↔ *bīr* logged as a cross-standard doublet.** The same object (a dug well) carries
  a Mande word in the river standard and a Hassaniya word in the Saharan one; both canon in their
  seat, bridged by the region tags, never merged.
- **G·5 — back-flow slate.** *jula, kora, jembe, terānga, bôgôlan* flagged as prestige loans likely
  to drift into the metropolitan and Atlantic lexicons (the music and hospitality terms especially).
  Promotion to another standard's canon only by that seat's merge, never automatic.
- **G·6 — *-ôr* only, again.** *sanuôr, kolonôr, duguôr, jayôr, rabôr* archived; the source agents
  *kolontigi, dugutigi, ràbbkat* logged as refused morphology. The seam holds across all three
  tributaries — which is the whole demonstration.
