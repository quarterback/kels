# Congolaise — the Riverine standard of Nelôxi

*Congolaise kēļ.* The Nelôxi of **Soninka & Toro** — the Senegal-River / Sahel state of the
African bloc (see `../world/african-bloc.md`), demonym **Sotorans**: the **third full standard**
of the language, a **co-standard**, not a dialect. It runs the full Nelôxi grammatical skeleton
unaltered and reroots its domestic, ecological, and cultural lexicon into the **Niger-Congo
tongues of the river valley** — **Pulaar, Soninke-Mande, and Wolof** — while keeping the
Romance-Latin learned crust it shares with the mainland and with Saharannaise. Governed from its
own seat, the **Kēļs Penc** (the Forum of the Language), counted on its own terms, bridged to the
system by tags, never merged into the coursebook.

## Why it exists — the thesis at a higher power

Metropolitan proves the sturdiness thesis on the Baltic quarries; Saharannaise proves it on a
single Afro-Asiatic substrate. Congolaise proves it **one level up**: the grammar carries not a
reroot but a **whole fused creole** — three unrelated Niger-Congo tongues converged under one
doctrine (the **Confluence Principle**, `CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md`) — and still parses, still
declines, still negates with *äb* and commands with a bare stem. If the Livonian–Karelian
skeleton can carry a three-way Sahelian creole from the far side of a continent and hold, then
the skeleton is not inheritance but infrastructure, and Nelôxi is a real language three times
over. *ɓiɗo* "child" is the fingerprint word the way *žəml* is for Saharannaise: a Pulaar root
wearing the stratum's implosive scar, declining like any native noun.

## The model — pluricentric, three sovereignties, one spine

Nelôxi is **pluricentric across three seats** now, not two — the fullest form of the Lusophone
pattern (Lisbon ↔ Brasília ↔ Luanda): one grammar, three standards, three academies, none
supreme. Soninka & Toro is a **sovereign state** of the African bloc, a peer of Atlanta and of
the mainland; its seat is a peer of theirs because the states are peers.

- **Metropolitan Nelôxi** — the coursebook, governed by the **Kēļs Kolēgi** (the College of
  Language), headed by the **Rector**. Five-quarry Baltic creole; the trunk.
- **Saharannaise** — the Atlantic standard, governed by the **Kēļs Dīwān** (the Saharan
  Register), headed by the **Amīn**. Hassaniya/Zenaga (Afro-Asiatic); Atlanta and Adrāra.
- **Congolaise** — the Riverine standard, governed by the **Kēļs Penc** (the Forum of the
  Language; English: the Confluence Register), headed by the **Ardo** (Pulaar *ardo*, "the
  guide/leader"; English: the Warden). Niger-Congo (Pulaar/Soninke/Wolof); Soninka & Toro.

The three seats are named in one frame — **Kēļs Kolēgi ↔ Kēļs Dīwān ↔ Kēļs Penc**, the same
Nelôxi genitive *kēļs* "of the language," the institution-noun drawn from each seat's own learned
register: Romance *kolēgi* (college), Hassaniya *dīwān* (chancery), Wolof ***penc*** (the village
assembly-square, the public forum where a community deliberates). The seat's own name enacts its
doctrine: where *Dīwān* is a written chancery, *Penc* is a **convening ground** — the right image
for a standard whose whole idea is three tongues meeting. And like *dīwān*, *penc* is itself a
first headword of the standard it names.

Neither seat owns another. What makes the three **one language** is not low divergence — the
vocabularies diverge heavily and by design — but the **shared spine**: the common grammar and the
common orthographic agreement, `../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md`, which Congolaise obeys
exactly as Saharannaise does.

## §1 · The Root Ecology — a three-way confluence

**Kept — the grammatical skeleton.** Unchanged mainland grammar: no verb agreement, strict SVO,
pre-verbal negation *äb*, bare-stem imperative and hortative *-m*, the full derivational suffix
set (**-ôr, -in, -mus/-mys, -ū, -stā, -ldā, -ām/-äm, -tū/-ty**, causative *-tā*, comparative
*-mi*), dozenal number. A mainlander parses any Congolaise sentence's structure effortlessly.

**Replaced — the domestic root stock, sourced by domain.** For terrain and river, floodplain
farming, pastoralism, kin, the body, the seasons, gold and trade, craft, music, and hospitality —
the whole lived layer — the roots are Niger-Congo, drawn from **whichever tributary owns the
domain** (the Confluence Principle, `CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §1): Soninke-Mande for the classical
trade-and-statecraft register, Pulaar for the pastoral-domestic core, Wolof for the coastal-urban
edge. The Baltic quarries are shut out of this layer entirely.

**Retained — the Romance-Latin learned crust.** Law, administration, scholarship, and formal
naming keep the shared Romance layer, transparent to the mainland and to Atlanta. This is what
keeps Level-1 legible in all three directions, and why the *Nelôxian* half of a Sotoran placename
is Romance while the Congolaise half is Niger-Congo.

So Congolaise is three strata like its sisters — a **Romance learned crust** (shared, Level 1), the
**Nelôxi grammar** (invariant), and a **Niger-Congo domestic core** (rerooted, Level 3) — but its
core is itself a *fused* three-tributary creole, which is what distinguishes it from Saharannaise's
single substrate.

## §2 · The Asymmetric Intelligibility Standard

Three tiers of transparency to the mainland, by register, identical in shape to Saharannaise §2:

1. **Level 1 — formal, state & archival** (high written intelligibility). Decrees, manifests,
   law. The Romance crust and the shared spine carry it; a mainland clerk reads it.
2. **Level 2 — urban vernacular** (asymmetric). The market at Ndar: the structural bridge holds,
   the content drifts. One or two roots swap (*markôt → lumo*). A Sotoran, schooled in the
   standard, follows the mainland easily; the mainlander needs exposure. The Brazil-reads-Lisbon
   asymmetry, again one-way.
3. **Level 3 — deep interior / domestic** (near-zero). The cattle-camp, the floodplain field, the
   family compound, the griot's recitation. Fully rerooted across all three tributaries; a
   mainlander is illiterate here without training. That is the target, not a failure.

## §3 · Orthographic sanctioning

Congolaise writes in the Nelôxian state-archive script under the shared orthographic agreement
(`../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md` §2), extended by the **fused-phonology table** in
`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §2 — and no further. In brief: implosives **ɓ ɗ** kept as the stratum
scar; prenasal onsets **mb nd nj ng** kept; **ŋ** and **ñ** legal; long vowels to macrons and
*oo→ô*; geminate consonants simplified; Wolof **ë → ə** (the shared schwa); Mande **ɔ→ô, ɛ→e**;
tone unwritten; Pulaar ATR harmony neutralized under the spine's own front/back harmony. The new
graphemes over the mainland inventory are exactly **ɓ ɗ ŋ** plus the shared **ə**.

## §4 · Hybrid morphology — the seam, with a discipline

Niger-Congo root + mainland derivational suffix, fully productive: **demôr** (farmer), **napôr**
(fisher), **kolonôr** (well-keeper), **sanuôr** (gold-panner). The suffix keeps its exact mainland
form. The discipline unique to Congolaise: each tributary has its **own** agent morpheme
(*-tigi, -kat, -oowo, -aako*) and Congolaise borrows **none** of them — the root is taken bare and
*-ôr* does the work (`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §3). Root borrowed, morpheme refused.

## §5 · The evaluation matrix

Submissions are processed with three actions, logged in the lexicon files:

1. **APPROVE & ARCHIVE** — assign the domain and its owning tributary (§1 of the doctrine), apply
   the fused phonology (§2), refuse any borrowed morpheme (§3), record the entry with source root,
   tributary tag, gloss, and any cross-standard doublet.
2. **REJECT — WRONG WELL** — flag a Baltic root/calque, *or* a root drawn from the wrong tributary
   for its domain, and correct it to the domain-owner's root.
3. **BACK-FLOW ASSESSMENT** — judge whether a Congolaise trade, music, or river term has drifted
   into the metropolitan or Atlantic lexicon as a prestige loan (*kora*, *teraanga*, *jula* are
   natural candidates). Noted here; becomes another standard's canon only by that seat's merge.

## Accounting

Congolaise headwords are counted **on their own terms**, as a third peer lexicon — not folded into
the coursebook total, not into Saharannaise's. They do not enter `../data/*`, the charter §7 list,
or the coursebook. Shared and back-flow words bridge by region tag **(Cong.)**, per the
COMMON-REGISTER convention, which this standard joins as a third tag alongside (Metr.) and (Sah.).
