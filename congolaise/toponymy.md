# Soninka & Toro — Toponymy & Gazetteer (Nelôxian | Congolaise)

The place-names of **Soninka & Toro**, in both official renderings. Formal name **the River State
of Soninka & Toro**; short endonym **Soninka & Toro** — a double-named country (the
Trinidad-and-Tobago model), the two halves being the **Soninke** upper river and **Toro** (Futa
Toro) on the middle river. The territory is the Senegal-River valley and its Sahel — the green,
riverine, agricultural member of the African bloc (see `../world/african-bloc.md`), with its own
standard (Congolaise) and **bilingual official naming**. Every place carries two archive names,
written **Nelôxian | Congolaise**.

Because Nelôxia reached this coast and river **before any French claim, there are no French names
to keep** — no *Saint-Louis*, no *Bakel* in a colonial spelling. Each name is rendered in the two
standards' own roots.

## The two naming registers

As in Atlanta (`../saharannaise/toponymy.md`), the pairing is the two registers the state actually
writes in:

- **Nelôxian (metropolitan)** draws on the **Romance-Latin learned crust** — the shared cultivation
  register that names chartered places on the mainland and in Atlanta. So the great river's formal
  name is the native **Flūmô** "the river" (Lat. *flumen*), not a French form.
- **Congolaise (local)** is the Niger-Congo name run through the Confluence phonology
  (`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §2) — sourced from the tributary that owns the place: the classical
  Soninke-Mande for the old trade and gold sites, Pulaar for the pastoral interior, Wolof for the
  coast and the port.

Romance naming is a **point-foundation, not a wave**: the marquee places — the capital, the river,
the two regions, the gold field, the confluence — get a distinct Romance metropolitan name. Minor
waypoints (river towns, wells, floodplain villages) are **adopted** directly, the local name
serving both registers.

## The river & its country

| Feature | Nelôxian | Congolaise | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| the great river | **Flūmô** (Lat. *flumen* "river") | **Māyo** [Pul.] | Romance point-foundation | native Pulaar core-word (lexicon R). The spine of the whole state. |
| the floodplain province | **Provinsô Viridô** ("green province") | **Wālo** [Wol./Pul.] | The flood-recession land (lexicon R); the fertile, farmed, populous half. |
| the dryland province | **Provinsô Aridô** ("dry province") | **Jēri** [Wol./Pul.] | The rainfed interior; pastoral, Pulaar, sparser. The *Wālo/Jēri* split is the state's geography. |
| the confluence | **Konfluô** (Lat. *confluere*) | **Daŋga-Māyo** ("river-meeting") | Where the tributary meets the main river — and the state's own emblem: the Confluence Principle made a place. |

## The historic halves

| Region | Nelôxian | Congolaise | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| the Soninke upper river | **Regjô Superô** ("upper region") | **Gajāga** [Mnd.] | The old Soninke trade country (historic Gajaaga/Galam); the classical, gold-road, caravan half. |
| Toro, the middle river | **Regjô Mediô** ("middle region") | **Futa** [Pul.] | Futa Toro, the Pulaar heartland; pastoral and scholarly, the domestic core of the state. |

## Rulings applied on archive

- **T·1 — the source tributary names the place.** Gold and caravan sites take Soninke-Mande names
  (*Gajāga, Sanudugu, Bakəl, Kolon-Sira*); the pastoral heartland takes a Pulaar name (*Futa*); the
  port and the market take Wolof names (*Ndar, Lumo-Wālo*). Toponymy obeys the Confluence
  Principle's domain map exactly as the lexicon does.
- **T·2 — no French substrate.** As Atlanta carries no Spanish names, Soninka & Toro carries no
  French ones; the priority of Nelôxian settlement is written into the map.
- **T·3 — the confluence is the state's emblem.** *Konfluô | Daŋga-Māyo* is named deliberately: the
  meeting of waters is the same figure as the meeting of tongues the standard is built on.
- **T·4 — the demonym is shared and fused.** *Sotoran* is one word in both registers — the state's
  two names welded, the way the standard welds its three tributaries.

*The place-name roots draw on lexicons [R](lexicon-R-river-floodplain-agriculture.md) and
[G](lexicon-G-gold-trade-craft-griot-hospitality.md); the register logic mirrors Atlanta's
`../saharannaise/toponymy.md`; the state context is `../world/african-bloc.md`.*
