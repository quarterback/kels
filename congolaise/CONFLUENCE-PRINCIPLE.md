# The Confluence Principle — the doctrine of Congolaise

*Congolaise's answer to the mainland CREOLE-PRINCIPLE (`../college/CREOLE-PRINCIPLE.md`, §79).
Where Saharannaise reroots into a single substrate/superstrate pair (Zenaga under Hassaniya),
Congolaise is a **three-quarry Niger-Congo creole** on the Nelôxian frame. This document is the
rule that makes those three tongues into one language rather than a grab-bag.*

The Senegal-River valley is not two-layer diglossia the way the Sahara is. It is a genuine
three-way living multilingualism — **Pulaar, Soninke, and Wolof** side by side as peer tongues,
each with its own domains of authority. Congolaise is faithful to that ground: it does not pick
one and demote the others; it assigns each its territory and lets them **converge** on the shared
Nelôxian spine, the way three tributaries converge into the river the state is named for. Hence
the doctrine's name, and hence the seat's — the **Kēļs Penc**, the Forum of the Language, the
place where the three convene.

---

## 1 · Domain ownership — which quarry owns what

The core rule, exactly parallel to §79's "borrow from the quarry that owns the domain." When a
gap opens in Congolaise, the root comes from **whichever of the three tributaries owns that
domain** — not from whichever the writer knows best, and never from a Baltic quarry.

| Tributary | Role in the confluence | Owns the domains of |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Soninke / Mande** | the classical, prestige, deep-time layer — the Ghana-Empire trade tongue, the trans-Saharan gold road | long-distance trade, gold & salt, caravan & wells, statecraft & rule, measures, the ancestral and ceremonial register, the griot-as-lineage-keeper |
| **Pulaar** | the living productive core — ~40 million speakers, pastoral and scholarly, the deepest well | cattle & pastoralism, land & pasture, kinship, the body, the seasons & weather, the bulk of daily domestic life, Islamic scholarship |
| **Wolof** | the modern coastal & urban edge — the lingua franca, the growth surface | the coast & fishing, the urban market, city life, media & performance, hospitality & tourism, slang and new vocabulary |
| **Romance-Latin** *(shared with Metropolitan)* | the learned crust, the transparent top | law, administration, high scholarship, formal & ceremonial naming |
| **Livonian-Karelian** *(shared, invariant)* | the grammar | the spine — cases, negation *äb*, derivation, dozenal number; **never touched** |

**The tie-break.** When two tributaries both carry a root for one meaning, the split is by
*register*, not by coin-toss:

- **classical / ceremonial → Soninke-Mande.** *dugu* "the town as polity."
- **everyday / domestic → Pulaar.** *wuro* "the settled homestead."
- **urban / modern / lived-city → Wolof.** *dək* "the city you live in."

So "settlement" is not one word fought over but **three words doing three jobs** — *dugu* the
polity, *wuro* the homestead, *dək* the city. That is the confluence working: divergence that
*records* which social world the speaker is standing in. Likewise the griot: **jeli** is the
hereditary lineage-keeper (Mande, classical), **gewəl** is the praise-singer and performer
(Wolof, edge) — one institution, split by register across two tributaries.

## 2 · The fused phonology — one Congolaise, three sources

The three donors are harmonized into **one consistent sound and one orthography** — not three
separate scar-sets bolted together. A reader meets a single Congolaise phonology; the source
tributary shows in the *root*, not in a private spelling. The rules extend the shared
orthographic agreement (`../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md` §2) as Saharannaise's did, and no
further:

| Feature | Rule | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Implosives ɓ, ɗ** | **Kept** — the stratum's signature scar, as Saharannaise wears its emphatics *ṭ ṣ ḍ ẓ*. This is Congolaise's audible fingerprint. | *ɓiɗo* "child", *ɓernde* "heart", *huɗo* "grass" |
| **Prenasalized onsets** | *mb, nd, nj, ng* **kept** as onset digraphs | *mbēwa* "goat", *ndiyam* "water", *ngesa* "field" |
| **Velar & palatal nasals** | **ŋ** (velar) kept; **ñ** (palatal) kept — *ñ* already canon (*Muntāñ*) | *koyŋal* "leg", *fôñe* "fonio" |
| **Long / double vowels** | compress to a macron: *aa→ā, ii→ī, uu→ū, ee→ē*; **oo→ô** (no long *ō*, shared with Saharannaise) | *maayo→māyo*, *teraanga→terānga*, *fooñe→fôñe* |
| **Geminate consonants** | simplify: *kk→k, dd→d, bb→b, ll→l, mm→m, ññ→ñ* | *ɓiɗɗo→ɓiɗo*, *dëkk→dək*, *debbo→debo* |
| **Wolof vowels** | *é, e, è → e*; **ë → ə** (the schwa scar, shared with Saharannaise); *o, ó, ò → o* / *ô* | *géwël→gewəl*, *dëkk→dək* |
| **Mande low vowels** | *ɔ → ô*, *ɛ → e* (flatten to the archive) | *kɔgɔ→kôgô*, *bɔgɔlan→bôgôlan* |
| **Glottal / laryngeal** | Pulaar *'* (glottal) → vowel hiatus or length; *ƴ* (glottalized y) → **y** (glottalization dropped) | *na'i→nāyi* |
| **Tone** | **not written** — Soninke-Mande tone flattens, as every prosody the frame does not carry | *sanu* "gold" (tone lost) |
| **Vowel harmony** | Pulaar ATR harmony **neutralizes**; the Nelôxian front/back harmony of the spine governs every suffix | *dem-* + *-ôr* → *demôr* |

The extension is **closed**: a submission may not invent orthography outside this table without a
ruling from the Kēļs Penc. New graphemes over the mainland inventory are exactly **ɓ ɗ ŋ** plus
the shared schwa **ə** — and the prenasal digraphs, which use letters already in the archive.

## 3 · The hybrid seam — root borrowed, morpheme refused

The proof-pattern is Saharannaise's *žəmlôr* (Saharan root + mainland *-ôr*), and Congolaise runs
it hard — with one sharp discipline the three donors make necessary. **Each tributary has its own
agent morpheme**, and Congolaise borrows **none** of them. The root is taken bare; the Nelôxian
*-ôr* does all agent-formation:

| Concept | Source word | Native agent morpheme *(refused)* | Congolaise form |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| farmer | Pul. *demoowo* | *-oowo* | **demôr** (*dem-* + *-ôr*) |
| fisher | Wol. *nappkat* | *-kat* | **napôr** (*nap-* + *-ôr*) |
| townmaster | Mnd. *dugutigi* | *-tigi* | **duguôr** (*dugu-* + *-ôr*) |
| well-keeper | Mnd. *kolontigi* | *-tigi* | **kolonôr** (*kolon-* + *-ôr*) |
| gold-panner | — | — | **sanuôr** (*sanu-* + *-ôr*) |
| weaver | Wol. *ràbbkat* | *-kat* | **rabôr** (*rab-* + *-ôr*) |
| herder | Pul. *gaynaako* | *-aako* | **gaynôr** (*gayn-* + *-ôr*) |

You borrow the Niger-Congo **root**, never the Niger-Congo **grammar** — and the three rival agent
suffixes (*-tigi, -kat, -oowo, -aako*) are the sharpest thing you could accidentally borrow, so
the rule names them explicitly and refuses them. Every *-ôr* form is a small demonstration that
the spine is Nelôxian and only the well of roots is Sahelian.

---

## 4 · What this doctrine forbids

1. **No Baltic calque for a Sahelian reality.** A griot is *jeli*, not a "song-teller" compound; a
   flood-recession field is *wālo*, not a "wet-field." Name the reality; do not translate it. (The
   mainland compound ban, `../saharannaise/saharannaise.md` §1, applies here in full.)
2. **No borrowing across the wrong domain line.** Do not reach for a Wolof market word when the
   scene is a Pulaar cattle-camp, or a Pulaar pastoral word for a Mande gold-caravan. The
   domain-ownership map (§1) is the whole point.
3. **No borrowed morphology.** Roots only. The refused-suffix table (§3) is not exhaustive — the
   rule is general: if it is a Niger-Congo *ending*, it stays out; the Nelôxian ending does the job.
4. **No private spellings.** One fused phonology (§2). A root's origin shows in the root, never in
   an off-inventory grapheme.
