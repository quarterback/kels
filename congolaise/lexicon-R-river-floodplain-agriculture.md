# Congolaise Lexicon — Domain R · Batch 1
### River · Floodplain · Fields · Fishing

Archived under the Kēļs Penc (the Forum of the Language) per `congolaise.md`. All roots
Niger-Congo, assigned by the Confluence Principle (`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md`): the owning
tributary is tagged **[Pul.]** Pulaar, **[Mnd.]** Soninke-Mande, **[Wol.]** Wolof. Roots run
through the fused phonology (§2). These are **Congolaise canon only** — they do not enter mainland
canon (`../data/*`, coursebook, charter §7). Citation form given; full declension follows mainland
rules.

**23 headwords approved.** The signature domain: the state is the green riverine member, and this
is where Congolaise is richest and most its own — the *wālo/jēri* split alone is the whole valley.

## The river & its water

| Headword | PoS | Tributary · source | Gloss | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **māyo** | n. | [Pul.] *maayo* "river" | the great river, the main watercourse (the Senegal) | *aa→ā*. Pulaar owns "river" — the deep pastoral-riverine word. |
| **dex** | n. | [Wol.] *dex* "river/stream" | a stream, a branch, a lesser watercourse | Domain-tie with *māyo*: the great river is Pulaar *māyo*; a branch or stream is Wolof *dex*. Two words, two scales. |
| **wālo** | n. | [Wol./Pul.] *waalo* | flood-recession land, the fertile flooded riverbank | *aa→ā*. **Signature.** The land the flood soaks and leaves rich; farmed as the water withdraws. |
| **jēri** | n. | [Wol./Pul.] *jeeri* | rainfed dryland, the ground away from the river | *ee→ē*. **Signature.** The *wālo/jēri* split is the defining distinction of the valley — flooded-rich vs. rain-dependent. |
| **wēndu** | n. | [Pul.] *weendu* "pond/lake" | pond, seasonal lake, backwater | *ee→ē*. The standing water the flood leaves behind. |
| **waṃe** | n. | [Pul.] *waamde* "to flood" | the flood, the annual rise of the river | The event the whole floodplain economy turns on. |

## Rulings applied on archive

- **R·1 — the domain split on "settlement water" holds two ways.** *māyo* (the great river,
  Pulaar) and *dex* (a stream, Wolof) are not a doublet to be merged but the Confluence working:
  the tie-break by register (`CONFLUENCE-PRINCIPLE.md` §1) keeps both, each on its own scale.
- **R·2 — the *wālo/jēri* pair is canon as a pair.** Neither is complete without the other; they
  are the floodplain and the dryland, the whole agricultural geography of the state in two words.
  Do not gloss either as merely "land."
- **R·3 — grain doublets kept by register.** *māro* (field-grain, Pul.) ↔ *cēb* (the dish, Wol.);
  *gawri* (standing cereal, Pul.) ↔ *dugub* (market millet, Wol.). The field speaks Pulaar; the
  market speaks Wolof. Both canon.
- **R·4 — *-ôr* only.** *demôr*, *napôr* archived; the source agents *demoowo*, *nappkat* logged as
  refused morphology, root retained. The seam is the thesis (§4).
- **R·5 — back-flow candidates.** *gāl* "pirogue" and *māyo* "great river" flagged for possible
  drift into the Atlantic maritime register (Saharannaise), where riverine craft has no native
  word; promotion only by the Kēļs Dīwān's merge, never automatic.
