# Folktale — The Old Fisher and the Sea

*Corpus text, genre 3 (folktale, past tense). Metrolect standard. Written under
`college/CORPUS-BRIEF.md`: only attested headwords, past-tense narration, one character tracked
across the tale. **Settled canon** — the frontier this probe hit was ruled in charter §120
(past-tense two classes; `amplü` "no longer"; `elkü` "every"; `pelgô` + partitive; `dī→dīsi`),
and the text below is reconciled to those rulings.*

---

## The tale

> **1.** Vana kalāji elī rannãl naisôk.
> *An old fisher lived on the coast with his wife.*
>
> **2.** Elkü matī tä lǟdi merel ja kalāstī.
> *Every morning he went to the sea and fished.*
>
> **3.** Jedôn pǟvô tūļ ūli fort, ja sūr lain tulī.
> *One day the wind was strong, and a great wave came.*
>
> **4.** Kalāji pelgi, sest bōt ūli petīt.
> *The fisher was afraid, because the boat was small.*
>
> **5.** Tä sōudi kōju, agā lain ūli nobē.
> *He rowed homeward, but the wave was fast.*
>
> **6.** Mer votai bōtôn, agā kalāji vōigai rannãlô.
> *The sea took the boat, but the fisher swam to the shore.*
>
> **7.** Täs naisô ootai ja itki, kuni tä nǟgī tän.
> *His wife waited and wept, until she saw him.*
>
> **8.** Kalāji dīsi: "Mer donā, ja mer votā." Se um mer.
> *The fisher said: "The sea gives, and the sea takes." That is the sea.*
>
> **9.** Seejärõl tä äb pelgi mert amplü.
> *After that he no longer feared the sea.*

---

## What the tale exercises

- **The past-tense two classes (§120)** in one continuous text:
  - *Class I, root -a-/-ā → -ai:* **votai** (votā), **vōigai** (vōigā), **ootai** (ootā).
  - *Class I, other root vowel → -i:* **lǟdi** (lǟdô), **pelgi** (pelgô), **sōudi** (sōudô), **itki** (itkô).
  - *Class II, derived -stā → long -ī:* **kalāstī** (kalāstā).
  - *Strong basic (irregular long -ī):* **elī**, **tulī**, **nǟgī**.
  - *Monosyllabic vowel-root -si past:* **dīsi** (dī "say").
  - *Copula past:* **ūli** (×3).
- **`amplü` "no longer"** with preverbal *äb* closes the tale (sentence 9) — the move that was
  blocked before §120.
- **`elkü` "every"** carries the distributive (*elkü matī* "every morning"), replacing the old
  *alatü* "always" workaround.
- **`pelgô` + partitive** — sentence 9 names the feared thing in the partitive: *pelgi **mert***
  "feared the sea" (partitive of *mer*; note *merd* is a different word, "turd" — the partitive is
  **mert**, not *merd*).
- **One tracked subject** — the fisher — held by pro-drop, re-named only when the wife enters
  (§5 composition): sentences 2, 4, 5, 6, 8, 9 keep him without a pronoun after sentence 1 sets him.
- **Coordination + subordination**: *ja*, *agā*, causal *sest*, temporal *kuni* (until).
- **Comitative** (*naisôk*), **goal** (*rannãlô*), **place** (*merel*, *rannãl*), **possessive**
  (*täs naisô*), **object** (*bōtôn*, *tän*, *mert*).
- **Gnomic present inside a past frame** — the proverb in sentence 8 sits in the present (*donā*,
  *votā*) as a timeless truth, exactly as English does.

## Vowel-harmony note (§120)

- **mer** "sea" is **front-harmony**: place *merel*, object/genitive *meren*, partitive *mert*.
- **rannã** "shore" is **back-harmony**: place *rannãl*, goal *rannãlô*, object *rannãn*.

## Frontier still open (flagged, not blocking)

- **Reported speech beyond direct quotation** — sentence 8 uses a direct quote (*dīsi: "…"*);
  indirect discourse with sequence-of-tense is still undeveloped (composition §9). ⟨open⟩
- **"while" (simultaneous)** — *kuni* "until" carried sentence 7; a true simultaneous "while" is
  still a gap. ⟨open⟩

**Net:** the tale now runs end-to-end on settled canon — the three rulings the first probe demanded
(the `-ā`-verb past split, "no longer," "every") are folded in, and only the reported-speech and
"while" frontiers remain, both flagged for a later text.
