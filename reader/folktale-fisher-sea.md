# Folktale — The Old Fisher and the Sea

*Corpus probe, genre 3 (folktale, past tense). Metrolect standard. Written under
`college/CORPUS-BRIEF.md`: only attested headwords, past-tense narration, one character tracked
across the tale. **This is a probe, not settled canon** — the forms flagged in the gap-list below
await the Rector's ruling before the text is finalized.*

---

## The tale

> **1.** Vana kalāji elī rannãl naisôk.
> *An old fisher lived on the coast with his wife.*
>
> **2.** Alatü matī tä lǟdi merel ja kalāstī.
> *Every morning he went to the sea and fished.*
>
> **3.** Jedôn pǟvô tūļ ūli fort, ja sūr lain tulī.
> *One day the wind was strong, and a great wave came.*
>
> **4.** Kalāji pelgī, sest bōt ūli petīt.
> *The fisher was afraid, because the boat was small.*
>
> **5.** Tä sōudi kōju, agā lain ūli nobē.
> *He rowed homeward, but the wave was fast.*
>
> **6.** Mer votāi bōtôn, agā kalāji vōigāi rannãlô.
> *The sea took the boat, but the fisher swam to the shore.*
>
> **7.** Täs naisô ootāi ja itki, kuni tä nǟgī tän.
> *His wife waited and wept, until she saw him.*
>
> **8.** Kalāji dī: "Mer donā, ja mer votā." Se um mer.
> *The fisher said: "The sea gives, and the sea takes." That is the sea.*

---

## What the probe exercised (working, no gap)

- **Sustained past** across eight clauses (`elī, lǟdi, tulī, ūli, nǟgī` are module-attested pasts).
- **One tracked subject** — the fisher — held by pro-drop and re-named only when the wife enters (§5
  composition module): sentences 2, 4, 5, 6, 8 keep him without a pronoun after sentence 1 sets him.
- **Coordination + subordination**: `ja`, `agā`, causal `sest`, temporal `kuni` (until) all carry.
- **Comitative** (`naisôk` "with the wife"), **goal** (`rannãlô` "to the shore"), **possessive**
  (`täs naisô` "his wife"), **object** (`bōtôn`, `tän`).
- **Gnomic present inside a past frame** — the proverb in sentence 8 sits in the present (`donā`,
  `votā`) as a timeless truth, exactly as English does.

## Gap-list (the valuable output — for the Rector)

**Missing words**
1. **"every"** — no canon word; used *alatü* "always" as a workaround (*alatü matī* "every/always
   morning"). A dedicated distributive "every/each" may be wanted. ⟨word gap⟩
2. **"alive"** — avoided (the tale says he *reached* the shore, not that he "was alive"). If needed,
   a predicate "alive" is a gap. ⟨word gap⟩

**Missing / undecided grammar**
3. **Past of *dī* "to say"** — reported speech needs it; I left *dī* in the present-historical
   ("the fisher says/said"). The past form of the monosyllabic *dī* is undecided. ⟨grammar gap⟩ —
   this is the reported-speech frontier flagged in composition §9.
4. **Past-tense of several verbs — verify the form**: *pelgī* (fear, *pelgô*→?), *sōudi* (row,
   *sōudô*→?), *votāi* (take, *votā*→?), *vōigāi* (swim, *vōigā*→?), *ootāi* (wait, *ootā*→?),
   *itki* (weep, *itkô*→?). Each is built on the module patterns (*lǟdô→lǟdi*, *kantā→kantāi*,
   *elā→elī*) but the `-ā`-stem split (*donā→donai* vs *elā→elī*) means the Rector should confirm
   which `-ā` verbs take *-ai* and which take *-ī*. ⟨form check⟩
5. **Vowel harmony on *mer* and *rannã***: I wrote place *merel* (front link) and goal *rannãlô*
   (back link). Confirm *mer*'s harmony class (front `e`) gives *merel* / *merelä*. ⟨form check⟩
6. **Verb-government of *pelgô* "to fear"** — I used object `-n` (*meren*? — actually avoided by
   not naming the object). Does *pelgô* take the object case, the partitive, or the source? ⟨rection⟩
7. **"no longer / anymore"** — deliberately not used (the known module-09 gap); a natural sentence 9
   ("after that he no longer feared the sea") is **blocked** until it's ruled. ⟨known gap⟩

**Net:** the tale runs end-to-end on attested vocabulary and settled syntax; the frontier it hit is
exactly the predicted one — reported-speech past, the `-ā`-verb past split, and "no longer." Each is
a clean next ruling.
