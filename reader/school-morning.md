# Eskōl — A Morning at School

*Daily-life stretch IV under `college/DAILY-LIFE-BRIEF.md`: the classroom register, seen through
the child of `daily-life-workday.md` and `weekend.md`. School is where a language keeps its
**instruction verbs** (open, listen, repeat, answer, put away) and its small social order —
teacher to pupil, pupil to pupil. Metrolect; canon-only (v5.77); gaps harvested below.*

---

**1 · Klokkô.**

> Klokkô klingā, ja läpxôd istā. Talçāji dī: "Moin, läpxôd." — "Moin, talçāji!" kôg vastā, kôg
> jedôn vēu. "Avuī mēg ôpā arvôd ja rūnôd. Livrô, sulô — valmis? Gūd. Alkā mēg."

> The bell rings, and the children sit. The teacher says: "Morning, children." — "Morning,
> teacher!" they all answer, all one voice. "Today we learn numbers and letters. Book, pen —
> ready? Good. Let's begin."

**2 · Lēsô.**

> "Ken tēdô, mis um xeç ja xeç?" Jedôn läpx kôstô fluks: "Düna!" — "Ôigus! Gūd." Tôl talçāji
> kirjā rūnôn lōdôl: mä lugē sen, agā tard. "Lent, lent," dī talçāji, "kôg läpx lugē. Äb um
> kaçô — um lēsô." Se um jôvā talçāji: tä äb fīnā, tä ôpā.

> "Who knows what's six and six?" One child answers fast: "Twelve!" — "Correct! Good." Then the
> teacher writes a letter on the board: I read it, but slowly. "Slowly, slowly," says the teacher,
> "every child reads. It's not a test — it's a lesson." That's a good teacher: she doesn't
> penalize, she teaches.

**3 · Kysômus ja vastômus.**

> Amīk mä jeds äb komprē kysômusôn, ja tä muretõ. Mä dī tasõ: "Se um düna. Ainô düna." Talçāji
> nǟgô, agā tä äb fīnā — tä muigā: "Abī amīkôn? Se um gūd. Agā nū, sä isü." Ja amīk vastā, ôigus,
> ja tä um rôimõ.

> The friend next to me doesn't understand the question, and he worries. I say quietly: "It's
> twelve. Just twelve." The teacher sees, but she doesn't penalize — she smiles: "Helping your
> friend? That's good. But now, you yourself." And the friend answers, correctly, and he's happy.

**4 · Pēl-tīt.**

> Klokkô jällü, ja läpxôd kōrrô vällô — pēl-tīt! Mēg pēlā jalgpēlôn, kōrrô, itkô, naurā. Jedôn läpx
> langô ja itkô; talçāji tulē, panā abīn, ja dī: "Vabõ, vabõ. Tõusô. Kus valū?" — "Sīn." — "Gūd.
> Se pehā um jôvā. Kōrrô jällü." Ja tä kōrrô jällü.

> The bell again, and the children run out — playtime! We play football, run, shout, laugh. One
> child falls and cries; the teacher comes, gives help, and says: "Never mind, never mind. Get up.
> Where's the hurt?" — "Here." — "Fine. It's almost better. Run again." And he runs again.

**5 · Kōju-tȫ.**

> Lopõks talçāji dī: "Demāks: lugē livrôt, lehtô tri kuni peñç. Ja kirjā rūnôd, kôg rūnô düna kärt."
> Kôg läpx itkô: "Liig mult!" — "Nē, nē. Petīt-petīt. Sä vôī sen." Klokkô lopõks, ja mēg lǟdô
> kōju. Esmõ pǟvô um valmis — jedôntô pǟvô mult pǟvôlt.

> Finally the teacher says: "For tomorrow: read the book, pages three to five. And write the
> letters, each letter twelve times." Every child groans: "Too much!" — "No, no. Little by little.
> You can do it." The bell at last, and we go home. The first day is done — the first of many
> days.

## What the classroom exercises

- **The instruction register is nearly all light verbs + imperatives:** *alkā* (begin), *lugē*
  (read), *kirjā* (write), *istā* (sit), *tõusô* (get up), *abī / panā abīn* (help), *vastā*
  (answer), *kôstô* (answer back, snap out) — the hortative *alkā mēg* "let's begin" and bare-stem
  commands carry the whole scene. A lesson is a stack of imperatives softened by *bidô* and *gūd*.
  The classroom fixtures are all canon too: *eskōl* (school), *lēsô* (lesson), *sulô* (pen),
  *livrô* (book), *lehtô* (page), *lōdô* (board), *kadīr* (chair), *rūnô* (letter), *arvô* (number).
- **The moral vocabulary is already social:** the "good teacher" is defined by *äb fīnā, tä ôpā*
  (doesn't penalize, teaches) — canon *fīnā* "to fine/penalize" against *ôpā* "to teach," no
  coinage needed to draw the line.
- **Numbers live here first:** *xeç ja xeç um düna* (six and six is twelve) — the dozenal system
  learned on the page, and *düna kärt* "twelve times" for repetition.
- **Child-register warmth:** *vabõ, vabõ* (never mind), *petīt-petīt* (little by little), *sä vôī
  sen* (you can do it) — the exact consolation formulae, all attested.
- **One clean derivation on display:** *pēl-tīt* "playtime" (*pēl* play + *tīt* time) and
  *kōju-tȫ* "homework" (home + work) — both transparent compounds, module 04, no ruling needed.
