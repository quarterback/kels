# Arst — At the Doctor

*Daily-life stretch V under `college/DAILY-LIFE-BRIEF.md`: the clinic register — the body, the
symptom, the small dread and the reassurance. The narrator of `daily-life-workday.md` takes the
child in with a fever. This register keeps the **body lexicon** and the modal register of worry
(*must, should, will be fine*). Metrolect; canon-only (v5.77); gaps harvested below.*

---

**1 · Matī — the child is ill.**

> Läpx äb tõusô matīl. "Kap valū," tä dī, "ja kôht." Mä panā kǟsôn kapôl: tä um lǟmi — liig lǟmi.
> Fībôr, muidū. "Mä äb lǟdô eskōlôl avuī?" — "Nē, kohanü. Avuī sä magā. Mēg lǟdô arstôl."

> The child won't get up in the morning. "My head hurts," she says, "and my belly." I put my hand
> on her forehead: she's warm — too warm. A fever, of course. "I'm not going to school today?" —
> "No, sweetie. Today you rest. We're going to the doctor."

**2 · Ootkôrd.**

> Arstôl um ootkôrd, nagu alatü. Mēg istā, ja läpx magā mä kǟsôl. Infermô dī nimôn: "Läpx sīn?" —
> "Sīn." — "Tulē." Kambôrôl arst muigā läpxôl: "Moin, jūnôr. Kus valū?" Läpx äb dī midagü —
> tä pelgô. "Vabõ," dī arst. "Mä ainô nǟgô. Se äb valū."

> At the doctor's there's a queue, as always. We sit, and the child sleeps in my arms. The nurse
> says the name: "Child here?" — "Here." — "Come." In the room the doctor smiles at the child:
> "Morning, young one. Where's the hurt?" The child says nothing — she's scared. "Never mind,"
> says the doctor. "I'll just look. It won't hurt."

**3 · Kaçô.**

> Arst kaçā läpxôn: tä nǟgô kurkôn, kuulõ rindôn, panā kǟsôn kôhtôl. "Sōv, dī 'aaa'." Läpx dī
> "aaa," ja arst muigā. "Gūd. Um ainô fībôr — petīt tôbô, äb mult. Tri pǟvô lit, mult veç, ja
> se lǟdô." Mä kysô: "Äb um miskü sūr?" — "Nē, nē. Se um läpxôd; se tulē ja lǟdô. Sä äb pidā
> muretõ."

> The doctor examines the child: looks at the throat, listens to the chest, puts a hand on the
> belly. "Please, say 'ahh'." The child says "ahh," and the doctor smiles. "Good. It's only a
> fever — a small illness, not much. Three days in bed, lots of water, and it'll pass." I ask:
> "It's nothing serious?" — "No, no. It's children; it comes and goes. You mustn't worry."

**4 · Rohtô.**

> Arst kirjā rohtô-papôrôn. "Ün rohtô, dva kärt pǟvôl — matī ja vespôr. Ja veç, mult veç. Kui läpx
> äb um jôvā pǟrast tri pǟvô, tulē jällü." — "Dankô, arst." — "Vabõ. Lǟdô, ja saistā jôvā, jūnôr!"
> Läpx nū muigā: arst um äb hirmus pǟrast kôg.

> The doctor writes a prescription. "One medicine, twice a day — morning and evening. And water,
> lots of water. If the child isn't well after three days, come again." — "Thank you, doctor." —
> "Never mind. Go, and stay well, young one!" The child smiles now: the doctor wasn't scary after
> all.

**5 · Kōju.**

> Kōtôl mä panā läpxôn litelô, donā rohtôn ja veçôn. "Kap veel valū?" — "Petīt. Nū ainô petīt." — "Gūd.
> Magā nū." Tä magā fluks. Naisô tulē tȫlt, ja mä dī: "Kôg um jôvā. Ainô fībôr. Tri pǟvô, ja tä
> um sān jällü." Mēg istā läpxôk, ja kōt um vaikõ.

> At home I put the child to bed, give the medicine and the water. "Head still hurting?" — "A bit.
> Only a bit now." — "Good. Sleep now." She sleeps fast. The wife comes home from work, and I say:
> "Everything's fine. Just a fever. Three days, and she'll be healthy again." We sit by the child,
> and the house is quiet.

## What the clinic exercises

- **The body lexicon is all canon and all short:** *kap* (head), *kôht* (belly), *kurk* (throat),
  *rind* (chest), *kǟs* (hand), *verī* (blood), *kor* (heart) — the Livonian body-core, no loans
  needed. The symptom words too: *valū* (pain), *fībôr* (fever), *tôbô* (illness), *köhä* (cough),
  *malālt* (ill), *sān* (healthy).
- **The register of worry is modal:** *sä äb pidā muretõ* (you mustn't worry), *se tulē ja lǟdô*
  (it comes and goes), *se lǟdô* (it'll pass), *äb um miskü sūr?* (nothing serious?) — reassurance
  is built from the deontic *pidā* and the plain motion verbs, not a special medical register.
- **The symptom question is fixed:** *Kus valū?* "where's the hurt?" — the same three words the
  teacher used for the fallen child (`school-morning.md` §4). One clinic formula, reused across
  the corpus: pain is located, not described.
- **Compassion formulae, all attested:** *vabõ* (never mind), *saistā jôvā* (stay well), *kohanü /
  jūnôr* (sweetie / young one) — the doctor's bedside voice is the same warmth kit as the market
  and the classroom.
- **One clean compound:** *rohtô-papôr* "prescription" (medicine + paper) — module 04, transparent,
  no ruling.
