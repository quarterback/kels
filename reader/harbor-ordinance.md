# Harbor Ordinance — *Satām-Reglament*

*A civic-legal text: the by-laws of a Nelôxian port, issued by the Mayor and Council. Translating a
document like this is how the **institutional register** grows — it pulls the *verbs of governance*
(decree, register, levy, declare, confiscate) that narrative never touches. Composed under
`bundles/BUNDLE-translation-agent.md`. Legal-institutional vocabulary is borrowed by quarry (§79/§130):
Romance/Catalan for the national legal layer, Slavic for the frontier's toll-and-trade offices.
Metrolect standard, formal register. §132.*

---

## The ordinance

> **Batle ja Nôuk dekretā, ja se reglament um lēgô satāmôs:**
> *The Mayor and Council decree, and this ordinance is law in the harbor:*
>
> **§1.** Elkü xip, ken tulē satāmô, pidā registrā ja maksā tōlôn.
> *Every ship that enters the harbor must register and pay the toll.*
>
> **§2.** Kapitān pidā deklarā kôg krāmôn mytnikôle, ja donā manifestôn.
> *The captain must declare all goods to the toll-officer, and give a manifest.*
>
> **§3.** Tōl um imposādôt elkü lastô eest. Mytnik kōrjā sen.
> *A toll is levied for each cargo-load. The toll-officer collects it.*
>
> **§4.** Xip pidā sidā ainô täs kājôl. Äb jedôn xip pidā um väinäs.
> *A ship must moor only at its own quay. No ship may be in the channel.*
>
> **§5.** Xip, ken tulē pestô-satāmô-lt, pidā um karantēnôs, merel, tri setmān.
> *A ship that comes from a plague-harbor must be in quarantine, at sea, three weeks.*
>
> **§6.** Fôk um keldādôt xipôl kājôl. Mustveç um keldādôt satāmôs.
> *Fire is forbidden on a ship at the quay. Dumping waste is forbidden in the harbor.*
>
> **§7.** Ken rikā se reglamentôn, pidā um fīnādôt. Jôs tä rikā jällü, mytnik vôī konfiskā täs krāmôn.
> *Whoever breaks this ordinance shall be fined. If they break it again, the toll-officer may
> confiscate their goods.*
>
> **§8.** Satām-kapitān ja valvāji valvā se reglamentôn. Rīdômus lǟdô rēdāmô.
> *The harbor-master and the watch enforce this ordinance. Disputes go to the court.*
>
> **Donādôt Nôukô sīgelô al, düntri pǟvô jūni kūs.**
> *Given under the seal of the Council, the thirteenth day of the month of June.*

## What the document pulled into canon (§132) — the verbs of governance

Legal-institutional, so **Romance/Catalan** by doctrine (§79) — with endings spread, not a wall of `-ô`:
- **dekret** decree / **dekretā** to decree · **reglament** ordinance, by-law · **registrā** to register
  / **registrô** a register, roll · **deklarā** to declare · **manifest** cargo manifest ·
  **imposā** to levy · **karantēn** quarantine · **fīnā** to fine (from *fīnô*) · **konfiskā** to
  confiscate, seize.
- *(Zero-final nouns: dekret, reglament, manifest, karantēn. Verbs on -ā, as verbs must. One -ô
  noun, registrô. The borrowed layer stays layered.)*

## What was already there (the register was half-built)

The offices came from §130–§131 (**batle** mayor, **nôuk** council, **mytnik** toll-officer,
**valvāji** watch); the port from the maritime core (**satām** harbor, **kājô** quay, **väinä**
channel, **last** cargo, **xip** ship, **tōl** toll, **sīgel** seal); the law from before (**lēgô**
law, **lubā** permit, **keldā** forbid, **kōrjā** collect, **rikā** break/violate, **rēdām** court,
**fīnô** fine). **satām-kapitān** "harbor-master" is a transparent compound — no coin needed.

## Grammar the register leans on (all settled canon)

- **Passive participle -dôt** carries the impersonal legal voice: *imposādôt* "is levied,"
  *keldādôt* "is forbidden," *fīnādôt* "shall be fined," *donādôt* "given (under seal)."
- **Modals** *pidā* "must/shall" (obligation) and *vôī* "may" (permission) do the deontic work.
- **Conditional** *jôs* "if" for the repeat-offence clause; **ken** "whoever" heads the penalty rule.
- **Dozenal date** *düntri pǟvô* "the thirteenth (12+1) day," the native base-12 counting.
