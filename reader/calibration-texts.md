# Nelôxi — Calibration Texts

*Corpus probes, the standard cross-linguistic calibration passages. Metrolect standard, written
under `college/CORPUS-BRIEF.md`: **only attested headwords**, forms reconciled to charter §120
(past-tense two classes; negation `äb` + tense-marked verb; negated object → partitive). Where the
language has no word, the text **flags the gap and paraphrases** — it never invents (composition
module §9). Each gap below is a candidate ruling, not a licence to coin.*

---

## 1 · The North Wind and the Sun (Aesop)

The classic phonetic/typological calibration fable. It stress-tests **comparison** (who is
*stronger*), **manner**, and a **causative** ("make him take it off").

> **1.** Pôjātūļ ja päikü diskutai, ken ūli fortmi.
> *The North Wind and the Sun argued over which of them was the stronger.*
>
> **2.** Tôl reisāji tulī, lǟmi mäntlôk.
> *Then a traveler came along, with a warm cloak.*
>
> **3.** Ne otsi: fortmi um se, ken laskā reisājin votā mäntlôn maha.
> *They agreed: the stronger is the one who makes the traveler take off his cloak.*
>
> **4.** Pôjātūļ tūļāi fort, agā reisāji äb votai mäntlôn maha.
> *The North Wind blew hard, but the traveler did not take the cloak off.*
>
> **5.** Tôl pôjātūļ kapitulai.
> *Then the North Wind gave up.*
>
> **6.** Päikü nīrai lǟmi, ja reisāji votai mäntlôn maha.
> *The Sun shone warm, and the traveler took the cloak off.*
>
> **7.** Niimôdõ päikü ūli fortmi kui pôjātūļ.
> *So the Sun was the stronger of the two.*

**What worked (settled canon):**
- **Comparison** carries the whole fable: **fortmi** "stronger" (fort + comparative *-mi*, module 10),
  and the **kui** "than" frame in sentence 7 (*fortmi kui pôjātūļ*).
- **Past two-class (§120):** *diskutai, votai, nīrai, kapitulai, tūļāi* (Class I root-*-ā* → *-ai*);
  *otsi* (Class I *ô* → *-i*); *tulī* (strong); copula *ūli*.
- **"to blow" = tūļā** (§122) — denominal from *tūļ* "wind": *pôjātūļ tūļāi fort* "the North Wind
  blew hard." Built from their own wind-word, not imported.
- **Past negation:** *äb votai* "did not take off" (§: *äb* + the past-marked verb), with the
  particle-of-manner **maha** "off/down."
- **Compound** *pôjātūļ* "north wind" (*pôjā* + *tūļ*, head-final, module 04).
- **Analytic causative** *laskā X votā …* "make/have X take …" — *laskā* "let, make"
  (the *lassen*-type causative auxiliary) + object + bare verb.

**Gap-list (candidate rulings):**
1. **"to blow" (of wind)** — closed (§122): **tūļā**, denominal from *tūļ* "wind." ✓ closed
2. **Causative "make someone do X"** — the analytic *laskā* + bare verb carries it (§121); the
   synthetic *-tā* causative (module 02) is for de-intransitive pairs (*langô→langôtā*), not for
   "make someone perform a transitive." Both now codified. ✓ closed
3. **"warmly" (manner adverb)** — used the bare adjective *lǟmi* on an eventive reading (module
   10); a dedicated manner form is not needed, but the eventive reading of *lǟmi* is worth
   confirming. ⟨form check⟩

## 2 · Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 1

The standard abstract-vocabulary calibration. It probes **ethical abstractions** — and after §122
these render on Nelôxian **native metaphor**, not Latin relex. Watch how the imagery lands: dignity
is the *weight of a name*, conscience the *black-book you keep on yourself*, equality *one weight on
the scale*, brotherhood *one net pulled together*.

> **1.** Kôg inim syndā līberü ja ühüvāgô nimpundôs ja rētôs.
> *All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights.*
> *(lit. "…free and of-one-weight in name-weight and rights")*
>
> **2.** Ne um pôhjôk ja isülivrôk, ja ne pidā elä teinütõk ühüvôrks.
> *They are endowed with reason and conscience, and should live toward one another in brotherhood.*
> *(lit. "…have reason and inner-ledger, and should live with one another in one-net")*

**What worked — now with feel (§122):**
- **nimpundô** "dignity" (name + weight), **isülivrô** "conscience" (self + book, the inner
  black-book), **ühüvāgô** "equal" (of one weight on the scale), **ühüvôrk** "brotherhood" (one net)
  — every abstraction carries a *picture* from the material culture, not a shaved-down Latin root.
- **kôg inim** "all people," **syndā** "are born" (gnomic present), **līberü** "free," **rētô**
  "right" in the inessive *rētôs*; **pôhjôk / isülivrôk** "with reason and conscience" (comitative
  "endowed with"); **pidā** "should"; reciprocal *teinütõk* "with one another."
- The old *nagu veļ* "like brothers" workaround is gone — *ühüvôrks* "in one-net" says
  "in a spirit of brotherhood" *as an image*, which is exactly the texture that was missing.

**Gap-list — what's left (much smaller):**
1. **"reason" (the faculty)** — still uses **pôhjô** "reason = cause"; the *faculty* of reason is a
   different sense, and belongs to a metaphor field (the *head*? the *steady eye*?) yet to be
   chosen. ⟨semantic gap — coin by §122, not by loan⟩
2. **"to act / behave"** — paraphrased *elä* "live" (*elä teinütõk* "live toward one another"); a
   dedicated verb can verbalize existing *kondutô* "conduct," or take a native "carry one's name"
   idiom. ⟨open⟩

## What these two texts tell us

- **Structure is solid.** Comparison, negation-in-the-past, reciprocals, the comitative "have,"
  the deontic *pidā*, gnomic present, compounding, and the analytic causative all carry real
  cross-linguistic passages with no invention.
- **The abstract register now has feel (§122).** The UDHR cluster — dignity, conscience, equal,
  brotherhood — was reworked from Latin relex into **native metaphor** (name-weight, inner-ledger,
  one-weight-on-the-scale, one-net). "to blow" likewise comes from *tūļ* "wind," not German. The
  test each passed: *would a fisher-trader on this coast reach for this image?*
- **What's left is smaller and disciplined.** A faculty-"reason" and a "behave" verb remain — to be
  coined *by metaphor field* (`college/METAPHOR-FIELDS.md`), never by bulk relex.
