# Nelôxi — Calibration Texts

*Corpus probes, the standard cross-linguistic calibration passages. Metrolect standard, written
under `college/CORPUS-BRIEF.md`: **only attested headwords**, forms reconciled to charter §120
(past-tense two classes; negation `äb` + tense-marked verb; negated object → partitive). Where the
language has no word, the text **flags the gap and paraphrases** — it never invents (composition
module §9). Each gap below is a candidate ruling, not a licence to coin.*

## 1 · The North Wind and the Sun (Aesop)

The classic phonetic/typological calibration fable. It stress-tests **comparison** (who is
*stronger*), **manner**, and a **causative** ("make him take it off").

> **1.** Pôjātūļ ja päikü diskutai, ken ūli fortmi.
> *The North Wind and the Sun argued over which of them was the stronger.*
>
> **2.** Tôl reisāji tulī, lǟmi mäntlôk.
> *Then a traveler came along, with a warm cloak.*
>
> **3.** Ne otsi: fortmi um se, ken tekī, et reisāji votā mäntlôn maha.
> *They agreed: the stronger is the one who makes the traveler take off his cloak.*
>
> **4.** Pôjātūļ ūli fort, agā reisāji äb votai mäntlôn maha.
> *The North Wind blew hard, but the traveler did not take the cloak off.*
>
> **5.** Tôl pôjātūļ kapitulai.
> *Then the North Wind gave up.*
>
> **6.** Päikü nīrai lǟmi, ja reisāji votai mäntlôn maha.
> *The Sun shone warm, and the traveler took the cloak off.*
>
> **7.** Niimôdõ päikü ūli fortmi kui pôjātūļ.
> *So the Sun was the stronger of the two.*

**What worked (settled canon):**
- **Comparison** carries the whole fable: **fortmi** "stronger" (fort + comparative *-mi*, module 10),
  and the **kui** "than" frame in sentence 7 (*fortmi kui pôjātūļ*).
- **Past two-class (§120):** *diskutai, votai, nīrai, kapitulai* (Class I root-*-ā* → *-ai*);
  *otsi* (Class I *ô* → *-i*); *tulī* (strong); copula *ūli*.
- **Past negation:** *äb votai* "did not take off" (§: *äb* + the past-marked verb), with the
  particle-of-manner **maha** "off/down."
- **Compound** *pôjātūļ* "north wind" (*pôjā* + *tūļ*, head-final, module 04).
- **Analytic causative** *tekī, et …* "made it (so) that …" — *tekô* "make/do" + an *et*-clause,
  standing in for a morphological causative.

**Gap-list (candidate rulings):**
1. **"to blow" (of wind)** — no verb. Rendered "the North Wind was strong" (*ūli fort*) as a
   workaround. This is the headline gap of the fable. ⟨word gap⟩
2. **Morphological causative "make someone do X"** — worked around with *tekô, et …* (analytic).
   Whether Nelôxi should have a synthetic causative here (cf. the derivation engine, module 02)
   is undecided. ⟨grammar gap⟩
3. **"warmly" (manner adverb)** — used the bare adjective *lǟmi* on an eventive reading (module
   10); a dedicated manner form is not needed, but the eventive reading of *lǟmi* is worth
   confirming. ⟨form check⟩

## 2 · Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 1

The standard abstract-vocabulary calibration. It deliberately probes **legal/ethical abstractions**
— exactly the register Nelôxi sources from Romance (§79), and exactly where its gaps show.

> **1.** Kôg inim syndā līberü ja samā rētôs.
> *All human beings are born free and equal in [dignity and] rights.*
>
> **2.** Ne um pôhjôk, ja ne pidā elä teinütõk nagu veļ.
> *They are endowed with reason [and conscience], and should act toward one another like brothers.*

**What worked (settled canon):**
- **kôg inim** "all people," **syndā** "are born" (gnomic present — a timeless truth, as in the
  folktale's proverb), **līberü** "free," **rētô** "right, entitlement" in the inessive *rētôs*
  "in rights."
- **Reciprocal** *teinütõ* "one another" in the comitative *teinütõk* "with one another";
  **nagu** "like, as" + *veļ* "brother" paraphrases "in a spirit of brotherhood."
- **pidā** "should" for the deontic; **um … -k** (comitative) for "have / be endowed with"
  (*pôhjôk* "with reason").

**Gap-list (candidate rulings) — the abstract register:**
1. **"dignity"** — no word; omitted (bracketed in the gloss). A core ethical abstraction; Romance
   by doctrine (cf. *libertā*, *rētô*). ⟨word gap⟩
2. **"conscience"** — no word; omitted. Romance by doctrine. ⟨word gap⟩
3. **"equal" (adjective)** — approximated with **samā** "the same." *samā rētôs* reads "the same in
   rights," not precisely "equal." A dedicated "equal" is a gap. ⟨word gap / semantic⟩
4. **"reason" (the faculty)** — used **pôhjô**, which is "reason = cause"; the faculty of reason is
   a different sense. ⟨semantic gap⟩
5. **"to act / behave / conduct oneself"** — no verb; paraphrased with **elä** "to live"
   (*elä … nagu veļ* "live … like brothers"). ⟨word gap⟩
6. **"brotherhood / in a spirit of brotherhood"** — no abstract noun; paraphrased *nagu veļ* "like
   brothers." A derived abstract (brother → brotherhood) is a gap. ⟨word gap⟩

## What these two texts tell us

- **Structure is solid.** Comparison, negation-in-the-past, reciprocals, the comitative "have,"
  the deontic *pidā*, gnomic present, compounding, and the analytic causative all carry real
  cross-linguistic passages with no invention.
- **The lexical frontier is the *abstract* register.** The North Wind gap is a single concrete
  verb ("blow"); the UDHR gaps are a cluster of legal-ethical abstractions (dignity, conscience,
  equal, brotherhood) — precisely the Romance-sourced layer (§79). That cluster is the natural
  next lexicon batch, if the founder wants to open it.
- **No canon was bent.** Every rendered word is attested; every missing one is flagged, not coined.
