# The Angler and the Hearth-Mate — *Kalāji ja Ühüfôk*

*A translation of the Low German folktale (Grimm, "Von dem Fischer un syner Fru") — a homecoming,
since the tale was first told in Low German, one of Nelôxi's own quarries. Translating a long text is
the fastest way to surface what the language needs: the **complete tale** pulled thirteen words, two
idioms, and a structural rule into canon (§127–§129), the founder carving every culturally-loaded
image. Metrolect standard. Proper names (Ilsô) are register-exempt (§63).*

> **On the title.** English calls this "The Fisherman and *His* Wife." Nelôxi cannot — kin are not
> possessions, and the language does not gender them. So: **Kalāji ja Ühüfôk**, "The Angler and the
> Hearth-Mate" — two people named and bonded by *ja* "and," not one owning the other. See the
> kinship note below.

---

## Movement I — the flounder and the first wish

> **1.** Jedôn kôrd kalāji ja ühüfôk elī hīrlôukkōtôs merel. Elkü pǟvô tä lǟdi kalāstā.
> *Once an angler and a hearth-mate lived in a mousetrap-hovel by the sea. Every day he went to fish.*
>
> **2.** Jedôn pǟvô täs līnô lǟdi alūlô, ja tä tõmbāi sūr pakalān.
> *One day his line went to the bottom, and he pulled up a great loaf-fish.*
>
> **3.** Pakalā dīsi: "Laskā mä elä! Mä äb um pakalā — mä um taikô kuldapoig."
> *The flounder said: "Let me live! I am no loaf-fish — I am an enchanted prince (gold-boy)."*
>
> **4.** "Kalā, ken parlā, mä laskā lǟdô," dīsi kalāji, ja panā tän tagān selgô veçô.
> *"A fish that speaks, I let go," said the angler, and put him back into the clear water.*
>
> **5.** Tä lǟdi kōju hīrlôukkōtôlô. Ühüfôk kysi: "Ka sä äb kaptāi midagü avuī?"
> *He went home to the hovel. The hearth-mate asked: "Did you catch nothing today?"*
>
> **6.** Tä dīsi täle pakalā pǟl. "Ka sä äb sōvāi midagü?" ühüfôk dīsi.
> *He told them about the flounder. "Did you wish for nothing?" said the hearth-mate.*
>
> **7.** "Um kôv elä sīn hīrlôukkōtôs," ühüfôk dīsi. "Lǟdô, sōvā kivīkōtôn!"
> *"It is hard to live here in the mousetrap-hovel," said the hearth-mate. "Go, wish for a stone-house!"*
>
> **8.** Tä lǟdi merelä. Mer ūli rôhī ja kôlt. Tä dīsi merel:
> *He went to the sea. The sea was green and yellow. He called out over the sea:*
>
> > **Pakalā, pakalā, merel sīn,**
> > **tulē mäle, kūlô mä!**
> > **Ühüfôk Ilsô äb um vaik,**
> > **tä sōvā mult, äb kui mä.**
> >
> > *Loaf-fish, loaf-fish, here in the sea, / come to me, hear me! / Hearth-mate Ilsô is not at
> > peace, / she wishes much, not as I.*
>
> **9.** Pakalā tulī ja dīsi: "Lǟdô kōju — täl um kivīkōt." Ja ühüfôk istāi kivīkōtôs.
> *The flounder came and said: "Go home — she has a stone-house." And the hearth-mate was sitting
> in a stone-house.*

## Movement II — the silver manor

> **10.** Tôl ühüfôk äb ūli rahu. "Se kivīkōt um liig petīt," tä dīsi. "Lǟdô, sōvā ôbēkōtôn!"
> *Then the hearth-mate was not at peace. "This stone-house is too small," she said. "Go, wish for a silver manor!"*
>
> **11.** Kalāji lǟdi merelä. Mer **äb ūli amplü rôhī** — tä ūli purpôr ja sinī-karī.
> *The angler went to the sea. The sea was **green no longer** — it was purple and dark-blue.*
>
> **12.** Tä dīsi merel:
> > *Pakalā, pakalā, merel sīn, / tulē mäle, kūlô mä! / Ühüfôk Ilsô äb um vaik, / tä sōvā mult, äb kui mä.*
>
> **13.** Pakalā dīsi: "Lǟdô kōju — täl um ôbēkōt." Ja ühüfôk ūli jeds sūr ôbēkōt, kôgõl kivī ja ôbē.
> *The flounder said: "Go home — she has a silver manor." And the hearth-mate stood before a great silver manor, all stone and silver.*

## Movement III — king

> **14.** Agā ühüfôk volē veel mult. "Mä volē um kuning!" tä dīsi. Se ūli **vajômus**.
> *But the hearth-mate wanted still more. "I want to be king!" she said. That was **greed** — the count forever one short.*
>
> **15.** Kalāji lǟdi merelä. Mer ūli karī-grīs. *(the summons)*
> *The angler went to the sea. The sea was dark-grey.*
>
> **16.** Pakalā dīsi: "Lǟdô kōju — tä um kuning." Ja ühüfôk ūli kuning.
> *The flounder said: "Go home — she is king." And the hearth-mate was king.*

## Movement IV — emperor

> **17.** Agā ühüfôk veel äb ūli rahu. "Mä volē um ülehelmô!" tä dīsi.
> *But still the hearth-mate was not at peace. "I want to be emperor — he that wears the helmet over us all!"*
>
> **18.** Kalāji lǟdi merelä. Mer ūli must ja paks. *(the summons)*
> *The angler went to the sea. The sea was black and thick.*
>
> **19.** Pakalā dīsi: "Lǟdô kōju — tä um ülehelmô." Ja tä ūli ülehelmô, üle kôg kuning.
> *The flounder said: "Go home — she is emperor." And she was emperor, over all kings.*

## Movement V — pope

> **20.** Agā vajômus äb ūli tǟs. "Mä volē um pāpô!" tä dīsi.
> *But the greed was not full. "I want to be pope!" she said.*
>
> **21.** Kalāji lǟdi merelä. Mer ūli must, ja tūļā ūli fort. *(the summons)*
> *The angler went to the sea. The sea was black, and the wind blew hard.*
>
> **22.** Pakalā dīsi: "Lǟdô kōju — tä um pāpô." Ja ühüfôk ūli pāpô, jeds sūr kirīk.
> *The flounder said: "Go home — she is pope." And the hearth-mate was pope, before a great church.*

## Movement VI — to be as God, and the fall

> **23.** Agā ühüfôk veel volē mult. "Mä volē um kui deusô. Mä volē **perēm kun mä magā** — mä volē kuning üle päikü ja kū!"
> *But the hearth-mate wanted still more. "I want to be like God. I want **my prize as I sleep** — I want to rule the sun and the moon!"*
>
> **24.** Nū mer ūli must ja keetā pôhôlt, ja tūļā ūli fort — tormô.
> *Now the sea was black and boiling from the bottom, and the wind blew hard — a storm.*
>
> **25.** Tä dīsi merel, lopõks:
> > *Pakalā, pakalā, merel sīn, / tulē mäle, kūlô mä! / Ühüfôk Ilsô äb um vaik, / tä sōvā mult, äb kui mä.*
>
> **26.** Pakalā dīsi: "Lǟdô kōju — tä istā jällü hīrlôukkōtôs." Ja sāl ne elā avuī veel.
> *The flounder said: "Go home — she sits again in the mousetrap-hovel." And there they live to this day.*

## Kinship is not possession, and not gendered (§127)

The founder's structural ruling, the biggest thing this text surfaced. **Nelôxi does not say "his
wife," "her husband," "their children."** Kin are not owned, and *tä* is already genderless. You
**name people and bond them to each other** — by *ja* "and," by apposition, by the comitative *-k* —
never by a possessive pronoun, never by a gendered kin-word.

- **ühüfôk** "spouse, life-partner" — *ühü* "shared" + *fôk* "hearth": the one you share a hearth
  with. **Non-gendered**, and it extends the bond-family: *ühüvôrk* (shared-net = brotherhood),
  *ühüvāgô* (shared-scale = equal), **ühüfôk** (shared-hearth = spouse).
- **läpx** "child" is already genderless — no coin needed.

The model sentence, the founder's own:

> "Sergei, his wife Misha, and their children Mary and Miiko"
> → **Sergei ja ühüfôk Misha, ja läpxôd Mary ja Miiko.**
> *"Sergei and hearth-mate Misha, and the children Mary and Miiko."*

No *täs* "his," no *nes* "their," no "wife": everyone is named and bonded. (The gendered *naisô*
"wife" / *marīt* "husband" survive only as plain descriptors when a speaker genuinely needs to state
sex — never as the default, and never possessed. Owning a *thing* is fine: *täs līnô* "his line,"
*täl um kivīkōt* "she has a stone-house" — you may possess a line or a house, just not a person.)

## The rest of what the text pulled into canon (§127) — your images, not my -ô defaults

- **pakalā** "flounder" — *pa* "loaf" + *kalā* "fish": **the loaf-fish**, flat as a loaf of bread.
- **hīrlôukkōt** "hovel" — *hīr* "mouse" + *lôuk* "trap" + *kōt* "house": **a house so poor it is
  filled with mousetraps.** (New sub-words: **hīr** "mouse," **lôuk** "trap, the little snapping jaws.")
- **kivīkōt** "cottage" — *kivī* "stone" + *kōt* "house": **a stone-house**, the wished-for upgrade.
- **kuldxapkô** "crown" — *kuld* "gold" + *xapkô* "cap": **the gold cap** on a royal head. And
  **kuldapoig** "prince" — *kuld* "gold" + *poig* "boy": **the gold-boy**, the short word for the
  full image *taikô poig kuldxapkôk* "a boy child wearing a crown." No *kuning*-compound needed.

**Movements II–VI (§129) — the escalation, all founder-carved:**
- **ôbēkōt** "palace" — *ôbē* "silver" + *kōt* "house": **the silver manor**, the rung above the
  stone-house (the dwelling ladder: mousetrap-hovel → stone-house → silver manor).
- **vajômus** "greed" — from *vajā* "to lack, come up short": **the shortfall — the angler whose
  count is always missing one, forever a fish short.** (New verb **vajā** "to lack, be wanting.")
- **ülehelmô** "emperor" — *üle* "over" + **helmô** "helmet": **he that wears the helmet over us all.**
- **pāpô** "pope" — a plain loan (Romance/Latin *papa*): the church genuinely arrived from outside,
  so it keeps its outside name (§79), exactly the case where borrowing is right.
- Two idioms registered (`reader/idioms.md`): **greed** *"täs arvô alatü jedôn vajā"* (his count is
  always one short), and the last, hubristic wish **"perēm kun sä magā"** (your prize as you sleep).

## The refrain — a registered formula

The summons-quatrain is a **fixed formula** (`reader/idioms.md`), spoken unchanged each return, the
sea darkening a shade per wish.

## The whole tale, complete — the ladders it built

- **Dwelling:** mousetrap-hovel (*hīrlôukkōt*) → stone-house (*kivīkōt*) → silver manor (*ôbēkōt*) →
  … → back to the hovel. The arc *is* the moral.
- **Office:** — → king (*kuning*) → emperor (*ülehelmô*) → pope (*pāpô*) → to be as God (*deusô*).
- **The sea, darkening a shade per wish:** green·yellow → purple·dark-blue → dark-grey → black·thick
  → black-and-boiling storm (*tūļā ūli fort — tormô*). The new "to blow" got its storm.
- **The engine:** *vajômus* "greed," the count forever one short.
