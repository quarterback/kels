# Folktale — The Merchant and the Fisher's Daughter

*Corpus text, genre 3+ (folktale with **dialogue and simultaneous action**). Metrolect standard,
settled canon. Written under `college/CORPUS-BRIEF.md` on attested headwords only, and built to
**exercise every construction codified in charter §121** — the long-text toolkit: "while," reported
speech (with no backshift), embedded questions, the analytic causative, and the cleft. This is the
demonstration text for the composition frontier.*

---

## The tale

> **1.** Jedôn matī handlôji tulī satāmôl.
> *One morning a merchant came to the harbor.*
>
> **2.** Samā tītôl kui kalāji parandai vôrkôn, täs tytār vendai kalān.
> *While the fisher mended his net, his daughter sold the fish.*
>
> **3.** Handlôji kysi, kui mult kalā maksai.
> *The merchant asked how much the fish cost.*
>
> **4.** Se ūli tytār, ken vastai, agā äb pǟr.
> *It was the daughter who answered, not the father.*
>
> **5.** Tä dīsi, et kalā um värskē ja prēu um ôigus.
> *She said that the fish was fresh and the price was fair.*
>
> **6.** "Donā tri drobôt," tä dīsi.
> *"Give three coins," she said.*
>
> **7.** Handlôji laskai poigôn bringā kalān kōju.
> *The merchant had the boy carry the fish home.*
>
> **8.** Samā tītôl kui päikü lōjū, ne lǟdi kōju felīç.
> *While the sun set, they went home happy.*

---

## The §121 toolkit, one construction at a time

Each sentence is built to show a frame the calibration texts had left open:

- **Sentence 2, 8 — "while" (simultaneous):** the **samā tītôl kui** frame ("at the same time as")
  — *samā* "same" + *tīt* "time" in the adessive *tītôl* + *kui* "as." Two clauses run at once, with
  no dedicated conjunction and no new word. (Contrast *kun* "when," which sequences rather than
  overlaps.)
- **Sentence 3 — embedded content question:** *kysi, **kui mult** kalā maksai* "asked **how much**
  the fish cost" — the bare wh-phrase *kui mult* heads the embedded clause, no complementizer.
- **Sentence 4 — cleft / focus:** ***Se ūli** tytār, **ken** vastai* "**It was** the daughter
  **who** answered" — copula + *ken* relative, contrastive against *agā äb pǟr* "not the father."
- **Sentence 5 — reported speech, no backshift:** *dīsi, et kalā **um** värskē* "said that the fish
  **was** fresh" — the embedded verb stays **present** (*um*) under a past matrix (*dīsi*), because
  Nelôxi has no sequence-of-tense. This is *simpler* than English, not harder.
- **Sentence 6 — direct quotation:** the quoted imperative *"Donā tri drobôt"* (bare-stem command +
  number-driven partitive *drobôt* "coins") sits beside the indirect frame of sentence 5, so the
  two reporting strategies stand side by side.
- **Sentence 7 — analytic causative:** *laskai poigôn bringā kalān* "**had** the boy **carry** the
  fish" — *laskā* (the *lassen*-type auxiliary) + object *poigôn* + bare verb *bringā*. Used because
  *bringā* is already transitive, so the synthetic *-tā* causative (module 02) can't apply.

## Past-tense forms (all §120)

- Class I, root *-ā* → **-ai**: *parandai* (mend), *vendai* (sell), *vastai* (answer), *maksai*
  (cost/pay), *laskai* (make/have).
- Class I, *ô* → **-i**: *kysi* (ask), *lǟdi* (go).
- Monosyllabic vowel-root → **-si**: *dīsi* (say).
- Strong: *tulī* (come). Background *-ū* stem stays present here: *lōjū* "sets" (§121 addendum:
  *-ū* pasts → *-i*, *lōjū→lōji*, when past is wanted).

## What this proves

The tale runs **eight clauses with two speakers, a quotation, an embedded question, a focus
construction, and two simultaneous-action frames** — and reaches for **not one invented word or
form**. The long-text toolkit (§121) is complete enough that another agent can now write connected
narrative prose with dialogue. The remaining frontier is lexical (a generic "make/do"; the
abstract-ethical cluster) — flagged in composition §10, not structural.
