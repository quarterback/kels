# Tȫpǟvô — A Working Day

*A daily-life prose stretch under `college/DAILY-LIFE-BRIEF.md`: one ordinary person's day as
connected metrolect speech — waking, the commute, the office, lunch, a work snag, the match at the
bar, home. Written prose-first from attested canon (v5.77); every place the prose needed a word the
language lacks is **harvested in the gap-list below**, not silently filled. The point of the text
is the glue: watch the connectives, hedges, and light verbs do the work that turns vocabulary into
a person talking.*

## The text

**1 · Matī.**

> Klôk xeç mä ärkā, agā mä tõusô alõs klôk sedôm. Ajēr mä magai tard, ja nū mä um unü. Tems um
> kylm, ja vimõ jällü. Omīkel mä jōdô kafēt ja sȫ pat vôidôk. Naisô veel magā; läpx jubā kōrrô
> kôgõl. Mä dressā vaikõ, votā klamôtôn, ja lǟdô.

> At six I wake up, but I don't get up till seven. Yesterday I went to sleep late, and now I'm
> sleepy. The weather is cold, and it's raining again. At breakfast I drink some coffee and eat
> bread and butter. The wife's still asleep; the child is already running everywhere. I dress
> quietly, grab my coat, and go.

**2 · Gatôl.**

> Gatôl um jubā xumô. Busô tulē tard, muidū. Mä ootā vimel, xapkô ilma. Lopõks tä tulē, ja
> busô-melk muigā: "Moin! Tard jällü, njah?" Sitātôl elkü matī um samā stōrī.

> The street's already bustling. The bus comes late, of course. I wait in the rain, without a hat.
> Finally it comes, and the bus driver grins: "Morning! Late again, eh?" In this city every morning
> is the same story.

**3 · Amtôl.**

> Mä tȫdô amtôl, sitāt kesk. Esmõks mä lugē sônaktôd ja vastāsaatā mõnüt. Tôl mēg kônā tȫ pǟl —
> mis um valmis, mis äb. Kekõ alatü dī: "Se um pehā valmis." Se tähendä: äb valmis. Mä naurā,
> agā ju mä tēdô: demā mä dī samāt.

> I work at an office in the middle of the city. First I read the messages and answer a few. Then
> we go back and forth about the work — what's ready, what isn't. Someone always says: "It's almost
> ready." That means: not ready. I laugh — but you know, I know it: tomorrow I'll be the one saying
> it.

**4 · Lôunā, ja tôl — oi.**

> Lôunāl mēg lǟdô turgôlô ja menjā sāl; mä komprā kalāt ja pat. Pǟrast, tȫ um tasõ — kuni tulē
> fonômus: kontô äb um valmis, ja komprāji ootā. Oi. Nixō — mä pidā lopā sen avuī. Mä tȫdô kuni
> klôk peñç, ja tôl kôg um valmis. Lopõks!

> At lunch we go over to the market square and eat there; I buy some fish and bread. Afterward,
> work is quiet — until a phone call comes: the account isn't ready, and the buyer is waiting.
> Oops. Anyway — I have to finish it today. I work until five, and then everything's done.
> Finally!

**5 · Maçô.**

> Vespôrôl um maçô: sitātôs selkô pēlā. Mä ja tri amīkôd nǟgô sen barôl. Esmõks mēg kaotā,
> nolô–dva. "Nixō," dī amīk, ja komprā olūtôt. Agā tôl — voolpöörd! Mēg sāmā dva mērkôd, ja
> lopõks — tri! Mēg vôitā, tri–dva; kôg barô tõusô ja xumā. Sellü vespôr!

> In the evening there's the match: the city's team is playing. Three friends and I watch it at
> the bar. First we're losing, nil–two. "Oh well," says a friend, and buys a round. But then — the
> momentum-swing! We get two goals, and at the last — a third! We win it, three–two; the whole bar
> is on its feet roaring. What an evening!

**6 · Kōju.**

> Mä kǟvô kōju; äb vimõ amplü. Kōtôl um lǟmi. Naisô ja läpx jubā menjai ôhtôkôn; mēg istā ja kônā
> pǟvô pǟl, ja läpx naurā busô-melk stōrī pǟl. "Ja demā?" dī naisô. "Demā um samā," mä dī, "ja se
> um gūd." Klôk elva mä lǟdô litelô. Havs.

> I walk home; it's not raining anymore. The house is warm. The wife and the kid have already had
> supper; we sit and talk over the day, and the kid laughs at the bus-driver story. "And
> tomorrow?" says the wife. "Tomorrow's the same," I say, "and that's fine." At eleven I go to
> bed. Good night.

## What the text exercises (the working language, on display)

- **Aspect on the object's case, not the verb:** *jōdô kafēt / komprā kalāt / komprā olūtôt*
  (partitive: some, a round) against *votā klamôtôn / lopā sen / menjai ôhtôkôn* (object case:
  the whole thing, done). One morphological contrast carrying English's "some/a" vs "the… up."
- **The negation kit:** plain *äb* (*mis äb*), copular (*kontô äb um valmis*), weather-verb
  (*äb vimõ amplü* "isn't raining anymore" — the *äb … amplü* frame), and the echo-negation
  punchline (*pehā valmis … tähendä: äb valmis*).
- **The glue doing real work:** *alõs* (not until), *muidū* (of course), *nixō* (oh well /
  anyway), *ju* (you know), *elkü matī* (every morning), *samā / samāt* (the same — as
  determiner and as partitive object "say the same"), *kekõ* (someone), *sellü* (such a…).
- **Postpositions:** *sitāt kesk* (in the middle of the city), *tȫ pǟl / pǟvô pǟl* (about the
  work / over the day), *xapkô ilma* (without a hat).
- **The two-class past in passing:** *magai, menjai* (Class I *-ā → -ai*), *dī* narrative
  present beside it — a normal day needs almost no past tense, which is itself a finding.
- **Register reach:** the same grammar runs breakfast talk, office hedging, and the terrace
  press-word *voolpöörd* without changing gear.
