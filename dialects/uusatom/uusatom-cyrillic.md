# The Uusatôm Cyrillic — Metropolitan Nelôxi in full Cyrillic

*The script standard of **Uusatôm** (Odessa) — the founding-diaspora city on the Black Sea
(`../../world/boundaries.md`, Region 15). A script overlay on **Metropolitan** Nelôxi — not Maré,
not a dialect, not one word or rule of grammar changed. The same language, wholly transliterated:
Uusatôm is a two-script border city, Cyrillic locally, Latin everywhere else. The model is Dungan —
a whole language carried into Cyrillic because Cyrillic can hold all of it.*

## 1 · The governing principle

**Transliterate wholesale when the target script can carry the whole phonology.** Cyrillic can:
every Nelôxi sound has a clean Cyrillic rendering, including the sounds the Latin orthography
needs its special letters for. So there is no mixing — the opposite decision from Magau
(`../../mare/magau-script.md`), for the opposite reason.

The lore is already in the boundaries file: a 16th-century Nelôxian trading post that grew up
inside the Cyrillic-writing Black Sea world develops a Cyrillic written tradition alongside the
Latin one. Four centuries of Greek, Ukrainian, Yiddish, Turkish, and Genoese accretion gave the
city its crust; the Cyrillic page is that history made visible.

## 2 · The mapping — one grapheme per sound

The source inventory is the coursebook's, untouched: **a b d e f g h i j k l m n o p r s t u v x y
ä ö ô ç ñ**, long **ā ē ī ō ū ǟ ȫ**, the geminate **l·l**, the palatal **ļ** (*kēļ, tūļ*), and the
living word-final ending **-õ** (*kaunõ, sagõ*). This table renders it; it does not amend it.

### Vowels

| Latin | Cyrillic | Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| a · ā | **а · а̄** | |
| e · ē | **е · е̄** | always plain [e], never iotated — the Ukrainian value, not the Russian |
| i · ī | **і · і̄** | the Black Sea *і* — Ukrainian today, and the letter Odessa's old print used |
| o · ō | **о · о̄** | |
| u · ū | **у · ӯ** | |
| ä · ǟ | **ә · ә̄** | the Turkic-Cyrillic [æ] letter |
| ö · ȫ | **ө · ө̄** | the Turkic-Cyrillic [ø] letter |
| y | **ү** | the Turkic-Cyrillic [y] letter |
| ô | **ъ** | the Bulgarian value: ъ = [ɤ] exactly. The old yer, a Black Sea native, promoted back to a full vowel — the signature letter of the standard, as ô is of the Latin |

Length is a macron in both scripts: the diacritic carries over one-to-one (а̄ е̄ і̄ о̄ ӯ ә̄ ө̄).

### Consonants

| Latin | Cyrillic | | Latin | Cyrillic |
|:---|:---|---|:---|:---|
| b | **б** | | n | **н** |
| d | **д** | | ñ | **нь** |
| f | **ф** | | p | **п** |
| g | **г** | | r | **р** |
| h | **х** | | s | **с** |
| j | **й** | | t | **т** |
| k | **к** | | v | **в** |
| l | **л** | | x [ʃ] | **ш** |
| ļ | **ль** | | ç [ts] | **ц** |
| m | **м** | | | |

- **The soft sign is a pure palatal mark** (ñ → нь, ļ → ль) and keeps that job even before a
  vowel: *tūļāi* → **тӯльа̄і**. This is the Turkic-Cyrillic habit, not the Russian one.
- **No iotated letters.** я ю ё є do not exist in this standard; [j] is always **й** (*jôvā* →
  **йъва̄**, *jā* → **йа̄**). One grapheme per sound cuts both ways.
- **Geminates double**, as in Latin: *tt kk ss nn* → тт кк сс нн; the interpunct geminate *l·l*
  is written **лл**.
- **§47 carries over by sound:** x is **ш** where it reads [ʃ] and **кс** where a proper noun
  reads it [ks] — *Nelôxi* → **Нелъкси**, exactly as the Latin rule says tant-SEE-ka.

### Scar letters (worn openly in Cyrillic too)

| Latin scar | Cyrillic | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| ž, dž | **ж, дж** | *dīdžē* → **ді̄дже̄** |
| w | **ў** | *wifī* → **ўіфі̄** |
| ü (loan-spelling of [y]) | **ү** | *mersü* → **мерсү** |
| œ | **ө** | *flœzī* → **флөзі̄** |
| legacy š (grandfathered only) | **ш** | *krašô* → **крашъ** |

## 3 · Where Cyrillic is flatter than Latin — the Dungan effect

Latin Nelôxi keeps etymology in its spelling; a phonemic transliteration cannot. Four Latin
distinctions merge, by design, and the merges are documented rather than ambiguous:

| Latin distinction | One Cyrillic grapheme | What is lost |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **x** / legacy **š** — both [ʃ] | **ш** | the §109 spelling history |
| **ô** / the living ending **-õ** — both [ɤ] | **ъ** | the v4.0 reform's trail (*kaunõ* → **каунъ**) |
| **y** / loan **ü** — both [y] | **ү** | which quarry the word came through |
| **ö** / scar **œ** | **ө** | the *flœzī*-class scar |

Consequence: **Latin → Cyrillic is deterministic; Cyrillic → Latin needs the dictionary.** The
Latin page stays the etymological record — which is one reason Latin remains the standard
everywhere but the Black Sea, and the archive script of canon. This is precisely the Dungan
situation, and it is a feature: the Uusatôm page is the most purely *phonemic* text in the
language.

## 4 · Worked passage

Canon calibration texts (`../../reader/calibration-texts.md`), rendered — Cyrillic, Latin
original, English.

**The North Wind and the Sun (1, 4, 6, 7):**

> **Пъйа̄тӯль йа пәікү діскутаі, кен ӯлі фортмі.**
> *Pôjātūļ ja päikü diskutai, ken ūli fortmi.*
> "The North Wind and the Sun argued over which of them was the stronger."
>
> **Пъйа̄тӯль тӯльа̄і форт, ага̄ реіса̄йі әб вотаі мәнтлън маха.**
> *Pôjātūļ tūļāi fort, agā reisāji äb votai mäntlôn maha.*
> "The North Wind blew hard, but the traveler did not take the cloak off."
>
> **Пәікү ні̄раі лә̄мі, йа реіса̄йі вотаі мәнтлън маха.**
> *Päikü nīrai lǟmi, ja reisāji votai mäntlôn maha.*
> "The Sun shone warm, and the traveler took the cloak off."
>
> **Ніімъдъ пәікү ӯлі фортмі куі пъйа̄тӯль.**
> *Niimôdõ päikü ūli fortmi kui pôjātūļ.*
> "So the Sun was the stronger of the two."

**Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Article 1:**

> **Къг інім сүнда̄ лі̄берү йа үхүва̄гъ німпундъс йа ре̄тъс.**
> *Kôg inim syndā līberü ja ühüvāgô nimpundôs ja rētôs.*
> "All human beings are born free and equal in dignity and rights."
>
> **Не ум пъхйък йа ісүліврък, йа не піда̄ елә теінүтък үхүвъркс.**
> *Ne um pôhjôk ja isülivrôk, ja ne pidā elä teinütõk ühüvôrks.*
> "They are endowed with reason and conscience, and should live toward one another in brotherhood."

*(Note the flattenings live in this very passage: the ending of *Niimôdõ* and the *ô* of *pôjātūļ*
are both ъ; the *y* of *syndā* and the *ü* of *päikü* are both ү.)*

## 5 · Guardrails & governance

- **Script only.** No grammar changes, no vocabulary changes, no new phonemes. Spoken Uusatôm
  Nelôxi is Metropolitan; the coursebook's Latin orthography remains the archive form of canon,
  and this table is an *alternate rendering* of its inventory, never an amendment to it.
- **One grapheme per sound, no redundancy** — the same principle that governs the Latin
  orthography, applied strictly (hence §3's merges).
- Documented on the model of the Common Register (`../../saharannaise/COMMON-REGISTER.md`): the
  mapping table is **closed**; a text may not invent Cyrillic renderings outside it without a
  ruling. The keeper is Uusatôm's own register office under the Kēļs Kolēgi — the sound inventory
  is the Kolēgi's; only the letterforms are local.

*A deviation from the build brief, recorded: the brief listed the Slavic nasals **ę ą** and living
**š/č** in the inventory to be mapped. Canon says otherwise — the boundaries file rules *ę/ą →
en/an* ("no new phonemes"), and the lint gate retires š/č (legacy words only). This standard maps
the inventory the coursebook and `tools/regen_data.py` actually define, per the brief's own
coordination guardrail.*
