# Inland Nelõxi — *Nurõkēļ*

The conservative rural variety beyond the metroplex. The one true dialect boundary.
An overlay on the standard, but mostly *subtractive* — it resists what the metrolect
adds, and preserves older forms.

## Correspondence rules (standard → inland)

1. **No clipping.** Full -õ and geminate l·l always retained; often *more*
   conservative than the standard (*famīl·la*, never *famīl*).
2. **Native synonym preferred** over every loan pair: *rānd* over *strānd*; avoids
   *fluks* entirely; uses *nū* but rarely the reduplicated/cased ladder.
3. **Loan avoidance.** Late-stratum and Slavic lexemes are replaced by native
   circumlocutions or simply absent; a Marīsõ Slavic loan is typically not
   understood inland.
4. **Fuller vowel harmony.** Harmonizing suffixes never reduce; the -õ-/-e- linking
   vowels stay crisp where casual metrolect speech blurs them.
5. **Archaic retention.** Inland keeps older doublet members and fuller compound
   forms where the metrolect has shortened them.

## Sample (standard → inland)

- Std *Strānd um kaug.* → Inland *Rānd um kaug.* "The shore is far." (native synonym)
- Std/metro *fluks!* → Inland *nūnū!* (native reduplication, no loan interjection)
- Std *Famīl·la um sūr.* → Inland *Famīl·la um sūr.* (unchanged; full form is native here)
