# The Six Standards — Comparison Chart

*Metropolitan Nelôxi · Saharannaise · Congolaise · Maré · Verdenese · the East fork, laid side
by side.* The chart lineage: the three-standard chart (`../congolaise/comparison-chart.md`)
showed identical grammar under three divergent lexicons; the four-standard chart
(`../mare/comparison-chart.md`) added the one licensed grammar bend; the five-way showcase
(`../verdenese/showcase-pentalect.md`) added the settler saturation. This chart adds the East
fork and completes the grid — because the sixth standard diverges in the one dimension none of
the first five touched: **all of the words kept, all of the grammatical lead handed over.**

---

## 0 · The grid — who keeps what

| Standard | The words | The grammar | The move | A mainlander is lost in… |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Metropolitan** | the trunk (five quarries over a Livonian core) | the trunk | — | — |
| **Saharannaise** | domestic core rerooted (Hassaniya/Zenaga) | invariant | reroot one substrate | the words (Level 3 opaque) |
| **Congolaise** | domestic core fused (Pulaar/Soninke/Wolof) | invariant | fuse three tributaries | the words |
| **Maré** | Metropolitan word-stock | sanded in one closed, ratified place | bend the edge | almost nothing — clipped joints |
| **Verdenese** | Romance-saturated + Guaraní land-layer | invariant | saturate a settler crucible | the words (half-guessable) |
| **East fork** | **Metropolitan word-stock, whole** | **Circassian-led, wholly different** | **invert the layering** | **the structure** |

Read it as two columns of proof: the first five vary the *words* column and hold the grammar;
the sixth holds the words and varies the *grammar* column. One lexicon shown surviving any
structure; one structure shown surviving any lexicon.

## 1 · The structural contrast — feature by feature

The heart of the chart. Because Saharannaise, Congolaise, and Verdenese run the Metropolitan
frame exactly, they share its column; Maré's spoken bends and the East fork each get their own.

| Feature | Metropolitan (= Sah. = Cong. = Verd.) | Deck Maré | East fork |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Word order | SVO, fixed | SVO | **SOV — verb-final** |
| Alignment | nominative–accusative | same (object **-n** may drop) | **ergative — agent takes -r** |
| Object marking | object **-n**, partitive **-t** | -n droppable | **unmarked (absolutive); -n retired** |
| Person on the verb | none — verbs isolating | none | **obligatory agreement prefixes (ABS + ERG), role read from slot order** |
| Direction | adverbs and cases | same | **preverbs packed into the verb: sin-, tôl-, maha-** |
| Negation | pre-verbal particle **äb** | **äb** | **verb-internal suffix -p; äb does not exist** |
| Past tense | suffix **-i**, two classes | **particle ti** (spoken) | **-i kept**, riding inside the verb complex |
| Completion / prospect | object's case / time-words | **fin / kē** particles (spoken) | time-words (partitive aspect retired) |
| Future | none — time-words | none on paper | none |
| Possession | **Mäl um rā** (locative + copula) | same | **Rā m-um** — packed into an agreeing copula |
| Polar question | initial particle **ka** | same | **verb-final enclitic -ka** |
| Goal case | **-lô/-lä**, harmonized | **-lô** invariant | **-le** invariant (the Estonian allative) |
| Postpositions | ground in linking **-s** (*kōtôs al*) | same | ground in oblique **-m** (*kōtôm al*) |
| Counted nouns | partitive (*tri kalāt*) | partitive | **bare** (*tri kalā*) |
| Complement clause | *et* + clause, after the verb | same | **clause + et, before the verb** |
| Relative clause | participle or *ken/mis* clause | same | **prenominal participle primary** |
| Adj–N · Num–N · Gen–N · postpositional NP | head-final NP | same | **kept — identical** |
| Derivation, dozenal numbers, harmony, gradation | the shared machinery | same | **kept — identical** |

Two things to notice. Maré's column differs from Metropolitan in exactly three cells, by joint
ratification, spoken register only. The East column differs in **most of the clause rows and
none of the phrase rows** — the noun phrase and the word-building machinery are untouched,
which is why every East sentence is built of parts a mainlander already owns.

## 2 · One scene, six ways — the lines that show it

From the six-way showcase (`showcase-hexalect.md`), the two most diagnostic rows:

**"The night is cold"** — the domestic layer, where lexical divergence is deepest:

| | |
|:---|:---|
| **Metr.** | Nahtô um kylm. |
| **Sah.** | Līl um barād. |
| **Cong.** | Jemma um ɓūɓ. |
| **Mar.** | Nahtô um kylm. |
| **Verd.** | Notē um frīu. |
| **East** | **Nahtô kylm um.** |

The sisters go dark word by word; the East line keeps Metropolitan's letters and moves one.

**"Yesterday I went to the market"** — the grammar row, where structural divergence is deepest:

| | |
|:---|:---|
| **Metr.** | Ajēr mä lǟdi markôtôlô. |
| **Sah.** | Ajēr mä lǟdi sūg-lô. |
| **Cong.** | Ajēr mä lǟdi lumo-lô. |
| **Mar.** | Ajēr mä **ti lǟdô** markôt-lô. |
| **Verd.** | Ajēr mä lǟdi ferjā-lô. |
| **East** | Ajēr mä markôtôle **me-lǟdi**. |

Four standards swap the noun and hold the frame; Maré holds the noun and sands two joints; the
East fork holds the noun and rebuilds the clause — goal leveled to *-le*, verb to the end, the
subject echoed into it as a prefix.

## 3 · The lexical layering — same architecture, sixth loadout

Every standard is the same four-layer building with different tenants:

| Standard | Domestic core | Casual loan layer | Land / realia layer | Learned crust |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **Metropolitan** | Baltic-native | **Low German** (*melk, moin, dankô*) | — | Romance-Latin |
| **Saharannaise** | Hassaniya/Zenaga (rerooted) | *(inside the reroot)* | desert realia (*ṭāyf, gāfila*) | Romance kept |
| **Congolaise** | three-tributary fusion | *(inside the fusion)* | river realia (*wālo, jeli, kora*) | Romance kept |
| **Maré** | Metropolitan stock | the deck loan-layer (territorial words) | ocean gap-words (*taifūn, motū*) | Romance kept |
| **Verdenese** | Romance-saturated | *(the saturation is the casual layer)* | Guaraní land-layer (*jaguaretē, kaʼaguy*) | Romance kept |
| **East fork** | **Metropolitan stock, unchanged** | **Turkic** (*para, bazar, txai, dost, haidē*) | eastern realia (*txorbā, txarxô, mangāl*) | Romance kept |

The East fork's row is the tell: its casual layer sits in **exactly the Low German slot** —
*para* beside *rā* as *melk* beside *lēt* — which is why its doublets feel native to the
system even though its clauses do not.

## 4 · Signature forms — each seat's unmistakable line

The first five standards are known by their *words*; the East fork is known by its *shapes*:

| Only in… | Signature | Gloss |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Saharannaise** | **žəml · gāfila · ṭāyf** | camel · caravan · sandstorm |
| **Congolaise** | **jeli · terānga · kora** | griot · the guest-honor ethic · the harp-lute |
| **Maré** | **ti / fin / kē · taifūn · lēve-lēve** | the particle tense · the named storm · "no rush" |
| **Verdenese** | **jaguaretē · matē · saudādē** | the jaguar · the gourd · the ache |
| **East fork** | **Kalāji-r kalā vendā.** | the ergative *-r* — coursebook words, nobody else's clause |
| **East fork** | **Me-nǟgô-p.** | "I don't see it" — subject, object, and negation in one word |
| **East fork** | **Rā m-um.** | "I have money" — possession packed into two syllables of agreeing copula |

Back-flow rides the shared convention: **txai (Id.→Metr.)** and **haidē (Id.→Metr.)** are the
live candidates, drifting up through Uusatôm — but note what *cannot* back-flow: the grammar.
*-r*, *-p*, and the packed verb are namespaced to `east/` by design; a doublet can climb the
trunk, a clause structure cannot.

## 5 · How to read the six-way divergence

- **Lexical rows:** Metropolitan, Maré, and the **East fork** pair up — three seats, one
  word-stock; Saharannaise, Congolaise, and Verdenese each stand alone.
- **Grammar rows:** Metropolitan, Saharannaise, Congolaise, and Verdenese pair up; deck Maré
  stands a bounded step away; the **East fork stands entirely alone**.
- **The asymmetry always runs the same way** — the periphery reads the metropole better than
  the reverse — but for two different reasons: everywhere else the mainlander drowns in
  unknown words inside a known machine; in the east they float on known words inside an
  unknown one.
- **The gaps still carry the history.** The desert has no sea, the river no atoll, the Baltic
  no typhoon, the pampa no caravan — and only the east needed its grammar to shake hands with
  the Caucasus.
