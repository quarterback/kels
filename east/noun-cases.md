# The East Fork — the Estonian Noun-Case Layer

*The second chair. In every other fork the Finnic element is the whole orchestra; here it is
demoted to one section — the noun morphology — and that demotion is the East fork's defining
move. Estonian was chosen as the secondary partner because it is the most evolved, most
completely codified Finnic option: the case endings below lean on its shapes. It is the
thread back to the Neloxian family, felt more than seen — and §5 is the fence that keeps it
from ever becoming the master grammar.*

---

## §1 · The leveled case table

The East fork keeps the Metropolitan case *system* and re-cuts three things: the two
Circassian-motivated cases enter (**ergative -r**, **oblique -m**), the object *-n* leaves,
and the endings level to **one invariant shape each**, Estonian-style — the front/back
alternants of the goal case collapse to a single *-le* (the Estonian allative), the cleaner,
more evolved cut of the same Finnic cloth.

| Case | Ending | Use | Note |
|---|---|---|---|
| absolutive | — | intransitive subject; direct object | replaces both Metr. plain and object *-n* roles |
| **ergative** | **-r** | transitive agent | the fork's signature (`grammar-circassian.md` §3) |
| **oblique** | **-m** | ground of a postposition | *kōtôm al* "under the house" (Metr. *kōtôs al*) |
| genitive | -s | possessor, adnominal only | *mäs nim* "my name" — unchanged |
| partitive | -t | partial / mass quantity | **narrowed** — see §3 |
| locative | -l | in, at, on | unchanged; = Estonian adessive *-l* |
| goal | **-le** | to, into, onto; dative-recipient | **invariant** — Estonian allative; replaces harmonized *-lô/-lä* |
| source | -lt | from | unchanged; = Estonian ablative *-lt* |
| comitative | -k | with, using | unchanged |
| plural | -d | before case endings | unchanged; = Estonian *-d* |

The quiet joke of the table: half of it is "unchanged **and** Estonian" at once — *-l, -lt,
-t, -d, -s* are shapes Metropolitan and Estonian already share, the family resemblance that
was always there. The East fork's Estonian lean mostly consists of *noticing* this and
finishing the pattern: one shape per ending, and *-le* where Metropolitan harmonized.

## §2 · Morphophonology

Nelôxi mechanics, untouched: the harmony helper (**-ô-** back / **-e-** front) joins a
consonant-final stem to a consonant-initial ending; vowel-final stems attach directly;
consonant gradation (*kk/pp/tt → k/p/t*) fires as always. The invariant endings attach
*after* the helper, so a back stem can carry a front-shaped ending — that is the leveling,
worn openly (the Maré precedent: *satām-lô* whatever the stem).

Model nouns, East-fork declension:

| Case | *kōt* "house" | *mer* "sea" | *kalā* "fish" |
|---|---|---|---|
| absolutive | kōt | mer | kalā |
| ergative | kōtô-r | mere-r | kalā-r |
| oblique | kōtô-m | mere-m | kalā-m |
| genitive | kōtôs | meres | kalās |
| partitive | kōtôt | meret | kalāt |
| locative | kōtôl | merel | kalāl |
| goal | kōtôle | merele | kalāle |
| source | kōtôlt | merelt | kalālt |
| comitative | kōtôk | merek | kalāk |

Pronoun ergatives: *mä-r, sä-r, tä-r, mēg-er, tēg-er, ne-r.* The goal case of the pronouns
does dative work exactly as in Metropolitan, and lands on the identical form: *mäle* "to me"
— *Mäle sin-donā!* "Give it here to me!"

**The -ôr collision, named.** On a back consonant-stem the written ergative (*kōtô-r*)
coincides with the agent suffix *-ôr* (*žəmlôr*). The fork lives with the homograph the way
every language lives with a few: teaching texts hyphenate the case (*kōt-ôr* never means
"houser"), running prose relies on the syntax — an ergative stands before a transitive verb
complex, an agent noun declines. Xasā style is to prefer pronoun agents in casual prose
where the collision would distract.

## §3 · What leaves, what narrows

- **The object case -n is retired.** Its job — flagging the direct object — is done by the
  ergative on the agent, agreement in the verb, and SOV order. *Kalāji-r kalā vendā*, not
  \**kalān*.
- **The partitive narrows to quantity.** Metropolitan runs aspect through the object's case
  (partitive = ongoing) and puts counted nouns in the partitive (*tri kalāt*). The
  Circassian lead has no such machinery, so the East fork keeps *-t* only for true partial
  and mass readings (*veçôt donā!* "give some water!"; *jôvā pǟvôt parlāi* "talked of the
  good day"). **Counted nouns stand bare**: *tri kalā, dva txai* — the numeral itself is the
  quantifier. Aspect falls back on the time-word toolkit (*jubā, nū, sagõ*), where futurity
  always lived.

## §4 · The dative-recipient — goal *-le* at work

Ditransitives keep the Metropolitan frame with the leveled ending: recipient in the goal
case, before the verb like every dependent. *Mä-r livrô dostôle mô-donai.* "I gave the book
to a friend." (Metr. *Mä donai livrôn dostôlô* — same words, same *-le/-lô* logic, one
shape, the agent echoed in the verb.)

## §5 · The fence — Estonian stays secondary

The failure mode on this flank is drift into Estonian: the noun endings invite it, and a
generation of careless coinage would let the fork slide from "Nelôxi restructured" to
"bad Estonian." The fence, enforced at Xasā review like the core-vocabulary rule:

1. **Noun endings only.** Estonian supplies case shapes and nothing else — **no Estonian
   verb morphology** (no *-sin* past, no *ma-/da-* infinitives, no *-nud* participle), no
   Estonian pronouns, no Estonian function words.
2. **No Estonian vocabulary.** An Estonian citation-shape in an East-fork coinage is
   rejected exactly as it is on the mainland (§112/§113 of the charter) — the ban does not
   relax merely because the fork's case layer has an Estonian accent. Core words are
   coursebook Nelôxi; casual borrowings come from the Turkic quarry, not from Tallinn.
3. **No õ, ever.** The retired graphemes stay retired in this namespace (`script.md`); no
   East-fork form may wear the Estonian *õ* the mainland spent a reform removing.
4. **The case table is closed.** No fourteen-case creep: the fork does not adopt Estonian's
   illative, inessive, elative, translative, terminative, essive, or abessive. Ten rows in
   §1, and a new row is a joint matter, not an Estonian import.
