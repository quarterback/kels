# Saharannaise Lexicon — Domain A · Batch 3
### Administration · Documents · Port · Sea-trade

The Level-1 register — the department's paperwork and its working waterfront. This is where
the three strata show at once: the **Nelôxian** column runs the shared Romance-Latin learned
crust (reused canon where it exists), the **Saharannaise** column runs the Arabic maritime
and administrative vocabulary of the Atlantic coast, and the grammar underneath is one. Many
entries are **doublets** (both standards, transparent to a mainlander); the maritime ones are
the pipeline through which Saharan words **back-flow** into the coursebook.

Roots Hassaniya Arabic, processed through §3 and the shared orthographic agreement. Nelôxian
forms tagged *(canon)* are live coursebook headwords; *(Rom.)* marks a Romance metropolitan
form of the learned register. **34 headwords.**

## Back-flow candidates — proposed to the Kēļs Kolēgi (not yet merged)

Domain A is the maritime register, so it is the natural source of **back-flow** — Saharan
terms that, over generations of Atlantic trade, drift into metropolitan sailors' speech.
This is a **proposal from the Dīwān to the Kolēgi**, not a change made here: Saharannaise
does not edit the coursebook. Adoption is the Rector's call in the core, per
`COMMON-REGISTER.md` §4. If accepted, each would **nativize** on entry — the loan sheds its
Saharan scars to fit mainland phonology, exactly as the Creole Principle says a loan is
"sanded just enough," so the mainland form would diverge from the Saharannaise citation form:

| Saharannaise | Proposed Metropolitan form | Gloss | Nativization |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **ġabīn** | *gabīn* | sea-fog, coastal fog-bank | ġ→g (ghayn sheds); doublet of native **udū** |
| **gāfila** | *gāfila* | convoy, caravan | already mainland-legal; would fill a *convoy* gap |
| **ṭāyf** | *tāyf* | sea-haze, dust-fog (a sailing hazard) | ṭ→t (emphatic sheds) |

These stand in the VOC back-flow register (`VOC.md` §4) alongside **sayl, erg, girba,
nəžma**. If the Kolēgi adopts one, it enters the coursebook there — with a `(Sah.→Metr.)`
etymology — and only then becomes mainland canon. The federation loop points toward the
mainland; the mainland pulls the trigger.
