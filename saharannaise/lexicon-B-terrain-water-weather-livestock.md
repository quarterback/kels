## Rulings applied on archive

The batch was reviewed against the Kēļs Dīwān's register (`saharannaise.md`) and mainland
canon. Most entries **APPROVE & ARCHIVE** as submitted; the following were corrected.

1. **Agent suffix normalized `-ōr → -ôr`.** The hybrids were submitted with a long
   *-ōr*. §1/§4 require the mainland derivational suffix to keep its **exact** form —
   the skeleton is unaltered mainland. Archived as **žəmlôr, māzôr, xmārôr**. Long *ō*
   is not part of the Saharannaise inventory (§3); a Saharan long-o is written **ô**.

2. **Raw-source headwords sanctioned to the §47 form.** Three entries were listed with
   their un-processed Hassaniya *ḥ*: *ḥmār, ḥarr,* and the *ḥ* inside *ṣḥāb*. §47 maps
   ḥ → x systematically, so the **headword** carries the sanctioned form, not the raw
   root: **xmār, xarr, ṣxāb**. (The emphatic *ṣ* in *ṣxāb* is kept as a scar; only the
   *ḥ* maps.)

3. **Two asserted Domain-A equivalents demoted to *pending*.** The batch cited mainland
   forms *līvômō* "sand-land" (for **erg**) and *sōlapannô* "salt-pan" (for **sabx**).
   Neither is in mainland canon — a Domain-B daughter-lect batch has no authority to
   coin mainland words. They are held as **proposed** mainland equivalents; they become
   real doublets only if the Rector coins and merges them into the coursebook. The
   verified doublet **sāgī ↔ kanā** stands (kanā is canon).

4. **Recount.** 39 headwords, not 38 (submission miscounted by one).

**No REJECT — MAINLAND BIAS** was needed: every domestic/ecological term used a
Hassaniya or Zenaga root; no Baltic/Slavic compound was smuggled in, and no calque
("dust-wind" and the like) was submitted.

## Collisions with mainland canon (informational)

- **dūn** "small dune" ~ **düna** "twelve" — distinct words, distinct vowels; noted.
- **ṣxāb** "cloud" vs mainland **pilv** — the daughter lect names rain-cloud separately.
- **barād** "desert cold" vs mainland **kylm** — register/ecology split, not a synonym.

## Back-flow watch (for future *mainland* batches)

**ṭāyf** (sandstorm), **sayl** (flash flood), **erg** (sand sea), **ġabīn** (Atlantic
fog) are the likeliest to drift back into mainland maritime vocabulary as Atlanta's
weather becomes relevant to Atlantic navigation. Flagged only; they enter mainland canon
solely by a Rector merge, never automatically.
