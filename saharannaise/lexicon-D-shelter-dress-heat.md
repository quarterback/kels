# Saharannaise Lexicon — Domain D · Batch 4
### Shelter · Dress · Heat-Craft (the design of living cool)

The material-culture register of desert heat-adaptation — how the Atlantic Sahara dresses, builds,
and keeps cool. Exactly the local-conditions vocabulary the pluricentric model says Atlanta's own
life generates; a mainlander has no words for most of it because the mainland never needed them.
Sourced **Hassaniya Arabic (Hass.)** and **Zenaga Berber (Zen.)**, processed through the shared
orthographic agreement (`COMMON-REGISTER.md`): qaf→g, emphatic ṭ ṣ ḍ ẓ preserved as scars, ʿayn
kept, š/x per §47. Real design-words, borrowed whole — not Baltic calques (the compound ban holds:
no *cover-cloth* calque for a tagelmust).

The governing insight of the whole register, in the Atlantic Sahara's own logic: **covered is
cooler** — heat is met by *shading and layering*, not by stripping down; and *moving air*, not
cold, is what cools.

## Dress — covered is cooler

| Headword | PoS | Source root | Gloss | Doublet / note |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **dərrāʿ** | n. | Hass. *dərrāʿa* | the boubou; the wide loose robe that shades the body while air moves beneath | Metr. gap (mainland *mäntlô* is a fitted cloak, wrong principle). The garment IS the cooling system. |
| **tāgəlmust** | n. | Tuareg/Berber *tagelmust* | the veil-turban; the wound face-and-head cloth | Kept whole — the real word. Both dress and shade and dust-guard in one. |
| **xaf** | n. | Zen. (air-gap) | the loose layer / breathing gap — the space held between cloth and skin where air moves | The *principle* of the dress: it is the gap that cools, not the cloth. |
| **ləfḥa** | n. | Hass. *lefḥa* "blast of heat" | the scorch, the blast of midday heat the dress defends against | The enemy the whole register answers. |

## Heat-craft — cool without power

| Headword | PoS | Source root | Gloss | Doublet / note |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **zīr** | n. | Hass. *zīr* "large water-jar" | the zeer; the clay evaporative cooler — pot within pot, wet sand between, food kept fresh for days without electricity | Kept whole — the real word. The clay fridge. |
| **taxsint** | n. | Zen. (cool-root) | evaporative coolness; the cool a wet surface draws from moving air | The *principle* the zīr runs on — the same moving-air logic as the dress and the dome. |
| **rṭūba** | n. | Hass. *rṭūba* "damp" | the wetness / damp packed to cool (the wet sand of the zīr, the dampened cloth) | Emphatic ṭ; the working medium of taxsint. |
| **bārid** | adj. | Hass. *bārid* "cool/cold" | cool, cooled (of a thing kept in shade or by evaporation) | Cousin to **barād** (lexicon B, the desert cold-season). Here: the achieved cool of good design. |

**14 headwords.** The register's through-line: dress, shelter, and cooling are ONE idea in the
Atlantic Sahara — shade the sun, hold the thermal mass, and let moving air do the work. Every root
here is a real Saharan design-word; none is a Baltic calque. This is the vocabulary of a culture
that solved heat before electricity, and the language records the solutions.

## Note — the three "principle" words

Batch 4 introduces a device the earlier batches did not: alongside each concrete artifact, the
register names the *physical principle* it runs on. This is deliberate — the material culture is an
engineering tradition, and its vocabulary carries the theory, not only the objects.

- **xaf** (the air-gap) is the principle of **dərrāʿ** and **tāgəlmust** — the loose layer cools
  because air moves in the gap.
- **fəžža** (the crown-vent) is the principle of **tazəqqa** — the dome breathes because warm air
  escapes the peak and pulls cool air in at the door.
- **taxsint** (evaporative coolness) is the principle of **zīr** — the pot cools because the damp
  **rṭūba** gives its wet to the moving air.

One logic — *moving air over shade or damp* — surfaces three times, in dress, in building, and in
storage. The lexicon records a solved problem the mainland never had to solve.
