# Saharannaise Lexicon — Domain C · Batch 2
### Food · Kin · Market · The Day

The Level-3 core — the register where the reroot bites hardest and a mainlander is
illiterate without training. Drafted by the Kēļs Dīwān (the Saharan Register). Sourced deliberately
**Berber-forward** where the substrate owns the concept (water, bread, couscous, the
settled house). Roots Hassaniya Arabic (Hass.) or Zenaga Berber (Zen.), processed through
§3 and the shared orthographic agreement (`COMMON-REGISTER.md`). Doublets are tagged
**(Metr.)** ↔ **(Sah.)**; a verified mainland form is named, a mainland gap is marked.

**43 headwords.** Four new orthographic rulings issued (see rulings, folded into the
Common Register).

## Rulings — orthographic agreement extended

Batch 2 exercised source phonetics Batch 1 did not, forcing four rulings under the
`COMMON-REGISTER.md` §2 amendment clause. All four are folded into the agreement and into
`saharannaise.md` §3.

1. **ʿayn (ʿ) elides.** The Arabic pharyngeal *ʿayn* is not written; it drops, coloring or
   lengthening the adjacent vowel (*lḥam → lxam*, *ʿaṭsh → aṭx*, *bāʿ → bā*). Continues the
   Batch-1 treatment of *ʿerg → erg*, *ʿāləg → āləg*, now stated as a rule.
2. **Uvular qaf → g.** Hassaniya realizes classical *q* as [g]; it is written **g**
   (*sūq → sūg*, *qamar → gamar*, *qabīla → gabīla*, *qāfila → gāfila*, *gamḥ → gamx*). This
   is a source-phonetic fact, distinct from the mainland's own g.
3. **Velar fricative kh → x.** The friction rule (§47) already sent *ḥ* and *š* to **x**;
   the velar fricative *kh* joins them (*khū → xū*, *kheyma → xəyma*, *rkhīṣ → rxīṣ*,
   *ukht → əxt*). One landing, **x**, for the whole back-fricative set.
4. **Emphatic inventory completed — ẓ.** The emphatic-scar series is closed as **ṭ ṣ ḍ ẓ**;
   *ẓell → ẓəl* adds the last member. The full emphatic series is preserved, never mapped.

## Homophone noted

- **bā** "father" (n.) ~ **bā** "to sell" (*bāʿ*, v., held for a later market batch): same
  surface after ʿayn-elision, disambiguated by part of speech and SVO position. Kept, not
  engineered away — real languages carry such pairs. (Batch 2 archives only the kin sense.)
