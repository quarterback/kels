# Saharannaise — the Atlantic standard of Nelôxi

*Saharannaise kēļ.* The Nelôxi of **Atlanta** — the Atlantic Sahara Provinces & Sahel
State, Nelôxia's **sovereign sister state** on the Atlantic Saharan coast (a former union
with the mainland, now independent — see `../world/atlanta-lore.md`): a **co-standard**,
not a dialect. It runs the full Nelôxi grammatical skeleton and reroots the domestic,
ecological, and market lexicon wholesale in **Hassaniya Arabic and Zenaga Berber**, while
keeping the Romance-Latin learned crust it shares with the mainland. Governed from its own
seat — the **Kēļs Dīwān** (the Saharan Register) — counted on its own terms, bridged to the
mainland by tags, never merged into the coursebook.

## Why it exists — the sturdiness thesis

Saharannaise is the demonstration that Nelôxi is a real language, not a relexified cipher.
A cipher breaks when you change its words; a language does not. So we change the words as
hard as we can — flip the entire domestic root stock from the Baltic quarries to Arabic
and Berber — and watch the grammar hold. **The grammar is the invariant; the lexicon is
the variable.** *žəmlôr* "camel-driver" is the proof in one word: a Hassaniya root (*žml*)
taking the Livonian agent suffix (*-ôr*) and declining like any native noun. If the
skeleton can carry a root stock from the other side of a sea and still parse, still
decline, still negate with *äb* and command with a bare stem, then the skeleton is a real
grammar and Nelôxi is a real language. Saharannaise is that stress-test, run in public.

## The model — pluricentric, two sovereignties

Nelôxi is **pluricentric** rather than governed by a single supreme registry — the Lusophone
pattern (Lisbon ↔ Brasília), and now literally so: Atlanta is a **sovereign state**, as Brazil
is, sharing one language with the mainland across two independent seats. The relationship was
a shared-crown mercantile union until Atlanta's independence (the authoritative account is
`../world/atlanta-lore.md`); the seats were peers throughout, because the states were peers
in kind — the same civilizational operating system at both ends of a continent-connecting
route. Two standards, two seats, one language:

- **Metropolitan Nelôxi** — the coursebook, governed by the **Kēļs Kolēgi** (the College
  of Language), headed by the **Rector**. The Académie / Lisbon of this language.
- **Saharannaise** — the Atlantic standard, governed by the **Kēļs Dīwān** (the Register
  of the Language; English: the Saharan Register), headed by the **Amīn** (the Keeper;
  English: the Dean). The OQLF / Academia Brasileira of this language.

The two seats are named in deliberate parallel: **Kēļs Kolēgi** ↔ **Kēļs Dīwān**, same
Nelôxi genitive frame (*kēļs* "of the language"), the institution-noun sourced from each
seat's own learned register — Romance *kolēgi* on the mainland, Hassaniya-Arabic *dīwān*
(chancery, state register, account-book) on the coast. The seat's own name enacts the
sturdiness thesis: a Nelôxi skeleton carrying a Saharan root, exactly like *žəmlôr*. Both
head-titles likewise sit in their seat's learned register — Latin *Rector* ↔ Arabic *Amīn*
"the trusted keeper of the records." (*Dīwān* and *amīn* take zero phonetic transformation,
clean like *bīr*/*erg*; they are also the first two Domain-A administrative headwords.)

Neither seat owns the other. What makes them **one language** is not low divergence — the
vocabularies diverge heavily, the way Brazilian *ônibus* and European *autocarro* both
mean "bus" and neither is wrong — but the **shared spine**: the common grammar and the
common orthographic agreement, set out in `COMMON-REGISTER.md`. Divergent words live
side by side, region-tagged, and are never force-merged.

**Atlanta** is a **sovereign state** — the first independent state in Africa — formerly in a
shared-crown mercantile union with Nelôxia and still monetarily braided to it (its currency
shared with or pegged to the mainland's). Nelôxi there was **adopted, never imposed**: already
the working tongue of the merchants, clerks, and lawyers the coast partnered with, and a
polyglot creole with a Latin legal crust threatens neither Berber nor Arabic at home — which
is exactly why the reroot runs so deep. The reroot is not a stylistic choice but the
**predicted linguistic outcome of this history**. The timeline does not fork — only the lect
does. The territory is the Atlantic Saharan coast — real-world Western Sahara — the two halves
**Sāgī əl-Xamrā** (north) and **Wād-Ḍahab** (south), capital **Lāyūna**, southern port
**Daxla** — settled by Nelôxia before any Iberian claim, so it carries no Spanish names. The
full founding history is the authoritative account in `../world/atlanta-lore.md`; the
place-names are in `toponymy.md`.

## §1 · The Root Ecology Split (heavy reroot)

The split between what is kept and what is replaced is deliberate and total.

**Kept — the grammatical skeleton.** Unchanged mainland grammar: no verb agreement, strict
SVO, pre-verbal negation *äb*, bare-stem imperative and hortative *-m*, and the full
derivational suffix set (**-ôr/-õr, -in, -mus/-mys, -ū, -stā, -ldā, -ām/-äm, -tū/-ty**,
causative *-tā*, comparative *-mi*). A mainlander parses any Saharannaise sentence's
structure effortlessly even when every content root is opaque. This is the bridge that
keeps it one language.

**Replaced — the whole domestic root stock.** For **terrain, water, weather, livestock,
food, kin, the market, the day** — the entire lived, domestic, ecological layer — the
roots are Arabic or Berber, and the Baltic quarries are shut out. This is not loan-
sprinkling onto a Nelôxi core; it is a wholesale reroot of the domestic lexicon. At
Level 3 (below) a mainlander is illiterate without training. That is the point, not a bug.

**Retained — the Romance-Latin learned crust.** The reroot is heavy *in the domestic core*;
it does **not** strip the cultivation register. Law, administration, scholarship, formal and
ceremonial naming keep the Romance-Latin layer Nelôxi already carries (the CREOLE-PRINCIPLE's
learned register), shared transparently with the mainland. This is what makes Level 1 legible
in both directions — and why the *Nelôxian* half of a placename is Romance (**Kuldjôg**,
**Pūnkanā**, **Fôntāna**) while the Saharannaise half is Arabic/Berber. Saharannaise is thus
three strata: a **Romance learned crust** (shared, Level 1), the **Nelôxi grammar** (invariant),
and an **Arabic/Berber domestic core** (rerooted, Level 3) — the intelligibility tiers made
lexical.

**Two source strata, mirroring the mainland's own shape.** The mainland is a Livonian
substrate under loan superstrata; Saharannaise repeats the pattern with different stone:

- **Zenaga Berber — the substrate.** The deep, indigenous, older layer: terrain, rock and
  water features, indigenous pastoralism, the land itself (*galt, garg, māz*). The Livonian
  of this coast.
- **Hassaniya Arabic — the superstrate.** The Bedouin-and-trade layer that spread over it:
  the camel economy, weather naming, the market, the wider domestic vocabulary (*žəml,
  ṭāyf, sāgī, ġabīn*). The trade crust.

Where both offer a root, the choice records depth: bedrock and indigenous herding lean
Zenaga; commerce, livestock wealth, and named weather events lean Hassaniya.

**The compound ban.** No Baltic calque for a Saharan reality. A clerk who writes a
*dust-wind* compound for a sandstorm is corrected to the flattened root (**ṭāyf**). The
reality is named, not translated.

## §2 · The Asymmetric Intelligibility Standard

Three tiers of transparency to the mainland, by register:

1. **Level 1 — Formal state & archival** (high written intelligibility). Ledgers,
   manifests, decrees. Transparent structural frame; both sides read the skeleton. This is
   where the Level-1 doublets surface — the clerk writes the mainland word, the worker
   says the Saharan one (**kanā** ↔ **sāgī**).
2. **Level 2 — Urban vernacular** (asymmetric). The docks of Daxla: the structural bridge
   holds, the phonetics drift hard. A mainlander needs heavy exposure; a Saharannaise
   speaker follows the mainland fine, from state schooling. Intelligibility runs one way —
   exactly the Brazil-understands-Lisbon-more-than-the-reverse asymmetry.
3. **Level 3 — Deep interior / domestic** (near-zero). Market, family, camel-herding,
   desert weather. Fully rerooted; a mainlander is illiterate here without training.

## §3 · Orthographic & phonetic sanctioning

Saharannaise writes in the Nelôxian state-archive script, adapted for local phonetics.
The rules are the shared orthographic agreement (`COMMON-REGISTER.md`); in brief:

- **Vowel flattening (ô / ā dominance).** Vowels flatten toward forward-stressed **ô** or
  long **ā**. Source circumflex scars normalize to the archive: *â/î/û → ā/ī/ū* or *ô*
  (mainland §63). Hassaniya final *-a* compresses to zero (*ḥamāda → xamād*, *nāga → nāg*).
- **Article agglutination.** Arabic *al-/el-* welds to the noun as a fixed prefix
  (*al-ʿuyūn → **lāyūn***, whence **Lāyūna**). Zenaga *a-* agglutinates in earlier loans
  (*agargar → **garg***), drops in later ones (*amāz → **māz***); the split records timing.
- **§47 friction.** The whole back-fricative set — pharyngeal **ḥ**, sibilant **š**, and
  velar **kh** — maps to **x** (read [ks] in formal notation, [ʃ] in *š*-source speech):
  *ḥamāda → **xamād***, *šarg → **xarg***, *khū → **xū***.
- **ʿayn elision & qaf → g.** Arabic *ʿayn* (ʿ) is not written — it drops, coloring the
  adjacent vowel (*ʿerg → **erg***, *lḥam → **lxam***). Hassaniya uvular *q* [g] is written
  **g** (*sūq → **sūg***, *qamar → **gamar***).
- **Emphatics as foreign scars.** The full emphatic series **ṭ ṣ ḍ ẓ** is preserved, not
  mapped — the audible announcement of Saharan origin. The **flœzī / dž precedent**: canon
  already wears foreign scars openly (*flœzī* œ; *džezô, dīdžē* dž) rather than sanding them off.
- **The Saharannaise scar inventory.** Beyond the mainland letters: schwa **ə** (*žəml,
  bəkr, kədim*), **ž** (*žəml*), ghayn **ġ** (*ġabīn, ġanm*), emphatics **ṭ ṣ ḍ ẓ**. Long *ō*
  is not used; a Saharan long-o is written **ô**, and the agent suffix stays exactly **-ôr**.

## §4 · Hybrid morphology — the seam that proves the thesis

Saharan root + mainland derivational suffix, productive: **žəmlôr** (camel-driver),
**māzôr** (goatherd), **xmārôr** (donkey-driver). The grammar is Nelôxian, the root is
Saharan, the join is live. The suffix keeps its **exact mainland form -ôr** — the skeleton
is unaltered mainland. Every such weld is a small demonstration of the sturdiness thesis:
the machinery takes a foreign root and runs.

## §5 · The evaluation matrix

Submissions are processed with three actions, logged in the lexicon files:

1. **APPROVE & ARCHIVE** — apply §3, map the root ecology, record the dual entry (mainland
   formal ↔ Saharannaise vernacular) with source root and gloss.
2. **REJECT — MAINLAND BIAS** — flag a Baltic/Slavic root or calque used where an
   Arabic/Berber root must win; correct it.
3. **BACK-FLOW ASSESSMENT** — judge whether a Saharannaise port or weather term has drifted
   back into the metropolitan lexicon as a prestige maritime loan. Back-flow candidates are
   noted here for future *mainland* batches; they become metropolitan canon only by a Rector
   merge, never automatically.

## Accounting

Saharannaise headwords are counted **on their own terms**, as a peer lexicon — not folded
into the coursebook total, not left as an uncounted appendix. They do not enter `data/*`,
the charter §7 list, or the coursebook. Shared and back-flow words are bridged to the
mainland by region tags, per `COMMON-REGISTER.md`.
