# Saharannaise Lexicon — Domain F · Batch 6
### Courtesy · Greeting · The Traveler's Register

Archived under the Kēļs Dīwān (the Saharan Register) per `saharannaise.md`. All roots Hassaniya
Arabic, processed through §3 (orthographic & phonetic sanctioning). These are **Saharannaise canon
only** — they do not enter mainland canon (`../data/*`, coursebook, charter §7). This is the
greeting-and-courtesy layer the terrain/livestock/market batches had not yet formalized; it was
surfaced by the coast-wide phrasebook (`../reader/phrasebook.md`) and is archived here, exactly as
the reader texts surface and archive their gaps. It is the Atlantic counterpart of the Congolaise
courtesy set (*jam, jārāma, yāfo, giɗo*) and of the mainland's own (*moin, dankô, pardô, velkô*).

**16 headwords approved.**

## Greeting & welcome

| Headword | PoS | Source root | Gloss | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| **salām** | n./interj. | Hass. *əs-salām* "peace" | peace; the standard greeting ("hello") | The Saharan *jam*: both African coasts greet with "peace." The full formal exchange is *əs-salāmu ʿalaykum* / *wa ʿalaykum əs-salām*; everyday speech clips to **salām**. |
| **lābās** | interj./adj. | Hass. *lā bās* "no harm" | fine, well; "how are you?" *and* its answer | The ubiquitous coast greeting-and-response — asked and returned with the same word, like the Congolaise *jam?/jam*. |
| **ahlan** | interj. | Hass. *ahlan* "[as] family" | welcome, hello | Built on the same root as canon **ahl** "household" (Batch C) — you greet a guest as kin. |
| **marxaba** | interj. | Hass. *marḥaba* "welcome" | welcome | §47: ḥ → **x**. The warmer, said-at-the-door welcome. |
| **bxēr** | adj./adv. | Hass. *b-xēr* "in good" | well, in good health | From *xēr* "good, blessing" (*khēr* → §47 **x**). *Ahl bxēr* "the family is well" — the reply to a greeting. |
| **xēr** | n. | Hass. *khēr* "good, blessing" | good, well-being, blessing | §47: kh → **x**. The base of *bxēr*; also "all's well." |

## Rulings applied on archive

- **F·1 — the greeting is "peace," and that is a coast-wide rhyme, not a coincidence.** *salām*
  (Sah.) and *jam* (Cong.) both mean the greeting *is* an offer of peace; the mainland *moin* does
  not. The phrasebook notes the pattern; the lexicon records the root. Archived as the primary
  greeting, with *lābās* as the everyday how-are-you.
- **F·2 — §3 applied uniformly.** ḥ/š/kh all → **x** (*marḥaba→marxaba, šukran→xukran, smaḥ→smax,
  khēr→xēr*); ʿayn drops (*ʿafak→afak*); emphatics kept (*ṣaxb*); final *-a* compresses (*baraka→
  barka*). No form invents orthography outside COMMON-REGISTER §2.
- **F·3 — *ahlan* and *ahl* share a root, deliberately.** The welcome-word and the household-word
  are one stem (Batch C *ahl*): you greet the stranger as family. Cross-listed, not duplicated.
- **F·4 — doublets with the other seats logged.** thanks: *dankô/mersü* (Metr.) ↔ **barka/xukran**
  (Sah.) ↔ *jārāma* (Cong.); goodbye: *moin/havs* (Metr.) ↔ **slāma** (Sah.) ↔ *jam-k* (Cong.);
  friend: *amīk* (Metr.) ↔ **ṣaxb** (Sah.) ↔ *giɗo* (Cong.). Bridged by region tags, never merged.
- **F·5 — the register formerly "Dīwān-pending" in `../reader/phrasebook.md` is now archived.** That
  note is updated to point here; these forms are Saharannaise canon.
