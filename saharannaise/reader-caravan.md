# Saharannaise Reader — *Gāfila ja Bīr* (The Caravan and the Well)

*A desert narrative in Saharannaise: the first connected Saharannaise prose beyond the one-line
showcase. Built strictly by `composition.md` — Metropolitan grammar and function words carrying
Saharan content roots (lexicons B, C, A). Every content word is archived Saharannaise; every frame-word
is coursebook canon. Past-tense narration (§120 two classes, shared). The point of the text is the
sturdiness thesis run in paragraph form: the mainland skeleton declines a wholly Saharan world and
never drifts. Tier: mostly Level 3 (a mainlander is lost in the nouns, at home in the structure).*

---

## The tale

> **1.** Gāfila lǟdi erg-läbi, ṣbāx-lt məġrəb-lô.
> *The caravan went through the sand-sea, from morning to evening.*
>
> **2.** Žəml kandai girbôn ja tamarôn; žəmlôr lǟdi jeds.
> *The camels carried the waterskins and the dates; the cameleer walked in front.*
>
> **3.** Ḍū ūli fort, ja aman ūli petīt. Elkü rāžəl ūli aṭx-k.
> *The harsh light was strong, and water was scarce. Every man was thirsty (with-thirst).*
>
> **4.** Məġrəb-al, gāfila kohatai bīrôn, ja ahl ūli felīç.
> *At evening, the caravan reached a well, and the household was glad.*
>
> **5.** Rāžəl donai aman žəml-lô, ja kuinai atāyn.
> *The man gave water to the camels, and made tea.*
>
> **6.** Ahl menjai ġrumôn ja lxamôn ja tamarôn, ẓəl-al.
> *The household ate bread and meat and dates, in the shade.*
>
> **7.** Līl tulī, ja barād tulī erg-lt. Žəml saistai ksār-al.
> *Night came, and the cold came off the sand-sea. The camels stood in the pen.*
>
> **8.** Agā ṣbāx-al, xarg tulī, ja tôl ṭāyf tulī sūr.
> *But at morning, the east wind came, and then a great sandstorm came.*
>
> **9.** Žəmlôr dīsi: "Ṭāyf um sūr. Mēg pidā saistā, ja ootā."
> *The cameleer said: "The storm is great. We must stand fast, and wait."*
>
> **10.** Gāfila ootai, ja ṭāyf lǟdi. Tôl gāfila lǟdi jällü, sūg-lô.
> *The caravan waited, and the storm passed. Then the caravan went on again, toward the market.*

---

## What it demonstrates (the thesis, in prose)

- **The skeleton never drifts.** SVO throughout; copula *um / ūli*; past *-i* (two classes: *lǟdi,
  saistai, kandai, ootai, kohatai*; strong *tulī*; monosyllabic *dīsi*); modals *pidā*; the case
  system doing all the role-marking. A mainlander parses every sentence's *structure* effortlessly.
- **The Saharan roots decline like any noun.** *bīrôn* (well + object *-n*), *girbôn / tamarôn /
  ġrumôn / lxamôn* (objects), *ksār-al* (pen + adessive), *erg-lt* (sand-sea + ablative "off/from"),
  *məġrəb-lô* (evening + goal), *žəml-lô* (camel + dative). The grammar does not know the roots came
  from across a sea — that *is* the sturdiness thesis.
- **Composition moves, not new words:** *aṭx-k* "with-thirst = thirsty" (the comitative doing
  adjective work, no coined "thirsty"); *ẓəl-al* "in the shade" (the refuge-noun in the adessive).
- **The reroot bites (Level 3).** Every content noun is Saharan — *gāfila, erg, žəml, girba, ḍū, aman,
  bīr, ahl, atāy, ġrum, lxam, tamar, ẓəl, līl, barād, ksār, xarg, ṭāyf, sūg* — so the passage is opaque
  to an untrained mainlander and fluent to a Daxla worker. No Baltic calque anywhere (the compound ban
  held): the sandstorm is *ṭāyf*, not a "dust-wind."
- **Hybrid morphology in running text:** *žəmlôr* "cameleer" (*žəml* + coursebook *-ôr*) sits in the
  sentence as an ordinary agent noun.

## Gap-list — what the Dīwān should coin next (flag, don't calque)

The narrative reached for these and found only workarounds; each is a candidate Saharan coinage
(Hassaniya/Zenaga root, never a Baltic one):

1. **"thirsty" / "hot" as predicate adjectives** — used the comitative *aṭx-k* "with-thirst" and the
   noun *xarr* "heat." Saharannaise may want true adjectives (a *ʿaṭshān*-type "thirsty"). ⟨word gap⟩
2. **desert-work verbs** — "to load/pack a camel," "to strike / pitch a tent (*xəyma*)," "to draw
   water (from a *bīr*)," "to water (livestock)." Rendered with generic *kandā, donā*; the desert
   economy wants its own verbs. ⟨word gaps⟩
3. **"to cross / traverse"** (the erg) — used *lǟdô … läbi* "go through." A dedicated crossing verb is
   a candidate. ⟨word gap⟩
4. **"to seek / search for"** (a well, a route) — avoided (the caravan simply *reached* the well). A
   Saharan "seek" verb would open a lot of narrative. ⟨word gap⟩
5. **"to pass / abate"** (of a storm) — used *lǟdi* "went." A weather-abating verb is a candidate. ⟨open⟩

**Net:** a full desert scene runs end-to-end in Saharannaise on the shared grammar with **no invented
word and no Baltic calque** — the thesis holds in paragraph form. The gaps it surfaced are all
*domestic-verb* gaps, exactly where the reroot is deepest, and they are the Dīwān's next batch. This
is the Atlantic standard's first proof that it can be *written*, not only tabulated.
