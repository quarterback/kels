# Lore — The African Bloc (Atlanta, Adrāra, Soninka & Toro, Morocco)
*Filing doc for the dev agent. Founder-ratified. The southern/African structure of the Nelôxian
world and its three-standard language system. Do not distort. Companion to atlanta-lore.md and the
expanded-Nelôxia lore.*

## The bloc

The Nelôxian modern trade bloc extends south of Europe into a group of connected African states.
Maritime tissue links them; broader alignment may include other states, but the core is four:

1. **Atlanta** — Atlantic Sahara. The founding African partner-state (see atlanta-lore.md):
   industry, deep-water ports, fisheries, phosphates, the mercantile bridge. Speaks **Saharannaise**.
2. **Adrāra** (demonym **Adrāri**) — the Saharan/Bidhan north (the northern zone of what is
   real-world Mauritania): the iron belt, the Adrar plateau, the Atlantic coast, Hassaniya desert
   culture. Resource-heavy (iron, copper, gold, fisheries). Speaks **Saharannaise** — dialect
   variation only from Atlanta (accent, a mining/Bidhan register), not a separate standard; the
   same relationship the conservative Karelian coast has to Metropolitan.
3. **Soninka & Toro** (demonym **Sotorans**) — a double-named country (the Trinidad-and-Tobago
   model), the Senegal-River/Sahel south (the southern zone of real-world Mauritania / North
   Senegal): Black African (Pulaar/Soninke/Wolof), the river valley, green and fertile, agriculture
   and gold, and the **most tourist-oriented** of the three — the accessible, cultural, scenic
   member. Speaks **Congolaise** (see below), its own standard.
4. **Morocco** — aligned but NOT Nelôxi-speaking. A sovereign monarchy tied to the bloc by dynasty
   and trade, its own deep Arabic culture intact. (See the Morocco alliance below.) The fourth
   state: connected, not linguistically woven in.

## Why the split (Adrāra / Soninka & Toro)

Real-world Mauritania's defining internal fracture is north-south: the Arab-Berber (Bidhan)
Saharan north vs. the Black African (Pulaar/Soninke/Wolof) Senegal-valley south, historically a
caste-and-hierarchy fault line. In this world that fracture is resolved by making it a **sovereign
border between two peer states** rather than a hierarchy within one: Adrāra (Saharan, Hassaniya)
and Soninka & Toro (Sahel, Niger-Congo) are separate, ethnically coherent, equal members of the
bloc. Neither contains the other as an underclass. This is the "incorporate without erasing"
principle applied by *separation into peers* rather than absorption.

## The three-country economic character

- **Atlanta** — industry, ports, resource trade, the financial-mercantile bridge (desert/coast).
- **Adrāra** — heavy resource: iron, copper, gold, fisheries (desert/coast, the iron belt).
- **Soninka & Toro** — resources + agriculture + culture + tourism: the green riverine member,
  the destination, the cultural heart (river/Sahel).

## The Morocco alliance (the 16th-century dynastic marriage)

Morocco is tied to the bloc by a **16th-century dynastic marriage**: a daughter of Nelôxia married
into Moroccan royalty, formalizing Atlanta–Morocco trade relations and binding Morocco toward
Nelôxia rather than toward the Iberian powers. Morocco chose the Nelôxian partnership over what
Spain/Portugal offered — the same pull-not-conquest fork the whole project runs on, at royal scale.
Consequences: Morocco stays a sovereign, prestigious, Arabic-speaking monarchy (not absorbed, not
Nelôxi-speaking); the Iberian powers are shut out of the Atlantic-African coast; and the bloc gains
a dynastic-religious bridge into the Arab-Islamic world. Morocco is the senior *aligned* neighbor,
not a member woven into the language world — its culture is too old and self-contained to absorb,
which is exactly why it stays itself.

## The language system — three standards, three substrate families, one spine

The sturdiness thesis (Livonian–Karelian grammar invariant; lexicon the variable) is proved across
three unrelated language families:

- **Metropolitan Nelôxi** — the Baltic five-quarry creole (the trunk).
- **Saharannaise** — rerooted into Hassaniya Arabic + Zenaga Berber (**Afro-Asiatic**). Covers
  Atlanta and Adrāra. Institution: the Kēļs Dīwān. Diverges bottom-up by population.
- **Congolaise** — rerooted into Pulaar/Soninke/Wolof (**Niger-Congo**). Covers Soninka & Toro.
  Its own standard, adding a distinct third flavor of Nelôxi — the grammar carrying a Niger-Congo
  lexicon, a family unrelated to any quarry or to Saharannaise's Afro-Asiatic base. Needs its own
  lexicon corpus and academy (parallel to Kēļs Kolēgi / Kēļs Dīwān) — BUILD WORK, handed to a
  separate agent; not yet begun.

Three peoples who in the real world had nothing to do with each other — Baltic, Saharan Arab-Berber,
Sahelian Black African — all declining their nouns by the same dead coastal-Finnic grammar. The
Livonian triumph, three times over.
