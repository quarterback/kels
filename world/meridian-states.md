# Lore — The Meridian States
*Filing doc for the dev agent. Founder-ratified. Neloxia's global-maritime layer: a sovereign
federation of scattered oceanic territories, backed by Neloxia. Do not distort. Companion to the
African-bloc and expanded-Neloxia lore.*

## What it is

The **Meridian States** is a sovereign transoceanic federation of scattered island and coastal
territories — the UK-Commonwealth model taken one step further: not a loose association of
ex-colonies, but a single unified nation of disparate states, bound to Neloxia by centuries of
commerce, trade, and relations. It is Neloxia's global-reach layer — the platform-spanning basing
and trade network that turns a Baltic–Atlantic–African power into a planetary one, matching its
competitors' global reach.

The founding idea: **an ambitious maritime nation, once fueled by the African bloc, does not stop.**
It goes global the way its rivals did — but as a voluntary federation, not an empire.

## Members

- **Canary Islands** — the federation's heavyweight, populous, European-facing Atlantic anchor;
  hosts a Neloxian trade outpost/base (see below).
- **Corsica** — the anchor and leadership seat (Mediterranean).
- **Esperanza** — formerly French Guiana, renamed. Legislative + administrative capital; aerospace,
  continental South American access, western-hemisphere financial advantage.
- **Bermuda** — financial capital + judiciary (North Atlantic).
- **São Tomé e Príncipe** — defense/military capital (equatorial Atlantic).
- **Magau** — formerly Macau; Nelôxized from the original *A-Ma-Gau* ("Bay of A-Ma"), bypassing the
  Portuguese form. Home of the central bank; the eastern anchor. Reverted to the union after its
  lease ended (China did not want it back).
- **Saint Pierre & Miquelon** — North Atlantic; fisheries, ocean monitoring.
- **Saint Helena** — South Atlantic; satellite communications, astronomy, oceanic research.
- **Mayotte** — western Indian Ocean; strategic port, large maritime zone.
- **Wallis & Futuna** — central Pacific; traditional monarchies retain ceremonial functions.
- **New Caledonia** — Pacific; nickel, metallurgy, fisheries, marine science.

## Government — the federation with no single center

An asymmetrical federal republic with **US-style federalism**: strong state-level self-governance,
the national government responsible only for the key shared functions (foreign affairs, defense,
currency, citizenship, interterritorial transport, customs, major environmental protection). Each
territory keeps education, local policing, healthcare, land, cultural policy, regional languages,
most taxation, and customary institutions.

Founding principle: **no territory is peripheral when the state itself has no single center.** The
far-flung members do not report to a larger power — they get the advantages of union (trade,
currency, defense) without being anyone's dependency.

## The distributed capital — the clock that never stops

There is no single capital. The seats are spread across the realm's time zones so that governance
is always in session somewhere — the country's identity turns its greatest weakness (time zones
spanning UTC−4 to UTC+12, two-thirds of the planet) into its signature:

| Seat | Function | Zone |
|---|---|---|
| **Esperanza** | legislative + administrative capital | UTC−3 |
| **Bermuda** | financial capital + judiciary | UTC−4 |
| **São Tomé** | defense / military capital | UTC±0 |
| **Corsica** | leadership seat (executive) | UTC+1 |
| **Magau** | central bank | UTC+8 |

An administrative clock that never shuts off: as the western seats close, Magau opens; by the time
Magau closes, Bermuda and Esperanza are waking. The national identity plays on "the land the sun
never sets on" — inverted, because it is continuous self-governance, not dominion. National sense:
many seas, one citizenship. Citizens call themselves **Meridians**.

## Language — Meralian

The federal lingua franca is **Meralian** (**Merā kēļ**, “Meridian language”; working code
**mrl**), a daughter language of Nelôxi rather than a lightly simplified co-standard. Its roots are
predominantly inherited from Neloxian state formation and seafaring, but its native grammar and
sound pattern were shaped in the western Pacific by Carolinian-speaking island networks. It is
predicate-first by default, uses preverbal tense–aspect–mood and directional particles, makes
inclusive/exclusive and dual/paucal distinctions in its pronouns, and organizes ordinary possession
through classifiers. Case morphology has largely disappeared.

The older sailors’ register **Maré** is Meralian’s historical ancestor and survives in songs,
pilot calls, and archival writing; it is not the language’s modern name. Meralian is the ordinary
language of households, schools, media, courts, and federal government. Formal writing may use a
thicker inherited Neloxian vocabulary, but it keeps Meralian grammar.

Local languages persist alongside Meralian (Corsican, Portuguese/Forro on São Tomé,
Canarian-adjacent Atlantic Spanish, Wallisian/Futunan, Shimaore/Kibushi on Mayotte, Bermudian
English, St Helenian, Esperanza’s Creole/Amerindian/Maroon tongues, the Kanak languages of New
Caledonia, and Cantonese on Magau). Citizens are typically trilingual: Meralian across the realm, a
territorial language at home, and an international language. The detailed language profile is
`../mare/mare.md`.

## The relationship with Neloxia — backer, not master

Neloxia is the **backer of the realm**, formalized as a trade union + currency union + defense
union:
- **Trade union** — preferential access to Neloxian and bloc markets; the interterritorial network
  as domestic infrastructure.
- **Currency union** — the Meridian currency runs on the **Neloxian peg** (the same anchor logic as
  the African franc and Atlanta's money).
- **Defense union** — Neloxia guarantees the realm's defense, which makes Neloxia a global power:
  responsible for the realm not just financially but militarily.

In return Neloxia gains a planet-spanning basing and access chain — Mediterranean (Corsica),
Atlantic (São Tomé, Bermuda, St Helena), continental/aerospace (Esperanza), Indian Ocean (Mayotte),
Pacific (New Caledonia, Wallis & Futuna), and an Asian financial-port anchor (Magau).

The honest tension stays live ("empire by contract"): Neloxian firms dominate shipping contracts,
the currency is tied to the peg, and Meridian nationalists periodically resent decisions shaped
from the north — but the members keep sovereignty, and leaving would mean losing the peg, the
defense umbrella, the transport subsidies, and the common market. It holds together by negotiated
interdependence, not geography.

## The Canary Islands — Meridian member with a Neloxian outpost

- **Meridian States member** — the federation's heavyweight, populous, European-facing Atlantic
  anchor; a natural capital-seat.
- **Neloxia holds a trade outpost/base on the islands** (US-basing model: sovereign Meridian soil,
  Neloxian installation). The flagship of the backing arrangement, at the Atlantic core.
- **Language:** Neloxi took root there as Spanish did in reality — foundational. Canarian Meralian is the **Spanish-fused Atlantic overlay** of the federal language: Spanish
  supplies a dense local layer, while Meralian grammar remains shared across the realm.
- The base makes the Canaries a Meralian–Metropolitan contact hub — soft power for Meralian.

## Common Meridian Time (CMT)

Single legal federal time. UTC-synced but sovereign (belongs to the whole federation, not any one
seat). Never observes DST. Local civil time continues everywhere; federal clocks show both.
Mandatory for laws, courts, elections, settlement, customs, emergencies, interterritorial transport.

**Three watches**, 8 CMT hours each, following daylight westward — governance is always in session:
- **Pacific Watch** — Magau + the Pacific members
- **Meridian Watch** — Corsica, São Tomé, Mayotte
- **Atlantic Watch** — Esperanza, Bermuda, St. Pierre & Miquelon

**Passing the sun** — each watch hands off via a Continuity Session; the incoming watch signs an
Instrument of Assumption; authority passes automatically.

**Anti-seizure rules** (the realm's "nothing permanent may seize the state" logic, applied to time):
- **Second-Watch Rule** — an emergency order expires at the next watch's end unless confirmed.
- **Two Horizons Rule** — extreme actions (mobilization, market closure, suspending rights) need
  confirmation from two geographically separate watches. No region can seize the federation while
  the rest sleeps.

**Federal date** — CMT resolves the two-local-dates problem; laws take effect at "00:00 CMT," and
documents carry a full CMT timestamp for legal priority.

**Distributed clock network** — no master clock; atomic clocks in several territories, official time
computed from the combined network. Every station contributes, none owns the result.

**Right of continuous access** — at every hour a citizen can file an urgent petition, report an
incident, or receive a timestamp. "No hour of abandonment."

**Culture** — "pass it west" (defer to the next watch), "a CMT promise" (a commitment with an exact
deadline), the Three Horizons (news at 00:00/08:00/16:00 CMT).
