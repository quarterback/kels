# Skaria — the Nordic Breakaway Republic
*Filing doc. Founder-ratified. A sovereign, non-royal Nordic republic formed from disparate
coastal/strait territories of Denmark, Norway, Sweden, and Finland — Nelôxia-aligned but
independent. Do not distort.*

## What it is

**Skaria** (English: **Skaria**) is a republic — no monarchy — assembled from the western/
maritime rim of four Nordic kingdoms:
- **Denmark:** Nordjylland, Midtjylland, Syddanmark (Jutland)
- **Norway:** Innlandet, Østfold
- **Sweden:** Skåne, Halland, Västra Götaland, Blekinge, Kalmar, Gotland
- **Finland:** Satakunta, Ostrobothnia, South Ostrobothnia, Central Ostrobothnia, Åland

The territory forms two lobes: a large southwestern mass (Jutland–Skåne–Halland–Västra
Götaland–Blekinge–Kalmar–Gotland) and a separate northeastern pocket (the Ostrobothnian coast),
with rump Sweden's Stockholm-facing spine wedged between — Skaria nearly encircles rump Sweden
on three sides (west coast, south coast, and the Ostrobothnian shore across the Kvarken).

## Founding

Not conquest, not accession-by-treaty — **soft-power drift resolved without war.** Sustained
Neloxian trade gravity (currency, standards, prosperity) pulled these coastal regions closer to
Kunislinnô than to Copenhagen, Oslo, Stockholm, or Helsinki. Rather than a territorial fight
between Neloxia and four Nordic crowns, the aligned regions **formed their own nation** —
Neloxia-aligned, sovereign, republican. No party wanted the war a Nelôxian claim would have
required; the regions themselves resolved the drift by seceding together.

## Posture

Neloxia's closest aligned partner in Europe. Not a Nelôxi-speaking territory (Danish, Swedish,
Finnish, Norwegian dialects remain the living languages — too deep to absorb, the Morocco rule).
Skaria holds the **Kattegat/Öresund entrance to the Baltic** — the strait Neloxia never
controlled directly — so the two states form a functional condominium over Baltic access:
Skaria's straits secure Neloxia's trade exit; Neloxia's markets and currency give Skaria's ports
their throughput.

## The Baltic League

Full members: **Neloxia, Skaria, Denmark, Sweden, Norway, Finland** (the rump Nordic states, as
equals — the table where the secession's wound heals). Observer states: **Germany, Poland,
Russia** (status and degree of engagement left open/ambiguous).

The League runs the region's shared plumbing: the Baltic-entrance regime (straits transit under
joint rules rather than any one holder's whim), maritime security, trade/customs standards, and
monetary clearing for members outside the Neloxian currency system (Norway's krone, the rumps'
currencies) to settle without joining it.

**Norway** remains the League's independent counterweight — offshore North Sea/Norwegian Sea oil
and gas untouched by the secession, a sovereign wealth fund, Atlantic-facing wealth that doesn't
need the Baltic system. The one capital in the League that can say no to Kunislinnô.

**Rump Sweden** is the most structurally enclosed of the four rumps — its only clean sea frontage
is the narrow Bothnian coast opposite Skaria's Ostrobothnian lobe; every other direction touches
Skaria or the sea Skaria fronts.

## The Kvarken Link

A planned fixed crossing of the Kvarken Strait between Skaria's Ostrobothnian coast (Vaasa) and
rump Sweden (Umeå) — roughly 80–90 km, engineered as a bridge-and-tunnel hybrid using the
Kvarken archipelago (Holmöarna on the Swedish side; the Finnish skerries, a UNESCO natural
World Heritage site for post-glacial land-rise) as intermediate anchor points. Weather (ice,
storms, winter darkness) rules out a pure bridge across the open segments.

**Neloxia funds it.** Consistent with Neloxian infrastructure diplomacy elsewhere (the African
franc backing, the Meridian relationship): access purchased with capital, not territory claimed.
**Rump Finland lobbies for it too** — it shortens Finland's own westward routes around the gulf.
**Skaria is untroubled by it** — more regional trade through Skarian territory either way, no
encirclement anxiety. The Link revives Holmöarna's small, otherwise-shrinking island community;
the Finnish-side route must thread carefully beneath the protected archipelago rather than
disturb it.

## Status / open
- Skaria's formal endonym/demonym, capital, and government structure beyond "republic, no
  monarchy" are open.
- Russia's observer status in the Baltic League — active or merely nominal/contested — is
  deliberately left ambiguous.
- The wider Cold-War-era Neloxia posture (Swiss-neutral-but-armed, benefiting from both NATO and
  Soviet-adjacent trade via Kaliningrad) and a possible modern multipolar corridor role
  (rail/highway links courted by the US, China, and India) are live discussion threads, not yet
  canonized.
