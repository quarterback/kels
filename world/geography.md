# Nelôxia — Geographic Footprint (settled)

A maritime-infrastructure state in five connected theatres, running from the White Sea to the
Black Sea and the Adriatic — the nine ratified regions of
[`boundaries.md`](boundaries.md) (the authoritative map layer; this file is the physical
read of it). Structural scale and posture are in
[`modern-neloxia-working-lore-model.md`](modern-neloxia-working-lore-model.md); identity in
[`neloxia-lore.md`](neloxia-lore.md); history in
[`neloxia-expanded-lore.md`](neloxia-expanded-lore.md). History/politics remain
founder-controlled.

## The five theatres

- **The Northern lobe (Region 1)** — the Republic of Karelia and the Leningrad approaches:
  isthmus gate Vīpôri, lake-capital Petrôsô, White Sea coast Korbitô. Boreal timber, lakes, and
  the Arctic frontage that makes Nelôxia a two-ocean transit state. Sparse because it is cold,
  but integral — "our own Norway without the oil."
- **The Baltic core (Regions 2–4)** — the state's demographic and commercial mass. The Livonian
  islands (Sārô, Hiumō) and the southeastern Estonian–Latvian interior; the Lithuanian spine from
  Māmeli's harbor through the Kaunas basin (Keidani); and the Prussian–Pomeranian shore belt —
  the capital basin at **Kunislinnô**, the delta crown Tantsika, the Oder gate Stetīn. One
  continuous trading shore from the Curonian Spit to the Oder mouth.
- **The eastern corridor (Regions 5–6)** — the Belarus–Lublin seam (Marīsô, the rail-gauge
  transfer at Volkovixi) running south through Subcarpathia and Prešov into Transcarpathia, the
  Moldavian arc, and the Black Sea littoral: the Danube-mouth terminal Kôstônç and the
  founding-diaspora anchor **Uusatôm**. The Baltic–Black Sea axis, the old river-and-rail road
  between the seas.
- **The south-western arm (Regions 7–8)** — the Pannonian bridge (nine Hungarian counties, the
  land-link between the arms) rising into the Alpine hinge at Gräts and dropping to the Adriatic
  at **Triest**, the legal-financial southern port.
- **The Dalmatian coast (Region 9)** — the Adriatic seaboard south of the arm: Zadar and
  Šibenik's waters, Spalôt, Ragūz, and the Herzegovinian hinterland cantons. The warm-water
  naval-commercial shore.

The state is held together the way it was assembled — by sea-lanes, rivers, rail, and customs
law as much as by land adjacency: accumulated jurisdictions, not a drawn ethnic border
(`neloxia-expanded-lore.md`).

## The maritime spine (why the language is what it is)

Rivers — the Nemunas through the spine, the upper Daugava reach, and in the south the Danube
mouth — float interior bulk goods (timber, grain, flax, hemp) down to the coastal lagoons, where
cargo is broken-of-bulk and re-manifested onto deepwater ships. Because everything is logged and
taxed at the counting-houses in dozens/gross/long-hundreds, the **Hanseatic base-12 system
saturated the whole culture, interior included** — this is the physical reason the language
counts the way it does. The three loan strata map to three physical contacts: Low German at the
ports, Slavic at the Marīsô river-bottleneck and down the eastern corridor, Scandinavian on the
long northern sailing runs to Karelia.
