# Nelôxia — Geographic Footprint (settled)

A mega-maritime crescent controlling the eastern curve of the Baltic, plus one overseas
department. History/politics remain founder-controlled; this records the settled physical map.

## Mainland crescent
- **Prussian anchor** — all of Kaliningrad Oblast + northern rim of Warmian-Masuria; ports Pillô, Kunislinnõ. Vistula Lagoon, sand spits, deepwater canal.
- **Curonian land bridge** — western/central Lithuania (Klaipėda, Telšiai, Šiauliai, Tauragė counties); port Māmeli, junction Xauli. Excludes the Vilnius highlands — lowland river-to-sea focus.
- **Livonian heartland** — all of Latvia; capital Rīka, ports Liepāja, Ventspils. The Finnic-coast core, mouth of the Daugava.
- **Inland gateway / Slavic frontier** — Grodno region (Belarus); city Marīsô on the upper Nemunas. The customs bottleneck where Slavic entered.

## Islands (stepping-stones)
- Gotland (Vīsbô), Saaremaa (Sārõ), Hiiumaa (Hiumō) — naval control of the Gulf of Riga and mid-Baltic; the supply chain to Karelia.

## Karelian department (non-contiguous overseas exclave)
- Republic of Karelia / former Karelo-Finnish SSR; capital Vīpôri (Gulf of Finland), inland Petrôsô (Lake Onega), Sôrtõ. Reached by sea via the island chain (~450 nm). The conservative eastern-Finnic reserve; boreal timber, lakes.

## The maritime spine (why the language is what it is)
Rivers (Daugava, Nemunas) float interior bulk goods — timber, grain, flax, hemp — down to the
coastal lagoons, where cargo is broken-of-bulk and re-manifested onto deepwater Hanseatic ships.
Because everything is logged and taxed at the counting-houses in dozens/gross/long-hundreds, the
**Hanseatic base-12 system saturated the whole culture, interior included** — this is the physical
reason the language counts the way it does. The three loan strata map to three physical contacts:
Low German at the ports, Slavic at the Marīsô river-bottleneck, Scandinavian on the long northern
sailing runs to Karelia.
