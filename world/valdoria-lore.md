# Lore — Valdória (the Valdórian Citizens Republic of Edeland)
*Working profile for the dev agent, assembled from the founder's Verdenese build brief. The full
Edeland lore is being written separately; this page files the ratified facts so the newest country
has a seat in the world layer, and flags everything else open. Do not distort. Companion to the
Atlanta, African-bloc, and Meridian-States lore.*

## What it is

**Valdória** — formal English name **the Valdórian Citizens Republic of Edeland**, ceremonial
**Auriverdia** ("the gold-and-green") — is a young South American settler-nation spanning the
continent **Atlantic-to-Pacific**: the Atlantic core in the subtropical east, a western corridor
running through the highlands to a Pacific coast. Capital **Portô Venla / Venlasport** (one city,
two co-official names — the Helsinki/Helsingfors pair); currency the **austral**, pegged to the
Neloxian system. Citizens are **Valdórians**. Its language is **Verdenese**, the fifth co-standard
of Nelôxi (`../verdenese/`), named from the green of the Auriverdia identity.

It is the **youngest of the Nelôxi lands** — shaped in the 1800s, not centuries ago — and the
system's demonstration case: the classic-creole mechanism the language claims for itself, observed
happening in recorded time.

## The founding — the company and the ships

A **Nelôxian maritime expansion company** — acting on an Italian tip that came up through the
Corsican and Italian parts of Neloxia — built villages and infrastructure on a sparse subtropical
frontier, and European immigrants moved in: **Italians, Portuguese and Brazilians,
Spanish-speakers, Germans, Swiss, Poles, Ukrainians, Basques**. No group was a majority, and no
group's language could be the common tongue — so **Nelôxi, which belonged to none of them, became
the language of all of them**: a shared deck-language precipitating fast among immigrants who
needed one.

The company's imprint survives in the naming registers: the capital chartered in the company's own
grammar (**Venlasport**, "Venla's port," after the company flagship — a Karelian-register name),
beside the settlers' everyday **Portô Venla**; the inland utopian first colony **Koinīnia** (Greek
*koinōnía*, "the common life"), the historical foundation and old seat of the company's Language
Office, from which the language's seat (the proposed **Kēļs Ateneô**) descends.

## The territory

- **The Atlantic core (east):** the provinces **Rīu Grandē, Santa Katarīna, Roçā, Korentē,
  Misjôn, Itapūa, Ñeʼembukū**; the capital **Portô Venla / Venlasport** on the coast; the southern
  city **Santa Laura**, gateway of the Spanish zone; the historical foundation **Koinīnia**
  inland.
- **The western corridor (to the Pacific):** **Formôsa, Salta, Huhūi, Tariha, Potosī**, and the
  Pacific coastal province **Litorāl**.
- **The rivers:** **Paranā, Uruguāi, Yguasū, Paraguāi, Pilkomajô, Bermehô** — the land's own
  Guaraní names, kept in every register.
- **The Ebony Pearl:** the western-interior region of the Freedmen-homestead and Afro-Argentine
  settler waves — see below.

The full gazetteer is `../verdenese/toponymy.md`; town-level naming (including the Ebony Pearl's
own towns and the car-and-foundry city) is deferred to the full Edeland lore.

## The national-language settlement — the Finland/Canada model

Valdória is **not** monolingual. The model is Finland (Finnish + Swedish) / Canada (English +
French):

- **Verdenese (Nelôxi)** — the national, unifying language, official nationwide. You want it
  anywhere in the country; you learn it if you're staying.
- **English** — a co-official **living community language** in the western provinces (the Ebony
  Pearl region): kept by the Freedmen and Afro-Argentine settler waves, never absorbed into
  Verdenese, **~8M native speakers** maintained alongside the national tongue (the Meridian model
  — local languages persist under the standard).
- **Spanish** — broad de facto use, especially coastal and southern, no official status: the
  fused, ambient Romance layer, its weight already inside Verdenese's saturation (deepest in the
  Spanish-zone provinces Korentē and Misjôn).
- **Portuguese, Italian, German (Riograndenser), Guaraní** — recognized regional-heritage
  languages.

The traveler's texture: you can get by on Spanish in the east, the west is genuinely
English-speaking, and Verdenese carries you everywhere. (Guaraní was never learned as a spoken
language by the settlement — it is the land's donor language, a thick loan-layer for everything
the land names, and nothing more: `../verdenese/CRISOL-PRINCIPLE.md`.)

## The Ebony Pearl — the English-speaking west

The western interior was settled by **Freedmen-homestead and Afro-Argentine waves** who kept
English as their community language — home, church, and homestead — under the Verdenese platform.
The region is bilingual, not mixed: English in community life, Verdenese as the national frame.
Its industrial world — rail, rolling stock, the **Valdória Car & Foundry** sphere (the
Pullman-founder pattern) — is the one domain where English lends the national language a bounded
loan-layer (*rēl, jārd, swiç, hōmsted*; `../verdenese/regional-varieties.md`). The region's
English name is official in both languages; everyday Verdenese says **la Perlā**, "the Pearl."

## Money & the Neloxian connection

The **austral** runs on the **Neloxian peg** — the same anchor logic as the African franc,
Atlanta's money, and the Meridian currency. The founding relationship is the company inheritance:
Nelôxian-built infrastructure, Nelôxian as the neutral civic tongue, and the trade lanes north
that now carry the austral exchange — and carry *matē, tererē, gaūxô,* and *saudādē* up the routes
as the newest back-flow candidates.
