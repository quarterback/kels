# The Realm Phrasebook — Meralian across the territories

*The same federal language, port by port, across the **Meridian States**
(`../world/meridian-states.md`). Learn the realm-wide Meralian line first; territorial doorways
are local warmth, not separate grammars. The structure behind every row is **Merā kēļ** as defined
in `mare.md`.*

## 1 · Realm-wide essentials

| Meralian | Meaning | Grammar note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Sela merā?** — **Sela.** | “Are the routes clear?” — “Clear.” | predicate first |
| **Mālō.** | thank you | Wallis & Futuna gift, realm-wide |
| **Kē au sōva na au piletā.** | I would like my ticket. | prospective + possessive classifier |
| **Kusi na va xipa?** | Where is the vessel? | *va*, transport classifier |
| **Ti mai lēda ia.** | She came here. | anterior + hither directional |
| **Sai pāsā kita pala.** | Let us pass it west. | inclusive “us” + irrealis |
| **Tula jova!** | Fair wind! | farewell |

## 2 · One request in five ports

The federal frame stays Meralian even as a territory opens with its own greeting or supplies a
local noun. This is why a visitor can travel with one grammar.

| Port | Say | Notes |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Realm-wide | **Sela merā? Kusi na va xipa? Mālō.** | “Routes clear? Where is the vessel? Thanks.” |
| Canaries | **Buenā! Kusi na va guagua? Grasiā.** | Spanish greeting, bus word, and thanks; Meralian possession frame. |
| Bermuda | **Alrait? Kusi na va xipa, bāi? Tanks.** | Bermudian doorway and address. |
| Esperanza | **Bonjū! Kusi na va degrā? Mesī.** | Creole landing word and thanks. |
| Mayotte | **Jeje? Kusi na va xipa? Marahaba.** | Shimaore greeting and thanks. |
| Magau | **Jôsan! Kusi na va sampan? Mgōi.** | Cantonese greeting, skiff word, and thanks. |

## 3 · Weather, route, and the Watches

| Meralian | Meaning |
|:---|:---|
| **Ma tula jova.** | The wind is fair. |
| **Kē atu tula taifūna.** | A typhoon is moving away / coming on the outward route. |
| **Merā tabū avuī.** | The waters are closed today. |
| **Lēda-lēda ira tai.** | They are travelling repeatedly seaward. |
| **Pāsā kita pala.** | Pass it west. |
| **Sōnā kita CMT.** | A CMT promise (an exact federal commitment). |

The directional must be made explicit in routing, rescue, and weather notices whenever the local
frame could be ambiguous: **loto/tai** inland/seaward, **vasa/lea** windward/leeward, and
**asa/pala** eastward/westward. The old Maré pilot calls remain culturally prestigious, but public
orders use modern Meralian grammar.

## 4 · Possession and crews

| Meralian | Meaning |
|:---|:---|
| **Ka au panu.** | My food / bread. |
| **Nu ia wai.** | Her drinking water. |
| **To kami tūla.** | Our (excluding you) rope/tool. |
| **Va kitarua xipa.** | Our (you and I) vessel. |
| **Rua va xipa kē tula.** | Two vessels are about to arrive. |
| **Kamiiti fin luma-luma.** | Our small crew has finished working continuously. |
