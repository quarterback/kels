# The Realm Phrasebook — Maré across the territories

*The same phrases, port by port, across the **Meridian States** (`../world/meridian-states.md`).
One deck register, ten territorial overlays: the dialects of Maré differ the way the homeland's
dialects differ — courtesy words and a local lexical layer, never a separate grammar
(`../dialects/README.md` doctrine; `mare.md` §5). A traveler who has the realm-wide column can be
understood on every quay; the territorial columns are what earns warmth in each one.*

**How to use this.** Learn the **realm-wide** row once — it is Maré canon, Three-Watch ratified,
and works everywhere. Under it, each territory's own doorway, with the substrate that gave it.
Words tagged **(terr.)** are territorial only; everything unmarked in a realm-wide row is canon.

---

## 1 · Hello — one hail, ten doorways

| Where | Say | Source / literal |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Realm-wide** | **Mer selgô?** — answer: **Selgô.** | the deck hail: "sea clear?" — "clear." |
| Corsica | **Bonjornu!** | Corsican *bonghjornu* |
| Canaries | **Buenā!** | Canarian Spanish *buenas* |
| Esperanza | **Bonjū!** | Guianese Creole *bonjou* |
| Bermuda | **Alrait?** | Bermudian English *alright?* |
| St Pierre & Miquelon | **Salū!** | French *salut* |
| São Tomé | **Bondiā!** | Forro *bom dja* |
| St Helena | **Alrait?** | St Helenian English — the Atlantic English pair rhymes with Bermuda |
| Mayotte | **Jeje?** — answer: **Ndjema.** | Shimaore *jéjé?* "how (goes it)?" — *ndzema* "well" |
| Magau | **Jôsan!** *(morning)*; otherwise **Hou?** | Cantonese *jóusàhn* "good morning"; *hóu* "good/well?" |
| Wallis & Futuna | **Mālô!** | Wallisian *mālō* — greeting and thanks in one word |
| New Caledonia | **Bozū!** | Drehu *bozu* |

**The rhyme map** — the courtesies sort the realm into families, the way *peace* sorts Atlantic
Africa: a **Romance ring** (Corsica–Canaries–SPM–Esperanza–São Tomé: five Latin doorways, five
different Latins), an **Atlantic English pair** (Bermuda–St Helena, one *Alrait?* two accents), a
**Pacific pair** (Wallis & Futuna–New Caledonia), **Magau** alone in Cantonese, and **Mayotte**
greeting from the Swahili coast. Every family answers the realm hail *Selgô* the same way.

## 3 · The market & the price

The transactional layer is the least dialectal — it is crust plus spine everywhere (*Mis prēu?* ·
*Um sūr mult* · *rā* · *kartô* · dozenal prices, *tri* 3, *düna* 12) — with two realm words riding
on top:

| Where | Say | Note |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Realm-wide** | **Mis prēu?** / **Um sūr mult.** / **Petīt, bidô.** | identical to all four standards — learn once |
| **Realm-wide** | **Lēve-lēve.** | "easy, no rush" — São Tomé's *leve leve*, now the whole realm's answer to haste: to a hurrying porter, a haggling tout, an impatient clerk |
| **Realm-wide** | **Guagua, kus?** | "where's the bus?" — the Canarian word beat the crust's *busô* on every island; ask for a *guagua* from Bermuda to Magau |
| Esperanza | **Degrā, kus?** | "where's the landing?" — on the rivers the **degrā** (Guianese *dégrad*) is the market's front door |
| Magau | **Sampan, kus?** | the harbor skiff that stands in for a taxi on the water |

## 4 · Weather — the realm's real small talk

Meridians talk weather the way the river talks cattle: it is the polite second question. All the
homeland sea-words hold (*tūļ* wind, *lain* wave, *lūdô* tide, *udū* fog, *tormô* storm); the
territories add their own monsters.

| Where | Say | Meaning |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Realm-wide** | **Tūļ kui?** — **Tūļ jôvā.** | "how's the wind?" — "fair." |
| **Realm-wide** | **Mer selgô, lūdô prop.** | "sea's clear, tide's near" — all's well, time to move |
| Magau + Pacific | **Taifūn kē tulē.** | "a typhoon is coming" — **taifūn** (Cantonese *tai fung*) is realm canon; a *tormô* is any storm, a *taifūn* is the season's named monster |
| Canaries | **Kalima avuī.** | "dust-haze today" — the Saharan dust over the water; cousin of Saharannaise *ṭāyf* ashore |
| St Pierre & Miquelon | **Brüm sūr.** *(terr.)* | "the fog-wall is heavy" — the Banks **brüm**, thicker than any mere *udū* |
| Wallis & Futuna | **Mer tabū avuī.** | "the water's closed today" — **tabū** covers sacred waters, closed holds, and quarantined wards alike |

## 5 · Time, the Watch & the CMT phrases

Federal life runs on **Common Meridian Time** (`../world/meridian-states.md`), and the deck
language grew a phrase-set around it — realm-wide, no territorial variants, because CMT belongs to
everyone and no one:

| Say | Literal | Use |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **Vakt Pasifik / Meridiān / Atlantik** | the Pacific / Meridian / Atlantic Watch | **vakt** — the duty spell and the federal Watch both; the one realm word the homeland itself supplied (Low German *Wacht*) |
| **Pāsā vest-lô.** | "pass it west." | defer the matter to the next watch — the realm's "not my desk" said without shame, because the desk never closes |
| **Sōl-pāsāmus** | "the passing of the sun" | the Continuity Session, when the outgoing watch hands off |
| **Sônāstāmus CMT-al.** | "a promise on CMT." | a commitment with an exact federal deadline — the realm's strongest casual oath |
| **Klôk mis, CMT?** | "what time is it, CMT?" | the follow-up to any deadline; every federal clock shows both times |
| **Nūlt ōra nūlt vakt.** | "no hour without a watch." | the right of continuous access, as the quays say it — "no hour of abandonment" |

## 7 · One exchange, five ports

The same three lines — hail, request, thanks-and-parting — as each quay actually says them. The
skeleton never moves; only the doorway and the flavor words change. **Realm skeleton:**

> — **Mer selgô? Guagua satām-lô, kus?** — *Sea clear? Where's the bus to the harbor?*
> — **Selgô. Nūnū sāl, prop.** — *Clear. Right there, close by.*
> — **Mālô, bāi. Tūļ jôvā!** — *Thanks, mate. Fair wind!*

| Port | The same exchange, locally |
|:---|:---|
| **Canaries** | — **Buenā! Guagua satām-lô, kus?** — **Selgô. Nūnū sāl.** — **Grasiā. Tūļ jôvā!** |
| **Bermuda** | — **Alrait? Guagua satām-lô, kus?** — **Alrait. Prop, bāi.** — **Tanks, bāi. Tūļ jôvā!** |
| **Esperanza** | — **Bonjū! Guagua degrā-lô, kus?** — **Selgô. Nūnū sāl.** — **Mesī. Ti-momen!** |
| **Mayotte** | — **Jeje? Guagua satām-lô, kus?** — **Ndjema. Prop.** — **Marahaba. Lǟdô ndjema.** |
| **Magau** | — **Jôsan! Sampan satām-lô, kus?** — **Hou. Nūnū sāl.** — **Mgōi. Jôsan demā.** |

*Read down any column of §7 and you hear one language; read across any row of §1–§2 and you hear
ten worlds. That is the Meridian settlement in a traveler's mouth — the same thing the three-way
phrasebook (`../reader/phrasebook.md`) proves for the homeland and Atlantic Africa, stretched
around the planet.*
