# Magau Meralian — mixed hanzi + Latin writing

*The optional local writing convention of Magau (`../world/meridian-states.md`): Meralian written
with traditional **hanzi** for established Cantonese-sourced lexical roots and Latin spelling for
everything else.*

This is a **script overlay**, not a separate language. A Magau text uses the same predicate-first
Meralian grammar, TAM/directional particles, pronouns, possession classifiers, and open-syllable
pronunciation as a text from any other Meridian territory. Federal law, CMT records, and
interterritorial transport documents remain Latin-only so that one constitutional orthography
travels across the federation.

## Convention

| Material | Local presentation | Federal Latin presentation |
|:---|:---|:---|
| Cantonese-derived content root | traditional hanzi | established Meralian Latin form |
| Meralian grammar word | Latin | Latin |
| inherited Neloxian root | Latin | Latin |
| name or place-name | locally chosen; Latin index form required | Latin |

For example, **Jôsan! Kusi na va sampan?** (“Good morning! Where is the skiff?”) can display the
Cantonese-sourced greeting and *sampan* in hanzi in Magau local print, while the syntactic frame
remains Latin Meralian. The archive stores the Latin lexical form once; hanzi enriches local
readability without changing the word’s grammar or federal spelling.

The applicable sound and grammar profile is `mare.md`; territorial vocabulary enters the federal
lexicon through its Three-Watch Rule.
