# Four Neloxian outcomes — comparative chart

*Metropolitan Nelôxi · Saharannaise · Congolaise · **Meralian**. The first three are
co-standards of Nelôxi; Meralian is a historically related Meridian daughter language. The chart
therefore compares a family relationship, not four interchangeable registers.*

## 1 · What changes

| Question | Metropolitan | Saharannaise | Congolaise | Meralian |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Main divergence | baseline | domestic lexicon | domestic lexicon | grammar, sound, and inherited-root reshaping |
| Syntax | Livonian-derived SVO | same | same | predicate-first / focus-flexible |
| Nominal relations | cases | cases | cases | particles, classifiers, order, context |
| Verbal time | inflection + time words | same | same | preverbal TAM + directionals |
| 1st-person “we” | one series | one series | one series | inclusive/exclusive; dual/paucal/plural |
| Possession | ordinary nominal grammar | same | same | semantic possessive classifiers |
| Formal writing | metropolitan grammar | shared grammar | shared grammar | Meralian grammar, more inherited formal lexicon |

## 2 · Related roots, different sentences

| English | Metropolitan Nelôxi | Meralian | What changed |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| I went. | **Mä lǟdi.** | **Ti lēda au.** | inherited travel root reshaped; anterior particle; predicate-first |
| The ship is coming. | **Xipd tulē demā.** | **Kē tula na xipa.** | open-syllable root; prospective particle; transport classifier |
| I finished reading the book. | **Mä lugē livrôn.** | **Fin luga au na ia livu.** | completive particle and classifier possession, no object case |
| Let us pass it west. | **Pāsāmus vest-lô.** | **Sai pāsā kita pala.** | inclusive pronoun, irrealis, directional |

A mainland reader recognizes *lēda/lǟd-*, *xipa/xipd*, *luga/lug-*, and *pāsā/pās-*, but must
learn Meralian’s syntax and function words. This is the intended historical effect: continuity in
the dictionary, independence in ordinary speech.

## 3 · Lexical ownership

Meralian retains Neloxian inherited vocabulary alongside a federal territorial layer: **taifūna**
for a named Pacific storm, **mālō** for thanks, **guagua** for a bus, **degrā** for a landing, and
**sampan** for a harbor skiff. The **Three-Watch Rule** in `mare.md` admits a word to the federal
lexicon only after living use is recorded across the Pacific, Meridian, and Atlantic Watches.
