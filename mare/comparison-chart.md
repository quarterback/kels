# The Four Standards — Comparison Chart

*Metropolitan Nelôxi · Saharannaise · Congolaise · Maré, laid side by side.* One language, four
seats. The three-standard chart (`../congolaise/comparison-chart.md`) showed the African pattern:
identical grammar, three divergent lexicons. Adding Maré inverts the picture — read the lexical
rows and Maré sits closest to Metropolitan; read the grammar rows and Maré is the divergent one.
**Two ways to be a co-standard**: change the words (Africa) or sand the grammar (the ocean).

---

## 0 · The shared spine — and the one licensed bend

Everything in the three-standard chart's row 0 (SVO, pre-verbal **äb**, copula **um/ūli**, the
case set, the derivational set, dozenal numbers) still holds in all four — with the single, closed
exception of the **Maritime Amendment** (`mare.md` §3) in spoken Maré: leveled case shapes,
particle tense. Written Maré rejoins the others completely.

> **Level 1, shown:** *Provinsô agā tri rīgô* — "the province has three regions" — is still the
> **same sentence** in all four standards, spoken or written.

## 1 · Greeting & courtesy — four doorways

Atlantic Africa greets with **peace** (*salām*, *jam*); the Baltic greets like the Hanseatic port
it is (*moin*); the ocean greets the way one vessel hails another — **by asking her condition**.
*Selgô* "clear" is the realm's *jam*.

| English | Metropolitan | Saharannaise | Congolaise | Maré |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| Hello | **Moin!** | **Salām!** | **Jam!** | **Mer selgô?** *(sea clear?)* |
| How are you? | **Kui sä?** | **Lābās?** | **Jam?** | **Selgô?** |
| Fine, and you? | **Jôvā, ja sä?** | **Lābās, ja sä?** | **Jam. Sä, jam?** | **Selgô. Sä, selgô?** |
| All well (at home) | **Kōt jôvā.** | **Ahl bxēr.** | **Wuro jam.** | **Sār selgô.** *(the island's clear)* |
| Welcome | **Velkô!** | **Marxaba!** | **Gan velkô!** | **Velkô abôrd!** *(aboard)* |
| Thank you | **dankô / mersü** | **barka** | **jārāma** | **mālô** |
| Sorry / excuse me | **pardô** | **smax** | **yāfo** | **pardô** *(the mainland word held)* |
| Friend / mate | **amīk** | **ṣaxb** | **giɗo** | **bāi** |
| Goodbye | **Moin! / Havs.** | **Slāma!** | **Jam-k!** | **Tūļ jôvā!** *(fair wind)* |
| Go well / in peace | **Lǟdô jôvā.** | **Lǟdô b-slāma.** | **Lǟdô jam-k.** | **Lǟdô tūļ-k.** *(go with the wind)* |
| Regards at home | **Kōt-lô, jôvā.** | **Ahl-lô, slām.** | **Jam wuro-lô.** | **Selgô sār-lô.** |

The comitative farewell is the tell that these four are one language: *jôvā / b-slāma / jam-k /
tūļ-k* — four things to go with (goodness, peace, peace, wind), one **-k** construction.

## 2 · The same sentences, four ways — where the grammar bends

In rows like these the African columns match Metropolitan exactly (their divergence is lexical,
not grammatical), so the interesting column is the last one. Deck Maré shown; Paper Maré would
match the first column.

| English | Metropolitan (= Sah. = Cong. frames) | Deck Maré |
|:---|:---|:---|
| The ship comes to the harbor. | **Xipd tulē satāmô.** | **Xipd tulē satām-lô.** *(sanded case — invariant -lô)* |
| The ship came yesterday. | **Xipd tulī ajēr.** *(strong past)* | **Xipd ti tulē ajēr.** *(particle past — no verb classes)* |
| She gave the document to the office. | **Tä donai papôrdôn amtôl.** *(-ai past, harmonized cases)* | **Tä ti donā papôrd amt-lô.** *(ti + dropped object -n + invariant -lô)* |
| I've read the book through. | **Mä lugī livrôn.** *(completion on the object's case)* | **Mä fin lugē livrô.** *(completive particle)* |
| The ship comes tomorrow. | **Xipd tulē demā.** *(no future tense; time-word)* | **Xipd kē tulē (demā).** *(prospective particle; time-word optional)* |

One asymmetry to hold on to: every deck-Maré sentence above is **transparent to a mainlander** —
the words are all theirs, only the joints are sanded. A Saharannaise Level-3 sentence, by contrast,
is opaque to the mainland but grammatically pristine. Opposite divergences, same distance.

## 3 · Water, sea & weather — where Maré keeps the Baltic core

The domestic-core rows that split Africa three ways barely split Maré from Metropolitan at all: the
deck carried the homeland's sea-words around the planet. What Maré adds are the **gap words** — the
things the Baltic never had to name.

| Concept | Metropolitan | Saharannaise | Congolaise | Maré | Owning territory (Mar.) |
|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|:---|
| water | **veç** | **aman** | **ndiyam** | **veç** | *(homeland core held)* |
| sea | **mer** | — *(the coast faces it; the desert names the shore)* | — | **mer** | *(homeland)* |
| wind | **tūļ** | — | — | **tūļ** | *(homeland)* |
| wave / great wave | **lain / laintū** | — | — | **lain / laintū** | *(homeland)* |
| tide | **lūdô** | — | — | **lūdô** | *(homeland)* |
| storm | **tormô** | **ṭāyf** *(sandstorm)* | — | **tormô**; **taifūn** *(the named season-monster)* | Magau |
| dust-haze at sea | — | *(cf. ṭāyf ashore)* | — | **kalima** | Canaries |
| fog | **udū** | — | — | **udū**; **brüm** *(the Banks wall)* | St Pierre & Miquelon |
| island | **sār** | — | — | **sār** | *(homeland)* |
| islet / atoll islet | — | — | — | **motū** | Wallis & Futuna |
| river landing | — | — | *(cf. the Māyo's own bank-words)* | **degrā** | Esperanza |

*The kalima row is the chart's best cousin-pair: Saharannaise **ṭāyf** is the storm as the desert
meets it; Canarian **kalima** is the same Saharan dust as it arrives over open water. Two standards
naming the two ends of one wind.*

## 4 · The signature words — each world's own, now four worlds

Extending the three-standard signature table:

|:---|:---|:---|
| **Saharannaise** | **žəml · gāfila · ṭāyf** | camel · caravan · sandstorm |
| **Congolaise** | **jeli · terānga · kora** | griot · the guest-honor ethic · the harp-lute |
| **Maré** | **taifūn** | the great named Pacific storm |
| **Maré** | **lēve-lēve** | "easy does it — no rush" (São Tomé's gift to the realm's whole temperament) |
| **Maré** | **motū · tabū** | atoll islet · closed/off-limits |
| **Maré** | **guagua** | the island bus (beside the crust's *busô*, and winning) |
| **Maré** | **vakt** | the Watch — both the sailor's duty spell and the three federal Watches of CMT |

Back-flow candidates ride the same convention as before, with the new tag: **taifūn (Mar.→Metr.)**
is already in mainland newsrooms every Pacific season; **lēve-lēve** is heard on Kunislinnô quays.
A word enters another seat's canon only when that seat merges it — never automatically.

## 5 · How to read the four-way divergence

- **Lexical rows:** Metropolitan and Maré pair up; Saharannaise and Congolaise each stand alone.
- **Grammar rows:** Metropolitan, Saharannaise, and Congolaise pair up; deck Maré stands alone.
- **Courtesy rows:** all four stand alone — the human layer is local everywhere, and even so it
  rhymes (*peace / peace / clear / fair wind*: four ways to wish someone an untroubled passage).
- **Gaps still carry the history.** The desert has no sea; the river has no atoll; the Baltic has
  no typhoon; and only the ocean needed a word for a duty-spell of governance that follows the sun.
