# Canarian Maré — the Spanish-fused overlay

*The deep dialect of the **Canary Islands** — the Meridian nation's heavyweight member territory,
Atlantic anchor, and host of the Neloxian outpost (`../world/meridian-states.md`). Everywhere else
in the realm a Maré dialect is a courtesy tilt (`dialects-phrasebook.md`); on the Canaries the
fusion is **foundational**: Nelôxi took root here the way Spanish did in reality, and grew into the
Spanish already planted. Canarian Maré is a Spanish-fused Nelôxi — Spanish + Nelôxi base, with the
islands' oldest tongue surviving underneath as fragments.*

## 1 · What kind of variety this is

Not a fifth standard. Canarian runs the full Maré grammar — the sanded case, the *ti/fin/kē*
particles, the whole Maritime Amendment (`mare.md` §3) — and every realm-canon deck word. What
makes it the realm's one **deep overlay** is that its *domestic* lexicon is Spanish-lexified, the
way Saharannaise's is Hassaniya-lexified — but by foundational contact, not by an academy's
rerooting. No Dīwān chose these words; four centuries of shared kitchens did.

## 2 · The Harbor-and-Hearth rule

Canarian's counterpart to the Confluence Principle: **which lexifier owns a word is decided by the
domain the word lives in.** A Canarian does not switch languages; they cross the doorway between
quay and kitchen inside one grammar.

| Domain | Lexifier | Examples |
|:---|:---|:---|
| deck, harbor, sea, weather | **Nelôxi core** | *mer, tūļ, lain, lūdô, xipd, satām, akôr, vakt* |
| law, school, transport, the modern | **the shared crust + Federal Maré** | *politsī, otelô, piletô, pāsportô, farmasī* |
| kitchen, home, kin, field | **Spanish** | *agwa, kasā, pan, lēçē, kēsô, papā, kampô* |
| the oldest ground — herding, plants, pots, the meal itself | **Guanche fragments** | *gofiô, baifô, gānigô, tabaibā* |

The doctrine in one table-row: **water is *veç* on the boat and *agwa* at the table** — one
concept, two domains, two lexifiers, zero confusion. This is the register gradient of the homeland
metrolect (`../dialects/README.md`) run between languages instead of between registers.

## 3 · The Spanish layer — how it passes into the script

No new letters. Spanish maps onto the existing inventory (Metropolitan rules + the Common Register
scar set), so the conventions below are habits, not an orthographic extension:

| Habit | Rule | Example |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **seseo** | Canarian Spanish never had [θ]; *z / ce / ci* land as **s** | *gracias → **grasiā*** |
| **ch → ç** | the *veç* consonant serves | *leche → **lēçē*** |
| **Spanish j/g [x] → h** | the open breath, not the Nelôxi *x* [ʃ] | *mojo → **mohô*** |
| **ñ → ny** | | *niño → **ninyô*** |
| **y / ll → j** | Nelôxi *j* is [j] | *playa → **plajā*** |
| **stressed finals take the macron; unstressed syllables clip** | the Canarian ear eats final *-s* and whole pretonic syllables, as at home | *por favor → **porfá***, *hasta luego → **Talwēgô!*** |

## 4 · The Guanche fragments — the oldest layer

Under the Spanish, the islands' pre-contact language survives exactly as it does in real Canarian
Spanish: as embedded vocabulary in the domains that never left the land. Each fragment is archived
with its layer named, the same discipline as the Congolaise tributary column:

| Word | Gloss | Layer |
|:---|:---|:---|
| **gofiô** | the roasted-grain meal — the islands' staple, the realm's ship-biscuit rival | Guanche |
| **baifô** | kid goat | Guanche |
| **gānigô** | the clay vessel | Guanche |
| **tabaibā** | the spurge — the dryland shrub the goats know | Guanche |
| **perenkēn** | gecko | Guanche |
| **magô** | countryman, inlander (said with affection or edge, as at home) | Guanche-era |

## 6 · The contact hub — three standards in one harbor

The Neloxian base makes the Canaries the realm's **Maré–Metropolitan contact point**: Metropolitan
is heard daily on sovereign Meridian soil, and the islands answer *Moin* without blinking — nowhere
else in the realm is the mainland standard a living neighbor. Across the water sits Atlantic Africa,
a ferry-line away, so Canarian is also the realm's closest contact with **Saharannaise** — the
*kalima/ṭāyf* cousin-pair (`comparison-chart.md` §3) is a lived fact here, the same Saharan wind
named on both shores of the strait.

The back-flow ledger runs through this harbor in both directions:

- **(Can.→Mar.)** *guagua*, *kalima* — Canarian words already Three-Watch ratified as realm canon.
- **(Mar.→Metr.)** the base's garrisons carry deck Maré home; *lēve-lēve* and *taifūn* ride north
  through Canarian quays as much as anywhere.
- **(Metr.→Can.)** mainland courtesy (*moin, dankô*) is passively understood islands-wide — soft
  power exactly as the lore intends.

## 7 · A worked exchange — the market stall

> — **Buenā, ninyô! Gofiô ja papā, mis prēu?** — *Morning, dear! The gofio and the potatoes, how
> much?*
> — **Tri rā. Kalima avuī — lēve-lēve, nē?** — *Three rā. Dust-haze today — no rush, eh?*
> — **Selgô. Mohô um, porfá?** — *Fair enough. Is there mojo, please?*
> — **Um, um. Sīn, gānigô-al.** — *There is. Here, in the clay bowl.*
> — **Grasiā! Talwēgô!** — *Thanks! See you!*
> — **Tūļ jôvā, ninyô.** — *Fair wind, dear.*

Every joint in this exchange is realm Maré (the sanded case in *gānigô-al*, the crust's *rā*, the
deck's *selgô* and *tūļ jôvā*); every hearth word is Spanish or Guanche. That is the whole overlay
in six lines.
