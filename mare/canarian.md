# Canarian Meralian — the Spanish-fused Atlantic overlay

*The deep Meralian variety of the Canary Islands: the Meridian federation’s Atlantic heavyweight,
European-facing anchor, and host of a Neloxian outpost (`../world/meridian-states.md`).*

Canarian Meralian is not a fifth standard and not Spanish vocabulary laid over Metropolitan Nelôxi.
It shares the federal Meralian grammar—predicate-first clauses, preverbal TAM and directionals,
classifying possession, and the inclusive/exclusive pronoun system—while carrying the realm’s
densest Spanish lexical and discourse layer. This is an Atlantic overlay on an oceanic daughter
language.

## What is locally distinctive

- **Doorway and domestic vocabulary.** *Buenā*, *grasiā*, *guagua*, and the weather word *kalima*
  are ordinary island choices. A court order or classroom lesson nevertheless has Meralian syntax.
- **A stronger inherited Neloxian layer.** The Neloxian base and constant contact with the outpost
  preserve more recognizable formal roots than in some territories. They are still pronounced and
  inflected as Meralian: *xipd* is **xipa**, not a restored metropolitan noun.
- **Atlantic rhythm.** Spanish prosody and familiar formulae are audible, but do not license Spanish
  word order as the default federal grammar.

| Meaning | Federal Meralian | Canarian Meralian |
|:---|:---|:---|
| hello | **Sela merā?** | **Buenā!** |
| Where is the bus? | **Kusi na va xipa?** | **Kusi na va guagua?** |
| thank you | **Mālō.** | **Grasiā.** |
| Let us pass it west. | **Sai pāsā kita pala.** | **Sai pāsā kita pala.** |

The Canaries are therefore a Meralian–Metropolitan contact hub, but not a route back to
metropolitan morphology. Their prestige gives Spanish-derived words wide federal circulation only
when the Three-Watch Rule in `mare.md` is met.
